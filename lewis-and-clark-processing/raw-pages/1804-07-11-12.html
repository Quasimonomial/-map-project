\n July 11, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark] [1]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n July 11th Wednesday, set out early \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Willow (1) Island in a bend to the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Sent out Dreweyer & Jo: Fields to hunt, Back of this Island a creek coms in on the S. S. called by the Indians Little Tarkio Creek\n [2] \u00a0\u00a0 I went on Shore above this Island on the S. S. found the bottom Subject for overflow wet and verry thickly interwoven with grape Vines\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on at about \u00bd a miles from the river about 3 ms. and observed fresh Sign of a horse, I prosueed the track, with an expectation of finding a Camp of Indians on the river, when I got to the river, I saw a horse on the Beech, this horse as appears was left last winter by Some hunting party, probable the \n Othouez\n [Otos], I joined the Boat on the Sand Island Situated opposit the mouth of the Ne Ma har River, [3] this river Coms in on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 is about 80 yds Wide and navagable for Perogues Some Distance up \u00a0\u00a0 the praries Commnce above the mouth and Continus on both Sides of this R \u00a0\u00a0 Drewyer killed 6 Deer to day J. Field one \u00a0\u00a0 Several hunters Sent out up the Nemahar R

[Clark]

\n \n
July 11th, Wednesday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Willow Island (1) in a bend on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 back of this Island a Creek Coms in Called by the Indians \n Tar-ki-o\n \n

\n

\nI went on Shore above this Creek and walked up parrelel with the river at ab ut half a mile distant, the bottom I found low & Subject to overflow, Still further out, the under groth & vines wer So thick that I could not get thro: with ease \u00a0\u00a0 after walking about three or 4 miles I observed a fresh horse track where he had been feeding \u00a0\u00a0 I turned my course to the river and prosud the track and found him on a Sand beach \u00a0\u00a0 This horse Probably had been left by Some party of Otteaus hunters who wintered or hunted in this quarter last fall or Wintr. I joined the party on a large Sand Island imediately opposit the mouth of \u2329\n Ne Ma haw\n \u232a [NB: N\u0103m\u0103h\u0101w] [4] River, at which place they had Camped, this Island is Sand \u00a0\u00a0 about half of it Covered with Small Willows of two different Kinds, one Narrow & the other a Broad Leaf. [5] \u00a0\u00a0 Several hunters Sent out to day on both Sides of the river, Seven Deer Killed to day. Drewyer Killd Six of them, made Some Luner observations this evening.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n July 11th Wednesday, 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 Drewyer & Jo. Fields went out to hunt, proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek which comes in behind a willow\nIsland on the North Side called by the Indians Little Tar-ci-o Creek, we proceeded on 6 miles \u00a0\u00a0 camped on the North Side of a willow Island opposite a Creek which came in on the South Side of the Missouris Called the Grate Nahhau. [7] \u00a0\u00a0 Several men went out hunting to day \u00a0\u00a0 2 came in with five Deer (Drewyer killed 6 Deer to day) and brought them in. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men Stayed out all night, Capt. Clark found a grey horse on the beach \u00a0\u00a0 Supposed to be left by hunters.\n\n\n

\nWendesday July, 11th 1804. Set out errley this morning \u00a0\u00a0 prosed on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek on the N. Side Called Tarcio Creek \u00a0\u00a0 it Comes in Back of a Isd. on the N. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Came to about 12 oclock P. m for the porpos of resting on[e] or two days \u00a0\u00a0 the men is all Sick \u00a0\u00a0 encamt on an Isd. on the Southe Side floos in Creek Called Granma \u2329maugh\u232a mohug Creek\n [8] \u00a0\u00a0 it is about 100 yards wide \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is good and well timberd \u00a0\u00a0 High and well Waterd \u00a0\u00a0 this \u2329R\u232a Creek Runs up and Heds near the River platt\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wed. 11th. \u00a0\u00a0 We also embarked early this morning; passed a creek on the north side, called Tarico, and halted at an island, opposite a creek called Moha\n [9] on the south side of the river. Seven hunters went out to day and two [10] of them brought in five deer. Here we found another horse on the bank of the river, supposed to have been left by a hunting party last winter. Two of our men, who had gone to hunt on the south side of the river, did not return at night.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWedndy. 11 \u00a0\u00a0 Got Under way at an Early hour \u00a0\u00a0 It appeard. like rain but cleard up \u00a0\u00a0 passd Some Islands to the E. of us \u00a0\u00a0 Got to Grande, mo, haugh\n [11] at Eleven Oclock \u00a0\u00a0 Halted that day and Next\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Roed 4\u00bd Miles\u2014

\n

\nWednesday July 11th \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early this morning having cloudy weather & appearance of Rain, at 10 oClock A. M. the Clouds dispers'd and the weather became clear, we passed by some Islands which lay to the No \nEast of us, and got to the Grand na Mahaws River about 11 oClock A. M. which is a noted place, & halted there this day distance come being 4\u00bd Miles. \u00a0\u00a0 The Grand na Mahaw River lies on the South side & is 35 Yds. wide at its mouth\n\n\n

1. \n Biddle placed this notation at the top of this sheet of the Field Notes (document 30), above the July 11 entry: \"July 9 to 13th.\" (back)
\n
2. Probably later Little Tarkio creek, in Holt County, Missouri. The routes of both Big and Little Tarkio creeks through the bottom to the Missouri seem to have shifted frequently. MRC maps 18, 19. (back)
\n
3. The Big Nemaha River, whose Oto Indian name, n\u012f\u0301m\u0105ha, signifies \"miry water,\" enters the Missouri in Richardson County, Nebraska, just above the Nebraska-Kansas line. The camp, opposite the river mouth on an island, may have been in present Holt County, Missouri. The island itself (Lewis's \"Newfound\" of July 11 \"New\" of the next day) appears unnamed on Nicollet's map and is missing on later maps. Fitzpatrick, 105; Nicollet (MMR), 377; MRC map 19. (back)
\n
4. \n Biddle apparently crossed out \"Ne Ma how\" to substitute his own word. (back)
\n
5. The small willows with narrow and broad leaves are probably Salix exigua Nutt. ssp. interior (Rowlee) Cronq., sandbar, or coyote, willow, and S. amygdaloides Anderss, peach-leaved willow, respectively, although seedlings of the larger willow tree S. nigra Marsh., black willow, could also be found on the sandbars described. Steyermark, 497, 494, 496; Barkley, 102\u20134. (back)
