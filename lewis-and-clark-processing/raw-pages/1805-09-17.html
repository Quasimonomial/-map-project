[Clark]

\n Sunday [NB: Tuesday] 17th Septr. 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\nCloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 our horses much Scattered which detained us untill one oClock P. M. at which time we Set out the falling Snow & Snow \u2329falling\u232a from the trees which kept us wet all the after noon \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Several high ruged Knobs and Several dreans & Springs passing to the right, & passing on the ridge devideing the waters of two Small rivers. [2] road excessively bad \u00a0\u00a0 Snow on the Knobs, no Snow in the vallies \u00a0\u00a0 Killed a fiew Pheasents which was not Sufficient for our Supper which compelled us to kill Something. \u00a0\u00a0 a coalt being the most useless part of our Stock he fell a Prey to our appetites. The after part of the day fare, we made only 10 miles to day \u00a0\u00a0 two horses fell & hurt themselves very \u2329well\u232a much. \u00a0\u00a0 we Encamped on the top of a high Knob of the mountain at a run passing to the left. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 we proceed on as yesterday, & with dificulty found the road\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday 17th Sept. 1805. Cloudy and cold \u00a0\u00a0 we went out to look for our horses \u00a0\u00a0 found Some of them much Scattered. \u00a0\u00a0 we did not find them all untill about 12 oClock at which time we Set out and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 the Snow melted of the timber. the trail verry rough \u00a0\u00a0 we came up and down bad Steep places of the Mountain, the afternoon clear and pleasant & warm. \u00a0\u00a0 the Snow melted fast. \u00a0\u00a0 the water Stood in the trail over our mockns Some places Slippery. \u00a0\u00a0 we assended a steep high rockey part of the Mountain \u00a0\u00a0 high rocks and high pricipicies. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on this Mountain at a small creek and dry pine timber. \u00a0\u00a0 we being verry hungry oblidged us to kill another colt the last we had. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters chased a bear up the Mountain but could not kill it. \u00a0\u00a0 we hear wolves howl Some distance a head.\n\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 17th. \u00a0\u00a0 Our horses scattered so much last night, that they were not collected until noon, at which time we began our march again. It was a fine day with warm sunshine, which melted the snow very fast on the south sides of the hills, and made the travelling very fatiguing and uncomfortable. We continued over high desert mountains, where our hunters could find no game, nor signs of any except a bear's tract which they observed to day.\u2014 At dark we halted at a spring on the top of a mountain; killed another colt, and encamped there all night.\n\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 17th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy and cold. \u00a0\u00a0 we went out to hunt our horses, but found them much Scatered. \u00a0\u00a0 the mare which owned the colt, which we killed, went back & led 4 more horses back to where we took dinner yesterday. \u00a0\u00a0 the most of the other horses found Scatd. on the mountain, but we did not find them all untill 12 oClock at which time we Set out and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 the Snow lay heavy on the timber. \u00a0\u00a0 passed along a rough road up and down the Mountains \u00a0\u00a0 descended down a Steep part of the moutn. \u00a0\u00a0 the afternoon clear & warm. \u00a0\u00a0 the Snow melted So that the water Stood in the trail over our mockasons in Some places. \u00a0\u00a0 verry Slippery bad travvelling for our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 we assended verry high mountains verry rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 Some bald places on the top of the mountn. \u00a0\u00a0 high rocks Standing up, & high precepices &c. \u00a0\u00a0 these motn. mostly covred with Spruce pine & bolsom fer timber. \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several creeks or Spring runs in the Course of the day \u00a0\u00a0 Came about [blank] miles this day, and Camped at a Small branch on the mountain near a round deep Sinque hole full of water. \u00a0\u00a0 we being hungry obledged us to kill the other Sucking colt to eat. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters chased a bear in a mountn. but killed nothing. \u00a0\u00a0 we expect that their is game near a head. \u00a0\u00a0 we hear wolves howl & Saw Some deer Sign &c.

\n

\nTuesday Septemr 17th \u00a0\u00a0 We had a cold Cloudy morning, the Men were sent out to hunt our horses, they found them, but they were much scattered, The Mare whose colt we had kill'd Yesterday, went back to where we halted Yesterday, to refresh ourselves, (or eat portable Soup) and took 4 of our horses with her, the other of our horses were found scattered on the Mountain & the whole of them were not found 'till 12 oClock A. M. \u00a0\u00a0 We then set out, and proceeded on our Journey, the Snow laying heavy on the trees. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed along a rough path, which was up & down the Mountain; & descended a steep part of the same, In the afternoon the weather cleared away, & then it became clear & warm, the Snow melted fast, & the water stood in the trail over our Moccasins, & in some places it was very Slippy, the travelling was very bad for ourselves & horses, We ascended some very high mountains, & very rockey paths & many bare places on the Mountains & high Rocks Standing upright on them.\u2014

\n

\nThese mountains were chiefly covered with Spruce pine, & balsam fir timber. \u00a0\u00a0 In the course of this day we crossed several Creek & Spring runs, lying in the hollows of the Mountains. \u00a0\u00a0 We came about 16 Miles this day, & encamped at a small branch on a Mountain; near a Round deep Sink hole which was full of water. \u00a0\u00a0 The party being all exceeding hungry we were obliged to kill a sucking Colt to subsist on. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters went out \nhunting. \u00a0\u00a0 He chased a bear in a Mountain; but did not get a chance to kill it. \u00a0\u00a0 The Wolves howled very much in the Night, & we saw some signs of deer, so that we expect that their is game to be had a head of where we are \nencamped.\u2014\n\n\n
