\n June 30, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n30th June, Set out verry early this Morng \u00a0\u00a0 Saw a verry large wolf [1] [blank] on the Sand bar this morning walking near a gange of Turkeys (1) at 10 miles above the Kansis passed the mouth of a Small River Call the (\n Petite Plate\n ) or the little Shole river, [2] this river is about 70 yds. Wide and has Several rapids & falls, well Calculatd for mills, the land on this river is Said to be Roaling, Killed 2 Deer Bucks Swinging [swimming] the river \u00a0\u00a0 the wind from the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 here we opened the Bag of Bread given us by [blank] which we found verry good, our Bacon which was given us by [blank] we examined and found Sound and good \u00a0\u00a0 Some of that purchased in the Illinois Spoiled, \u2329I found\u232a a relish of this old bacon this morning was verry agreeable, Deer to be Seen in every direction and their tracks ar as plenty as Hogs about a farm, our hunts. Killed 9 Deer to day \u00a0\u00a0 the land below the last river is good, that above, between the two rivers which is near together is Slaik'y [3] and bad on the N. Side, the other Side is good land, Landed on the L. S. below an Isd called Dimond Island\n [4]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
30th June Satturday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out verry early this morning, a verry large wolf Came to the bank and looked at us this morning, passd the (1) mouth of a Small river 10 ms. above the \n Kanseis\n Called by the french Petite River Platte (or Shoal river) from the number of falls in it, this river is about 60 yards wide at its mouth and runs Parrilel with the Missouries for ten or twelve miles, \u2329I am\u232a [NB: (Some of the party who went up] told that the lands on this Small river is good, and on its Several falls well Calculated for mills, the wind from S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 came to at 12 oClock & rested three hours, the [sun or day?] being hot the men becom verry feeble, Farnsts. Thermometer at 3 oClock Stood at 96\u00b0 above 0, emence numbs. of Deer on the banks, Skipping in every derection, the party Killed nine Bucks on the river & Bank to day, The Countrey on the S. S. between the Shoal River & Missouris is indifferent Subject to overflow, that below and on the L. S. is high & appers well timbered, Camped on the L. S. opsd. the Lower point of a Isd. Called diamond Island, Broke our mast

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday June 30th 1804, we Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 Saw a verry large woolf on the sand beach this morning \u00a0\u00a0 at 10 miles from or above the Kansis \u00a0\u00a0 passed the mouth of a Small River called \n Petete platt R. or little Shole river, this river is about 50 yd. wide and has Several Rapids & falls, well calculated for mills, the Land on this River is Said to be roaling \u00a0\u00a0 the men killed 2 Deer Swimming the River. The hunters killed 7 Deer \u00a0\u00a0 the most of them were bucks. \u00a0\u00a0 we broke our mast comming to Shore against a Small Tree which hung over the River. \u00a0\u00a0 came about 10 miles this day \u00a0\u00a0 camped [7] on the South Side of the Missouris,

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday June 30th 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set our verry early this morning \u00a0\u00a0 Saw a wolf on the Sind Bare \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Littel River platte on the N. Side \u00a0\u00a0 it is about 100 yards wide \u00a0\u00a0 Clear water \u00a0\u00a0 High Land on the Loer Side of it \u00a0\u00a0 on this River it is Sayed that thare is a nomber of \u2329fowl\u232a falls on it fitting for mills \u00a0\u00a0 the land is Rolling \u00a0\u00a0 campt on the South Side \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Low \u00a0\u00a0 that on the N is the Same.\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 30th. \u00a0\u00a0 The day was clear and we continued our voyage; found high land on both sides of the river; and passed a large creek on the north side, called Platt, fifty yards wide. We broke our mast and encamped [8] on the south side, where there were the most signs of game I ever saw.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaterday 30 \u00a0\u00a0 Got on our way at day light \u00a0\u00a0 the water Was Strong the land high on Each Side \u00a0\u00a0 the deer was plentifull on the Sand beech as we passd along \u00a0\u00a0 all sorts of fowls \u00a0\u00a0 likeway the woolves and Bears Every day \u00a0\u00a0 Roed 12 Miles\u2014

\n

\nJune 30th Saturday \u00a0\u00a0 This morning at day light we embark'd and proceeded on our Voyage, found the current setting strong against us, The land on both sides of the River lies high, We perceived the Deer in abundance on \nthe Land beaches, as we passed along, likewise Bears & Wolves, \u2329with\u232a and abundance of Wild fowl. \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped on the Bank of the River, having rowed 12 Miles this day\u2014\n\n

\n
1. Presumably a gray wolf, Canis lupus, which Lewis describes May 5, 1805. Hall 2:928\u201333. (back)
\n
2. The Platte, or Little Platte, River in Platte County, Missouri, not to be confused with the stream of that name in Nebraska. In 1804, its mouth was probably several miles down the Missouri from its present position. \"Platte\" in French implies that the stream is relatively level, wide, and shallow. Clark's \"Shole\" is, of course, \"shoal.\" Stewart (APN), 376; MRC map 15. (back)
\n
3. Slaky means miry or muddy. (back)
\n
4. \n Diamond Island was still on maps about 1890 but no longer is. The camp was in northeast Wyandotte County, Kansas, in the vicinity of the present village of Wolcott. MRC map 15; MRM map 42. (back)
\n
5. The courses and distances for this Field Notes entry come on the same sheet (document 25) after a portion of the entry for July 1 and were later crossed out. Here they are placed under the correct date. (back)
\n
6. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
7. In the vicinity of the village of Wolcott, Wyandotte County, Kansas. (back)
\n
8. Opposite Diamond Island, no longer in existence, in southeastern Wyandotte County, Kansas. (back)
