S. \n Piaheto\n 15 yds Wide, [1] \u00a0\u00a0 halted on a Sand bar and had the punishmt inflicted on Newmon, which caused the indian Chieif to cry untill the thing was explained to him \u00a0\u00a0 Camped opposit an antient fortification which is on the L. S, [2] when I explained to the Chief the Cause of whipping N\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 he observed that examples were necessary & that he himself had made them by Death, but his nation never whiped even from their bearth.

S. 15 yards wide \u00a0\u00a0 this Creek we Call after the 3rd Chief \n Piaheto\n (or Eagles feather) \u00a0\u00a0 at 1 oClock we halted on a Sand bar & after Dinner executed the Sentence of the Court Martial So far a[s] giveing the Corporal punishment, & proceeded on a fiew miles, the wind a head from N. E. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped in a Cove of the bank on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 imediately opposit our Camp on the L. Side I observe an antient fortification the walls of which appear to be 8 or 10 feet high, [NB: most of it washed in] \u00a0\u00a0 the evening wet and disagreeable, the river Something wider more timber on the banks

\nThe punishment of this day allarmd. the Indian Chief verry much, he Cried aloud (or effected to Cry) \u00a0\u00a0 I explained the Cause of the punishment and the necessity [NB: of it,] \u2329which\u232a He [NB: also] thought examples were also necessary, & he himself had made them by Death, his nation never whiped even their Children, from their burth.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 14th Oct. Cloudy & rain. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a bottom covered with Timber on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passd a creek [5] & Black Bluffs on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted at 12 oClock on a Sand bar. \u00a0\u00a0 the proceedings of the Court martial was read & put in to Exicution. \u00a0\u00a0 it [rained] Slowly the greater part of the Day. Camped on N. S. below a Timbred Bottom.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 14th. \u00a0\u00a0 We had a cloudy morning and some rain. We proceeded early on our voyage; passed a bottom covered with timber on the south side and low ground covered with willows on the north; passed a creek and black bluffs on the south side and encamped on the north. It rained slowly during the whole of the day.\n\n\n

\n Sunday 14th Oct. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 Some rain. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly, proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 campd. on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 nothing else extraordinary hapened this day.

\n

\nSunday October 14th \u00a0\u00a0 We sett off Early this morning and had some Rain, we proceeded on, & passed some bottoms of fine land, lying on the South side of the River, it continued raining all this day, We encamped on the North side of the River\n\n\n\n

\n
1. In Codex C and on Atlas map 26 Clark has used both the Indian name of the Arikara chief for whom he named the creek and the English version, \"Eagles Feather.\" Eagle Feather Creek, in Corson County, South Dakota, appears on MRC map 46; later it became Bald Head Creek. It enters the Missouri about a mile downstream from the state line. MRY map 119. (back)
\n
2. On the starboard side, in Emmons County, North Dakota, their first camp in that state; Fire Heart Butte is roughly opposite on the other side. The campsite and the \"antient fortification\" are now inundated by Oahe Reservoir. Atlas map 26; MRC map 47. (back)
\n
3. This distance is one mile in Codex C. (back)
\n
4. \n Clark wrote \"untill [blank] oClock,\" then put \"all\" in the blank and interlined \"Day.\" (back)
\n
5. \n Clark's\n Piaheto, or Eagles Feather, Creek, probably Baldhead Creek, Corson County, South Dakota, about a mile south of the North Dakota state line. (back)
