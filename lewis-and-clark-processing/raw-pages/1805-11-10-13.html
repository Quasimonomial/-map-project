\n November 10th Sunday 1805\n
\u00a0

\nRained verry hard the greater part of last night and continues this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind has luled and the waves are not high; we loaded our canoes and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Small and deep nitch on the Stard. Side, we proceeded on about 10 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Saw great numbers of Sea Guls, the wind rose from the N. W. and the waves became So high that we were compelled to return about 2 miles to a place we Could unload our Canoes, which we did in a Small nitch at the mouth of a Small run on a pile of drift logs where we Continued untill low water, when the river appeared calm we loaded and Set out; but was obliged to return finding the waves too high for our Canoes to ride, we again unloaded the Canoes, and Stoed the loading on a rock above the tide water, and formed a camp on the Drift Logs which appeared to be the only Situation we could find to lie, the hills being either a perpendicular Clift, or Steep assent, riseing to about 500 feet\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 our Canoes we Secured as well as we could\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we are all wet the rain haveing continued all day, our beding and maney other articles, employ our Selves drying our blankets\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 nothing to eate but dried fish pounded which we brought from the falls. \u00a0\u00a0 we made 10 miles today\u2014\n

[Ordway]

\nSunday 10th Nov. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 considerable of rain fell last night. \u00a0\u00a0 a rainy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 the waves not So high as yesterdy \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded the canoes and Set out and went on \u00a0\u00a0 passed high clifts of rocks, and fine Springs. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw porpises [2] in the River. Saw a number of Sea otter, Sea gulls, [3] ducks &C. \u00a0\u00a0 continued raining hard \u00a0\u00a0 abt. noon the wind rose So high that obledged us to turn back from a point of rocks and roe about 2 miles back into a cove before we could git a place to unload the canoes. \u00a0\u00a0 we got in to the harbour and unloaded the canoes and Stayed untill towards evening. \u00a0\u00a0 then loaded again thinking to find a Safer harbour. \u00a0\u00a0 we went on a Short distance to a Spring run where we Camped. [4] \u00a0\u00a0 the wind contind. So high that we could not proceed. \u00a0\u00a0 the hills and pine timber make close to the River on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 we had Scarsely room for to camp.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 10th. \u00a0\u00a0 We had a rainy morning, but the wind was not so high as it had been yesterday; and we set out from Cape Swell, coasted along 8 miles, passed some high cliffs of sandy rocks, and then came to a point; where we found the swells so high, the wind having risen, that we could not proceed; so we had to return back about a mile to get a safe harbour. Here we dined on some pounded salmon, that we had procured from the Indians; and unloaded our canoes. After we had been here about 2 hours, it became more calm, and we loaded the canoes again, but could not get round the point, the swells were still so high; we therefore put too at a branch of fresh water, under high cliffs of rocks and unloaded again. Here we scarcely had room to lie between the rocks and water; but we made shift to do it among some drift wood that had been beat up by the tide. It rained hard all night, and was very disagreeable. While on our way down today, we saw some porpoises, sea otter and a great many sea gulls. The water is become very salt.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday Novemr. 10th \u00a0\u00a0 We had rain the greater part of last night & a wet rainey morning, but the Waves did not run so high as they did Yesterday. \u00a0\u00a0 We loaded our Canoes & set out. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and passed high hills with pine trees growing on them, some high Clifts of rocks & several fine Springs of fresh water. \u00a0\u00a0 We saw in the River a number of Porpoises, & Sea Otters, Sea Gulls & Ducks in the greatest abundance. \u00a0\u00a0 We continued on our way, it raining hard on us, 'till about noon; when it ceased. \u00a0\u00a0 The Waves then ran so high that we had to turn back and went up the River about 2 Miles, before we could find a harbour to unload the Canoes. \u00a0\u00a0 We at last found a convenient place, at which we unloaded our Canoes. \u00a0\u00a0 We halted at that place, where we staid till towards evening, when we loaded our Canoes again, and proceedd on up the River in hopes to find a safer harbour, than \nthe one that we had left. \u00a0\u00a0 We went a small distance from the place we left, & came to a large Spring run; lying on the South side, in a bend of the River, where we stopped & Encamped. \u00a0\u00a0 we again unloaded the Canoes, & had scarcely room to lay down, the hills making in so close to the River. \u00a0\u00a0 We went about 7 Miles this day, our course being nearly West.\u2014\n\n\n
