[Clark]

\n \n
\n October 7th \u2329Sunday\u232a Monday 1805\n [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nI continu verry unwell but obliged to attend every thing \u00a0\u00a0 all the Canoes put into the water and loaded, fixed our Canoes as well as possible and Set out \u00a0\u00a0 as we were about to Set out we missd. both of the Chiefs [2] who promised to accompany us; I also missed my Pipe Tomahawk which Could not be found.

\n

\nThe after part of the day Cloudy proceded on passed 10 rapids which wer danjerous \u00a0\u00a0 the Canoe in which I was Struck a rock and Sprung a leak in the 3rd rapid, we proceeded on 20 [WC: 19] miles and Encamped on a Stard point oppost a run. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek Small on the Lard. Side at 9 miles, [4] \u2329a run at\u232a a Short distanc from the river at 2 feet 4 Inches N. of a dead toped pine Treee had burid 2 Lead Canisters of Powder

\n

\nHad the Canoes unloaded examined and mended a Small leake which we discovered in a thin place in her Side \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several \u2329old\u232a Camps of Indians to day

\n

\nour Course and distance Shall be given after I get to the forks. &c.\u2014 which the Indians Say is the last of the bad water untill we get to the great falls 10 day below, where the white people live &c. The Lodges are of Sticks set in a \u2329conocal\u232a form of roof of a house & covered with mats and Straw\n\n

\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMonday 7th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we put the other three canoes in to the River. \u00a0\u00a0 got them in readiness and loaded them about 3 oClock P. m. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out on our journay to descend the River. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on over Several Sholes and rapids where we halled the canoes over Sholes. Some part of the River is deep and current gentle &c. \u00a0\u00a0 the hills and clifts make near the River on each side. Saw old Indian Camps on Lard. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Came 21 miles and Camped on the Stard. Side.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 7th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we put the other three canoes in to the River and got them in readiness and loaded them. \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oClock P. m. we Set out on our way to descend the River. [5] \u00a0\u00a0 the 2 Indians [6] we came over the mount. with us continues on with us, and a chief & one more Indian who agreed to go down with us has gone by land Some distance down and then Intends comming on board. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on over a nomber of bad rapids where the canoes run fast and obledged us to git out in the cold water and hale them off. \u00a0\u00a0 Some places the water is deep & current gentle \nfor Some distance, but the Shole rapids are common & rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 the River hills make close to the River on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 Some clifts of rocks, a fiew Scattering pine trees on the hills, but they are mostly barron broken, & covred over with grass. \u00a0\u00a0 Some Small cotton wood along the Shores. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the rapids which are deep enofe to run clear are So bad that we take water over the canoes by the waves. \u00a0\u00a0 Strike Some large rocks & Slide of without Injury\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 Came 21 miles and Camped on Stard. Side \u00a0\u00a0 the officers canoe leaks So that they changes their Baggag in an other canoe for fear of gitting the Instruments &c. wet. \u00a0\u00a0 the Evening cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 one man taken Sick with the collick. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed Some old Indian Camps this afternoon & a Small \ncanoe on Shore\u2014

\n

\nMonday October 7th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we had clear pleasant weather, all our party that were able were employed in getting the other three new Canoes into the River, which they effected. \u00a0\u00a0 they got every thing in readiness on board of them and got them loaded. \u00a0\u00a0 About 3 oClock P. M. we set out on our way to descend the River, & the 2 Indians of the Snake Nation, that came to Pilot us across the Mountains, agreed to continue with us. \u00a0\u00a0 We also had a chief & one Indian from the last Town we came through who also \nagreed to accompany us.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 These two last Indians, set off down the River by land to go some distance, & intend to join our party again. \u00a0\u00a0 We then proceeded on our Voyage, and crossed a number of bad rapids, where our \nCanoes got fast, & obliged us to get out in the Water (that was cold) and hawl them off. \u00a0\u00a0 we found the Water in some places deep, & the current running gentle for some distance. \u00a0\u00a0 The Rapids were very frequent & Shoal, the bottom of the River rockey, and the hills making close in to the River on both sides of it. \u00a0\u00a0 There is some Clifts of rocks, lying along the Shore & a few scattering pine trees, growing on some of the hills \u2329but they\u232a which are mostly broken & covered with grass. \u00a0\u00a0 We saw some few cotton wood Trees, growing on each side of the River along the Shore, We also found, that in some of the Rapids where we had plenty of water for our Canoes to pass, that \nthe Waves ran so high that our Canoes took in a great deal of water, & we struck several Rocks, in passing over them, but the Rapidity of the stream forced us over them.\u2014

\n

\nWe came about 27 Miles this day, and encamped on the North side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 The Canoe that our Officers went in, leaked so bad, that they were forced to unload it, & put their baggage into another Canoe, for fear of getting their Mathematical Instruments & baggage wet. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed this afternoon some old Indian Camps & saw a Canoe lying on the Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 The Evening proved cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our party was taken ill, this evening of a Cholic occasioned by being so much in the water.\n\n\n\n\n
