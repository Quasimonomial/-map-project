\n May 22, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Wednesday May 22cd 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind blew so violently this morning that we did not think it prudent to set out untill it had in some measure abated; this did not happen untill 10 A. M. when we proceeded principally by the toe lines \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms somewhat wider than usual, the lands fertile or apparently so tho' the short grass and the scantey proportion of it on the hills would indicate no great fertility. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Windy Island on Lard. at 1 M. \u00a0\u00a0 5\u00bd miles above passed a large Island in a bend on Stard. side, and three miles further on the same side passed the entrance of grows Creek 20 yds wide, affords but little water. [1] \u00a0\u00a0 this creek we named from seeing a number of the pointed tail praire hen near it's mouth, [2] \u00a0\u00a0 these are the fist we have seen in such numbers for some days. I walked on shore this morning \u00a0\u00a0 the country is not so broken as yesterday tho' still high and roling or wavy; the hills on Lard. side possess more pine than usual; some also on the Stard. hills. Salts and other mineral appearances as usual. \u00a0\u00a0 the river continues about the same width or from 200 to 250 yds. wide, fewer sandbars and the courant more gentle and regular; game not so abundant as below the Muscle Shell river. I killed a deer in the course of my walk today. Capt. C. also walked out this evening and took a view of the country from a conspicuous point and found it the same as has been discribed. \u00a0\u00a0 we have caught but few fish since we left the Mandans, they do not bite freely, what we took were the white cat of 2 to 5 lbs. [3] I presume that fish are scarce in this part of the river. We encamped earlyer this evening [4] than usual in order render the oil of a bear which we killed. I do not believe that the Black bear common to the lower part of this river and the Atlantic States, exists in this quarter; [5] we have neither seen one of them nor their tracks which would be easily distinguished by it's shortness of tallons when compared with the brown grizly or white bear. I believe that it is the same species or family of bears which assumes all those colours at different ages and seasons of the year.\u2014 [6]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 22nd Wednesday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind Continued to blow So violently hard we did not think it prudent to Set out untill it luled a little, about 10 oClock we Set out \u00a0\u00a0 the morning Cold, passed a Small Island in the bend to the Lard Side, & proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 at 5 miles higher passed a Island in a bend to the Stard Side, and a Creek a Short distance above on the Stard Side 20 yds. w \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis walked out before dinner & Killed a Deer, I walked out after dinner and assended & but a few miles to view the Countrey, which I found roleing & of a verry rich Stickey Soil produceing but little vegitation of any kind except the prickley-piar, but little grass & that verry low. \u00a0\u00a0 a great deal of Scattering Pine on the Lard Side & Some fur on the Stard. Sd. The mineral productions as described in the proceeding days, game no So abundant as below, the river Continue about the Same width, fewer Sand bars & current more regular, river falls about an inch a day

\n

\nWe camped on the Stard. Side, earlier than we intend on account of Saveing the oil of a bear which the party killed late this afternoon.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n May 22nd Wednesday 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind continued to blow So violently hard we did not think it prudent to Set out untill it luled a little \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 oClock we Set out \u00a0\u00a0 the [morning] cold. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Island in the bend of the river to the Lard. Side, and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 at 5 miles higher passed a Isld in a bend to the Stard. Side & a creek [8] a Short distance [above] on the Stard. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis walked on Shore and killed a deer in the fore part of the day, after dinner Capt. Clark walked out a fiew miles to view the Country, which he found verry rich Soil produceing but little vigitation of any kind except the prickly pairs \u00a0\u00a0 but little grass & that verry low. \u00a0\u00a0 a great deal of Scatering pine on the Lard. Side & Some fiew on the Stard. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 the mineral production as described yesterday or in the proceeding days. \u00a0\u00a0 the game not So a bundant as below \u00a0\u00a0 the river continues about the Same width. \u00a0\u00a0 a fiew Sand bars, and current more regular. River falls about an Inch a day, we Camped on the Stard Side eairlier than we intended on account of Saveing the oil of a yallow bear which the party killed late this afternoon. Came 16\u00bd miles to day.\u2014

\n

\nMany of the creeks which appear to have no water near their mouths have Streams of running water high up which rise & waste in the Sand or gravel \u00a0\u00a0 the water of those creeks are so much impregnated with the Salt Substance that it cannot be drank with pleasantness.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 22nd. \u00a0\u00a0 A cloudy morning. The wind blew so hard this morning, we did not get under way until 9 o'clock. The forenoon was cold and disagreeable, but the afternoon became more pleasant. We killed a brown bear and some other game on our way. Having gone 16\u2153 miles we encamped on the North side.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 22nd May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew hard all last night (caught 2 beaver last night) and continues blowing this morning, Cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 oC. A. M. the wind abated So that we Set off and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Island near N. S. and one Near the S. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 the River hills make near the River. \u00a0\u00a0 passed black bluffs \u00a0\u00a0 the pitch pine close along Sd. bluffs to the bank of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek [9] on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we wounded a brown bear in the River. \u00a0\u00a0 (abt. 2 oC. we) halted to dine at a handsom timbred bottom on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 a cold chilly day. \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening Some of the hunters \nkilled a large brown bear. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saved the Skin & greese. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Came 16\u00bd Miles to day. \u00a0\u00a0 Sergt. Ordway and one of the hunters killed a large buffaloe.

\n

\nWednesday May 22nd \u00a0\u00a0 The wind continued blowing hard, all last night which was the case in the Morning till about 11 o'Clock A. M. when it abated, We then set off, and proceeded on our Voyage; and passed Two small Islands, one laying near the North & the other near the South side of the River, the Hills making in, near the River, and black bluffs, [crossed out, illegible] with Pitch pine Trees growing close along those bluffs, to the bank of the River, We likewise passed a Creek lying on the North side of the River, where one of the Hunters that was out wounded a brown bear, but we did not get him\u2014\n \u00a0\u00a0 he was in the River and made his escape.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 At 2 o'Clock P. M. we stopped to dine at a Handsome piece of Timbered bottom land, lying on the North side of the River; the day was chilly and Cold, towards Evening some of our hunters killed a large brown Bear, which they brought to us, We halted here to Skin & get the Oil of this animal & One of our hunters killed a large Buffalo; We encamped on the North side of the River & came 16\u00bd Miles this day.\u2014\n\n\n
