\n May 13, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Monday May 13th 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind continued to blow so violently this morning that we did not think it prudent to set out. \u00a0\u00a0 sent out some hunters. At 1 P. M. the wind abated, and altho' the hunters had not all returned we set out; the courant reather stronger than usual and the water continues to become reather clearer, from both which I anticipate a change of Country shortly. \u00a0\u00a0 the country much the same as yesterday; but little timber in the bottoms and a scant proportion of pine an cedar crown the Stard. hills. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt C. who was on shore the greater part of the day killed a mule and a Common deer, the party killed several deer and some Elk principally for the benefit of their skins which are necessary to them for cloathing, the Elk skins I now begin to reserve for making the leather boat at the falls. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters joined us this evening; Gibson had wounded a very large brown bear but it was too late in the evening to pursue him.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n 13th of May Monday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind Continued to blow hard untill one oClock P M. to day at which time it fell a little and we Set out and proceeded on verry well about 9 miles and Camped on the Lard Side. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 the countrey much the Same appearance as yesterday but little timber in the bottoms; Some Pine in places on the Stard. Hills. I killed two deer this evening one a mule deer & the other a common Deer, the party killed Several this morning all for the use of their Skins which are now good, one man Gibson wounded a verry large brown bear, too late this evening to prosue him\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We passed two Creeks in a bend to the Lard Side neither them had any water, [3]\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 13th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew verry hard all last night. Some\nSprinkling rain and high wind this morning. Some men out hunting \u00a0\u00a0 about\none oC. P M the wind abated So that we Set off though the hunters had not\nall returned. \u00a0\u00a0 the afternoon pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the bottoms and bluffs on each Side \u00a0\u00a0 passed the mouths of three creeks 2 on S. S.\nand one on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 9 miles and Camped in a large bottom on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters all joined \u00a0\u00a0 had killed several Elk & deer and wounded a\nbrown bear on the hills. \u00a0\u00a0 the current Swift (came 9 mls.)\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 13th \u00a0\u00a0 The weather continued stormy, and some few drops of rain \nfell. At 1 P. M. we embarked; passed three creeks, one on the North side and \ntwo on the South; went seven miles and encamped [4] in a large bottom.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMonday 13th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew hard all last night. \u00a0\u00a0 Some rain \nhigh wind and Squawls of rain this morning, So that we did not Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 \nSome men went out hunting and killed Several Elk and deer. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 oC. \np. m. the weather cleared off pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind abated and we Set off \u00a0\u00a0 \nSome of the hunters had not returned. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 the current \nSwift \u00a0\u00a0 passed hills on each Side which make near the River only the bottoms \non the points & in the bends. \u00a0\u00a0 came 9 miles and Camped in a bottom on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters joined us one of them had wounded a white or brown \nbear. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a creek at the lower end of this bottom, the Current verry \nSwift in the Missourie. \u00a0\u00a0 came about 9 miles this day.

\n

\nMonday May 13th \u00a0\u00a0 The wind continued blowing hard all last night, and \nthis morning, we had Squalls of rain, & high wind, which occasioned our not \nsetting off Early,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 some of our Men went out hunting, and killed several \nElk and deer\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 o'Clock P. M. the weather cleared off & became \npleasant, and the wind abated.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We then set off and proceeded on our \nVoyage, (some of the hunters that went out this morning had not returned \nto us,) the current of the River running very swift, we passed some hills, \nwhich make in, near to the River, the bottom land being only on the points, \nand in the bends of the River, We came 9 Miles, and encamped in a bottom, \nlying on the South side of the River \u00a0\u00a0 At the lower end of this bottom \u2329is\u232a \nRun a large creek, Our hunters joined us here with the game that they had \nkilled, One of the hunters had wounded a brown bear, but he did not get \nhim, The current running very Strong in the Mesouri at this place\u2014\n\n\n\n
