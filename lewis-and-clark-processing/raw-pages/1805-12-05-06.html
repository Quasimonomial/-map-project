\n December 5, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n December 5th Thursday 1805\n [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSom \u2329Showers of\u232a hard Showers of rain last night, this morn Cloudy and drisly rain, in the bay above the Showers appear harder. \u00a0\u00a0 High water to day at 12 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 this tide is 2 Ins. higher than that of yesterday \u00a0\u00a0 all our Stores again wet by the hard Showers of last night \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis's long delay below has been the cause of no little uneasiness on my part for him, a 1000 conjectures has crouded into my mind respecting his probable Situation & Safty\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 rained hard. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis returned haveing found a good Situation and Elk Suffient to winter on, his party killed 6 Elk & 5 Deer in their absence in Serch of a Situation and game

\n

\nRain continued all the after pt. of the day accompanied with hard wind from the S W. which provents our moveing from this Camp.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n Thursday 5th of December 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSome hard Showers of rain last night, this morning Cloudy and drisley at Some little distanc above the isthmus the rain is much harder. \u00a0\u00a0 high water to day at 12 \u00a0\u00a0 this tide is 2 inches higher than that of yesterday. \u00a0\u00a0 all our Stores and bedding are again wet by the hard rain of last night. Capt. Lewis's long delay below, has been the Sorce of no little uneasness on my part of his probable Situation and Safty, the repeeted rains and hard winds which blows from the S, W. renders it impossible for me to move with loaded Canoes along an unknown Coast \u00a0\u00a0 we are all wet & disagreeable; the party much better of indispositions\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis returned with 3 men in the Canoe and informs me that he thinks that a Sufficient number of Elk may be prcured Convenient to a Situation on a Small river which falls into a Small bay a Short distance below, that his party had Killed 6 Elk & 5 Deer in his rout, two men of his party left behind to Secure the Elk

\n

\nthis was verry Satisfactory information to all the party. \u00a0\u00a0 we accordingly deturmined to proceed on to the Situation which Capt. Lewis had Viewed as Soon as the wind and weather Should permit and Comence building huts &c.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday 5th Decr. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 rainy dissagreeable weather. \u00a0\u00a0 about noon Capt. Lewis and three men returned and informed us that they had found a tollarable good place for our winters quarters about 15 miles down the South Shore, a Short distance up a Small River. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 they had killed 7 Elk and five Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men stayed to take care of the meat\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 5th. \u00a0\u00a0 Again we had a wet stormy day, so that the men were unable to proceed with the canoes. About 11 o'clock Capt. Lewis and three of his party came back to camp; the other two were left to take care of some meat they had killed. They have found a place about 15 miles from this camp, up a small river [3] which puts into a large bay on the south side of the Columbia, that will answer very well for winter quarters, as game is very plenty, which is the main object with us; and we intend to move there as soon as circumstances will admit. There is more wet weather on this coast, than I ever knew in any other place; during a month, we have had three fair days; and there is no prospect of a change.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday Decemr. 5th \u00a0\u00a0 We had hard rain & stormy weather; which was very disagreeable. \u00a0\u00a0 About 12 o'Clock A. M. Captain Lewis & 3 Men who were part of those that went with him returned to Camp with the Canoe. \u00a0\u00a0 They informed us, that they had found a tolerable good place, [4] to build our Winter quarters at. \u00a0\u00a0 The place they said lay up a small river, [5] about 4 Miles on the South side; & about 15 Miles from this place. \u00a0\u00a0 They had killed seven Elk; and had left 2 of the Men to take care of the meat hides & had also killed 5 deer. \u00a0\u00a0 They brought some of the Meat with them. \u00a0\u00a0 It continued raining the whole of this day.\u2014\n\n\n\n\n
