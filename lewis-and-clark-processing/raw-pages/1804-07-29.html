\n July 29 Sunday \u00a0\u00a0 We Sent one frenchman \n le Liberty\n \n [2] & the Indian to the Camp to envite the party to meet us at the next bend of High Land on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 a Dark morning \u00a0\u00a0 wind from the W. N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 rained all last night\u2014 Set out at 5 oClock &, proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Island, opposit this Island on the S. S. the Creek called Indian Knob Creek which mouths Several miles on a Direct line below, is within 20 feet of the Missouri & about 5 feet higher

\n

\nCought three large Cat fish to day verry fat \u00a0\u00a0 one of them nearly white those Cat are So plenty that they may be Cought in any part of this River but fiew fish of any other Kind.\u2014

\n

\n(4) at the commencement of this course passed much fallen timber apparently the ravages of a dreadful haricane which had passed obliquely across the river from N. W. to S. E. about twelve months since. \u00a0\u00a0 many trees were broken off near the ground the trunks of which were sound and four feet in diameter. [3]\n

\n

\n Willard lost his gun in Bowyers R. \u00a0\u00a0 R. Fields Dive & brought it up [4]\n

\n

\nAll the Wood Land on this part of the Missouries Appear to be Confined to the Points & Islands

\n

\n Boyers River is provably [probably] 25 yds. Wide, Willard near loseing his Gun in this river, two men Sick & Sevral with Boils, a Cold Day \u00a0\u00a0 Wind from the N W. \u00a0\u00a0 Som rain the fore part of the Day. [5]\n

\nSent a french man \n la Liberty\n with the Indian to Otteaze Camp to invite the Indians to meet us on the river above\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 a Dark rainey morning \u00a0\u00a0 wind from the W. N. W.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 rained all the last night\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out at 5 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 opposit the (1) Island, \u2329in\u232a the bend to the right or S. S. is within 20 feet of Indian Knob Creek, the water of this Creek is 5 feet higher than that of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Isld. \u00a0\u00a0 we Stoped to Dine under Some high Trees near the high land on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 in a fiew minits Cought three verry large Catfish (3) one nearly white, Those fish are in great plenty on the Sides of the river and verry fat, a quart of Oile Came out of the Surpolous fat of one of these fish \u00a0\u00a0 (4) above this high land & on the S. S. passed much falling timber apparently the ravages of a Dreadfull harican which had passed obliquely across the river from N. W. to S E about twelve months Since, many trees were broken off near the ground the trunks of which were Sound and four feet in diameter, (2) about \u00be of a Me. above the Island on the S. S. a Creek coms in Called Boyers R. this Creek is 25 yards wide, \u00a0\u00a0 one man in attempting to Cross this Creek on a log let his gun fall in, R. Fields Dived & brought it up \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on to a Point on the S. S. and Camped. [6]\n

[Ordway]

\u00a0

\nSunday July 29th 1804. Rain all last night. Cloudy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out Eairly. Jo. Barter\n [7] a Frenchman who could Speak the Zoteau language went with the Indian, in order to Git as many of them together as possable & bring them to the River above this \u2329where\u232a place So that we may See & treat with them &C.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we proceded on along a large bottom prarie on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Creek on N. S. called Boyer Creek, about noon we came to high land on the S. S. where we Stoped to Dine & jerk our meat which Drewyer brought to us. Willard Sent back to last nights Camp for his Tommahawk, which he left \u00a0\u00a0 we Delayed about 2 hours, Caught Several of the largest cat fish we have ever caught in this River.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 (one Swallowed a hook bit of the line, caught the Same G. hook) \u00a0\u00a0 the Missouri is much more crooked since we passed the Great River Platte than before but not So Rapid in general; & more praries, the Timber Scarser &C\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Timber mostly Cottonwood except on the hills: which is oak Black Walnut hickery Elm Bass wood [8] &C\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Willard lost his rifle in a \u2329Small\u232a large Creek Called Boyer\n [9] N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 came back for help to find it. \u00a0\u00a0 the white pearogue went back with him & got out his Rifle, which was sunk deep in the mud, we proceded on along a round bend & prarie on N. S. We Camped on the North Side in a thin Grove of cottonwood.\u2014

July 29th Sunday 1804

\nSunday July 29th \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out after we Dspashed the Indian and one of ouer men [10] with him to bring the Rest of his party \u00a0\u00a0 the Reasen this man Gives of His being with So Small a party is that He Has not Got Horses to Go in the Large praries after the Buflows but Stayes about the Town and River to Hunte the Elke to Seporte \u2329His\u232a thare famileys \u00a0\u00a0 passed the mouth of Boyers River on the N. Side \u00a0\u00a0 it about 30 yards wide \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Low Bottom Land \u00a0\u00a0 out from the River is High Hills \u00a0\u00a0 Campt on the North Side at a prarie\n\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday, 29th. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early, and continued our voyage. One of our Frenchmen [11] went with the Indian to bring more of them to meet us at some convenient landing place. At 12 one of our hunters came in with a deer and some elk meat. We renewed our voyage at 3, passed a bank, where there was a quantity of fallen timber, and encamped on the north side.\n\n

\nSunday 29 \u00a0\u00a0 the Morning was Rany \u00a0\u00a0 the indian and LiBerty\n [12] went to the nation to Bring the rest of them to a treaty \u00a0\u00a0 the hunter Come to us at 12 CLock with Some EaLk meat and one deare \u00a0\u00a0 the Camp [13] was near the Same Praerie Land \u00a0\u00a0 Some groves of timBer weL wartered \u00a0\u00a0 I Cut my [illegible] on the 27 \u00a0\u00a0 had to Lay By my ower \u00a0\u00a0 the Cout was one inch and half Long \u00a0\u00a0 WiLard Left his tommehake weare we Camped on the night of the 28 Instan [illegible words] \u00a0\u00a0 we Came to the Grean Prarie \u00a0\u00a0 it [is] very hansom \u00a0\u00a0 the hils Com in near the river \u00a0\u00a0 th[ere] Come in smale Creak on the West Sd of the river \u00a0\u00a0 Cald it Potts Creak about 20 yds at the Mouth. [14] \u00a0\u00a0 Roed 11\u00bd \u00a0\u00a0 Campd. on the E. S.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Joseph fields Shot a Brareowe [15] \u00a0\u00a0 he is the form of a dog. \u00a0\u00a0 his colour is Gray his talents on the four feet is 1\u00bd Inch long his picture never was Seen by any of the party before.

\n

\nSunday July 29th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning was rainey, we started at sunrise. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis sent the Zoto Indian that had come to us the day before; and a Canadian named Liberty; to the Zoto nation; to bring the Warriors and chiefs to Council Bluff, in order to hold a treaty with them, The Canadian Liberty never returned to us, this put the Captains much at a loss to know what had become of him, fearing the Indians had killed him.\u2014

\n

\nWe rowed 11 Miles this day & encamp'd on the South side of the River\n\n\n
