\n May 18, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Saturday May 18th 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind blew hard this morning from the West. \u00a0\u00a0 we were enabled to employ our toe line the greater part of the day and therefore proceeded on tolerably well. \u00a0\u00a0 there are now but few sandbars, the river is narrow and current gentle. \u00a0\u00a0 the timber consists of a few cottonwood trees along the verge of the river; the willow has in a great measure disappeared. \u00a0\u00a0 in the latter part of the day the hills widened, the bottoms became larger, and contained more timber. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a creek [EC: Wisers] on the Stard. side about three oclock, which afforded no water; [1] came too and encamped on the Lard. side opposite to the lower point of a small Island, two miles short of the extremity of the last course of this day. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Clark in the course of his walk this evening killed four deer, two of which were the black tailed or mule deer; the skins are now good, they have not yet produced their young.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we saw a number of buffaloe, Elk, deer and Antelopes.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the saline substance frequently mentioned continues to appear as usual.\u2014

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 18th Satturday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\t\t\t\t \nA windey morning wind from the West \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on verry well with the assistance of the Toe Coard, river narrow but fiew Sand bars, & current jentle, but a few Cotton Trees Contained in the bottoms \u00a0\u00a0 willow is not common on the bears [bars] as usial \u00a0\u00a0 Some little on the Sides of the river is yet to be Seen, the after part of the day was Cloudy & at about 12 oClock it began to rain and continued moderately for about 1\u00bd hours, not Sufficient to wet a man thro' his clothes; this is the first rain Since we Set out this Spring \u00a0\u00a0 The hills widen and the bottoms Contain more timber than for Several days past, we passed a Wisers Creek on the Std. Side about 3 oClock and Camped on the Lard Side opposit the lower point of a handsom little Island near the middle of the river. I walked on Shore and killed four Deer, 2 common & 2 mule deer, one of which had 3 fauns, 2 others had 2 each, those deer are fat, & their Skins tolerable good, which are now in demand with us for clothes Such as Legins & Mockersons, I Saw great numbers of Buffalows & Elk; Some of the party Shoot & Catch beaver every day & night

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 18th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear warm morning. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party caught a beaver last night. \u00a0\u00a0 another killed a rattle Snake [5] this morning \u00a0\u00a0 at 7 oClock Set off and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed verry high rough hills, which look mountainous and make in to the River on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms verry narrow, but little timber only Spots of pitch pine on Sd hills. \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms are generally covered with rose [6] bushes & Rabbit berry bushes \u00a0\u00a0 [two lines illegible] [7] to dine at a large bottom covered with timber on N. S. [8] which was filled with buffaloe Elk deer &.c. Capt. Clark killed a fat deer. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party killed 2 Elk. another killed a beaver \u00a0\u00a0 about one oC. P. M. cleared up pleasant, about 2 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed pitch pine hills on each Side of the River \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oC. we passed a large Creek [9] on N. S. & a large timbered bottom on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 the Missourie is gitting clear and gravelly bottom, & Shore \u00a0\u00a0 we passed no falling in banks as we did below these pitch pine hilly country. \u00a0\u00a0 a pleasant warm afternoon. Capt Clark killed three Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 19 miles to day and Camped in a Smooth bottom thinly covered with c. w. timber on the South Side.\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 18th. \u00a0\u00a0 A cloudy morning. We proceeded as usual. The country much the same as yesterday; until about 12 o'clock, when the bottoms became more extensive on both sides of the river. There is still a small quantity of pine timber on the hills. We had some showers of rain in the forenoon; hail in the afternoon; and a fine clear evening. We went nineteen miles and encamped on the South side opposite an island.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 18th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear warm morning. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party killed a rattle Snake. \u00a0\u00a0 another caught a beaver. \u00a0\u00a0 about 7 oC. we Set out and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Some narrow bottoms of timber & covered with rose bushes. \u00a0\u00a0 about 10 oC. clouded up and began to rain. \u00a0\u00a0 we had Several Small Showers. \u00a0\u00a0 about 12 oC Capt. Clark killed a fat deer. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted to dine and dry our Selves at a large bottom covered with timber on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 this bottom is filled with buffaloe Elk Deer &c \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party killed 2 Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 another killed a beaver. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 the weather cleared up and we had a pleasant afternoon \u00a0\u00a0 the River water is gitting clear and gravelly \nbottom &c. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 19 miles and Camped at a Smooth high bottom on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 N. B. (Capt. Clark killed three deer)

\n

\nSaturday May 18th \u00a0\u00a0 We had a fine warm morning, One of our party killed a rattle Snake of a large size and another caught a beaver, About 7 o'Clock we set out, and passed some narrow bottoms of timber, \u2329and\u232a covered with Rosebushes.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 At 10 oClock A. M. it clouded up and began to rain; and we had small Showers. \u00a0\u00a0 at 12 o'Clock A. M. Captain Clark went on shore, and killed a fat deer, We then halted to dine and to dry our Cloathes, at a large bottom covered with timber lying on the North side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 This bottom is filled with buffalo, elk, deer & other game, Two of our party killed 2 Elk and One beaver, About 2 o'Clock P. M We proceeded on our way, and \nthe weather cleared up, and we had a pleasant afternoon, Here the Water of the Mesouri River, that had been muddy ever since we first entered it, began to get clear, and the bottom that was mud, is gravelly\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Clark who had been out a hunting joind us in the Evening; having killed 3 deer, which we took on board the Crafts, We encamped that Evening on a high smooth bottom, lying on the South side of the River, having come 19 Miles this day\n\n\n\n
