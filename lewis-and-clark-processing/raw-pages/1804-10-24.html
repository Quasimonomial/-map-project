\n 24th October Wednesday 1804\n \n

\n

\nSet out early \u00a0\u00a0 a Cloudy day \u00a0\u00a0 Some little Snow in the morning \u00a0\u00a0 I am Something better of the Rhumutim in my neck\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 a butifull Countrey on both Sides of the river. The bottoms Covd. with wood, we have Seen no game on the river to day a prof of the Indians hunting in the neighbourhod \u00a0\u00a0 (1) passed a Island on the S. S. made by the river Cutting through a point, by which the river is Shortened Several miles\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 on this Isld. we Saw one of the Grand Chiefs of the Mandins, with five Lodges hunting, this Cheif met the Chief of the \n Ricares\n who accompanied us with great Cordiallity & Sermony \u00a0\u00a0 Smoked the pipe & Capt. Lewis with the Interpeter went with the Chiefs to his Lodges at 1 mile distant, after his return we admited the Grand Chief & his brother for a few minits on our boat. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on a Short distance and Camped on the S. S. below the old Village of the Mandins & ricares.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Soon after our landg. 4 Mandins Came from a Camp above, the Ricares Chief went with them to their Camp, \n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 24th Oct. Cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Bottom on N. S. covered with fine Timber Such as oak & large cottonwood &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 at 12 o.C. we halted at an Isl. on N. S. where we found a hunting camp of the Mandan Nation of Indians. \u00a0\u00a0 the chief we had on board Spoke to the chief of this party told them our business &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 they had Some handsome women with them. \u00a0\u00a0 at 2 oC. we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Isl where the River cut th[rough?] [paper worn, several words illegible] [6] which formerly used to be 10 miles Round by its cutting across forms the Isl. Camped on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 4 of the natives came to our Camp but did not remain with us but a\nShort time.\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 24th. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early in a cloudy morning. At 9 it began to rain and continued to rain for an hour. At 12 we came to a hunting party of the Mandan nation of Indians, and remained with them untill 2 and then continued our voyage. There were three lodges of these Indians on an island, which has been cut off the Grand Bend, a short distance below the Mandan village. We encamped on the north side. Five of the Indians came to us, and our Indian [7] went over with them and returned in the \nmorning.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWednesday October 24th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning was Cloudy, we set off early as usual. \u00a0\u00a0 at 9 o'Clock A. M. it began to rain, we stopped the Boat and Pettyaugers for about one hour, and then proceeded on, at 12 oClock A. M., we came too, were a party of Indians belonging to the Mandan Nation were hunting, we halted about two hours with them, and then proceeded on our Voyage, 'till the Evening and Encamped on the North side of the River, where five Indians of the Mandan Natlon came to us.\u2014\n\n\n\n

1. The only old village of this group of Indians on Atlas map 29 is the one mentioned in Codex C on October 23; the map clearly indicates that the twenty-third was the day they passed the site. The reference in Codex C, however, was clearly inserted later, placed by what Clark knew to be the correct date. MRC map 51. (back)
\n
2. Indicated clearly on Atlas map 29, this cutoff left an \"oxbow\" lake, later called Painted Woods Lake, on the McLean County, North Dakota, side of the Missouri, just above present Sanger. MRC map 51; MRY 151. (back)
\n
3. Perhaps two miles below present Washburn, McLean County. Atlas map 29 shows some six old villages in present Oliver County, below Hensler, some of them indicated as having been abandoned nine years, that is, since 1795. (back)
\n
4. The distance is one mile in Codex C. (back)
\n
5. This bearing is 35\u00b0 in Codex C. (back)
\n
6. The bottom of this page, and several pages following, are so badly curled and worn that\nsome of the text is lost or illegible. (back)
\n
7. The Arikara chief, Toone or Arketarnashar, who had accompanied them in hopes of making peace with the Mandans. See note to Clark's entry of October 9, 1804.\n (back)
