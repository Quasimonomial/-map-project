[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 10th \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly \u00a0\u00a0 a fair day. Some fish & one Beaver caught last night. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a place where the River had cut through & Shortened the river Several miles, we passed high Ridge of prarie land on S. S. Smooth plains back from the river, the Timber Scarse, a Great nomber of Elk Sign on the Sand beaches &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed high clifts on S. S. about 60 feet from the Surface of the of Sand Stone. \u00a0\u00a0 the ridge on the top is covered with Short Grass Some bushes &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind hard from the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 (Sailed Some.) \u00a0\u00a0 we came 22\u00bd miles this day & camped [6] on a Sand bar N. S. of the Missouri River.\n

\n Friday 10th. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early, passed high yellow banks on the south side, and encamped on the north.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nFriday 10 \u00a0\u00a0 the morning was Clear \u00a0\u00a0 the musquitoes was mighty troublesome Untill The Sun rose to Some hight. \u00a0\u00a0 Campd. On the E. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Roaed 24 mils

\n

\nFriday August 10th \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 We found the Musketoes very troublesome untill the Sun rose to some heighth, when they quitted us, the wind being fair we Sail'd the greatest part of this day. \u00a0\u00a0 We encamped in the Evening on the North [7] side of the River having rowed & sail'd 24 Miles.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n \n
\n
1. The courses and distances appear to be the only entry for August 10, 1804, in the Field Notes. The similar brevity of the Codex A entry for this day suggests that Clark had little time or energy left at the end of the day. A figure near the course heading appears to be 19\u00be. (back)
\n
2. Coup\u00e9e \u00e0 Jacques means simply \"Jacques's Cut.\" Part of it apparently forms present Blue Lake, at Lewis and Clark State Park, Monona County, Iowa. Atlas map 15; McDermott (WCS), 150; Osgood (FN), 103 n. 8; MRC map 26; MRR map 72. (back)
\n
3. Two quarries along the valley and bluffs near Fort Calhoun, Nebraska, have been excavated in limestones of Pennsylvania age, but no sandstone is reported in them. The Dakota sandstone (Cretaceous) has not been reported in this area. The hill is immediately north of Decatur, Burt County, Nebraska, and appears prominently in Nicollet. Atlas map 15; Nicollet (MMR), 391; MRC map 26; MRR map 72. (back)
\n
4. See Atlas map 15. They visited this landmark the next day. (back)
\n
5. In Monona County, considerably east of the Missouri River's present course and, as Clark indicates, east or southeast of Blackbird Hill. Atlas map 15; MRC map 26; MRR map 72. (back)
\n
6. In Monona County, Iowa, across the river and a few miles above the Thurston-Burt\ncounty line in Nebraska. (back)
\n
7. Again written over \"South.\" (back)
