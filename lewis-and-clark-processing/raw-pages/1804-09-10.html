\n \n 10th September Monday 1804.\n

\n

\na Cloudy dark morning \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early, a Gentle breeze from the S. E, passed two Small Islands on the L. S. and one on the S. S. all in the first Course \u00a0\u00a0 at 10\u00bd miles passed the lower point of an (2) Island Covered with red Ceeder Situated in a bend on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 this Island is about 2 Moles [miles] in length \u00a0\u00a0 (1) below this on a hill on the L. S. we found the back bone of a fish, 45 feet long tapering to the tale, \u2329Some teeth\u232a &c. \u00a0\u00a0 those joints were Seperated and all petrefied, opposit this Island 1\u00bd miles from the river on the L. S. is a large Salt Spring of remarkable Salt water. \u00a0\u00a0 one other high up the hill \u00bd me. not So Salt.

\n

\nwe proceeded on under a Stiff Breeze. \u00a0\u00a0 three miles above Ceder Island passed a large Island on the S. S, no water on that Side (3) \u00a0\u00a0 Several elk Swam to this Island \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Island near the Center of the river, of a mile in length, and Camped on one aboav Seperated from the other by a narrow Chanel, Those Islands are Called Mud Islands\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters killed 3 fuffalow & one Elk to day. The river is falling a little, Great number of Buffalow & Elk on the hill Sides feeding \u00a0\u00a0 deer Scerce\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 10th \u00a0\u00a0 a foggy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a run & a Bluff on N. S. which is of a hard blue clay & verry rough on the top \u00a0\u00a0 (a Small crk on L. Side) \u00a0\u00a0 we Sailed on verry well \u00a0\u00a0 halted at 12 oClock took dinner. Newman went out on the hills & killed a Deer South Side \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw the rack of Bones of a verry large fish [10] \u00a0\u00a0 the Back bone 45 feet long. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a high Black Bluff S. S. See 8 Elk Swimming the River. See a nomber of Buffalow. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Islands & Several Creeks & Sand bars little Timber in the course of the day. \u00a0\u00a0 and camped on an Island S. S. [11] \u00a0\u00a0 Sergt. Pryor who walked on Shore to day joined us \u00a0\u00a0 informed us that he Saw a large Salt Spring S S 1\u00bd miles from the river, & killed one Buffalow, Corpl. Worvington of the pearogue walked the afternoon on S. S \u00a0\u00a0 joined us also killed 2 Buffalow\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 10th. \u00a0\u00a0 We had a foggy morning, but moved on early; passed high bluffs on the north side, and saw some timber in the bottom on the south side. At 12 we came to black sulphur bluffs on the south side. [12] On the top of these bluffs we found the skeleton or back bones of a fish, 45 feet long, and petrified: part of these bones were sent to the City of Washington. One of our sergeants [13] discovered a large salt spring about a mile and an half from the river. A hunter went up the bank and killed an elk. We left a periogue for the men who were dressing the elk, and proceeded up the north side of the river two miles, when we were obliged to return on account of sand bars, \nand to take the south side. Here we saw eight elk swimming the river, and had seen a great many buffaloe during the day. We encamped on an island and killed one buffaloe.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0

\nMonday 10th Sept. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 a foggy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw a ruck of Bones on the Bank S. S. which appeared to be the Bones of a monstrous large fish the Back Bone is 45 feet long. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters killed three Buffalow & a Deer

\n

\nMonday September 10th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning was foggy, we set out early, and proceeded on; we landed and saw lying on the banks on the South side of the River, the Bones of a monstrous large Fish, the back bone of which measured, forty five feet long. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters returned, having killed 3 Buffalo, and one deer. \u00a0\u00a0 We encamp'd on the South side of the River on the bank.\u2014\n\n\n\n\n
