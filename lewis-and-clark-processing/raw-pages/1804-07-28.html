\n July 28, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nJuly 28th Satturday \u00a0\u00a0 Set out this morning early, the wind blou from the N. W. by N. \u00a0\u00a0 a Dark Smokey Morning, Some rain \u00a0\u00a0 at 1 me. passed a Bluff on the S. S. it being the first high land approachig the river above the Nodaway, a Island and Creek S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 just abov this creek I call \u2329Bald\u232a Indian Knob\n [1] \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer Came with a Dee[r] & informs he heard fireing to the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 I walked on Shore on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 found some good Prarie out from the S. pt. The High Lands approach the river 1st bend to left \u00a0\u00a0 The party on Shore brought in a \n Missouri\n Indian who resides with the Otteauz, this Indian & 2 others were Hunting in the Prarie \u00a0\u00a0 their Camp is about 4 miles off. This Indian informs that his nation [2] is in the Plains hunting the Buffalow, the party with which he is encamped is about 20 familey Hunting the Elk, we landed on S. S. below an Island [3]\n

[Clark]

\n July the 28th, Satturday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out this morning early, the wind from the N W. by N. \u00a0\u00a0 a Dark Smokey morning \u00a0\u00a0 Some rain \u00a0\u00a0 passed at 1 me. a Bluff on the S. S. the first high land above the Nodaway aproaching the river on that Side \u00a0\u00a0 a Island and Creek 15 yds. wide on the S. S. above this Bluff, as this Creek has no name call it Indian Knob Creek \u00a0\u00a0 our party on Shore Came to the river and informs that they heard fireing to the S W. \u00a0\u00a0 below this High Land on the S. S. the Aiawuay [Iowa] Indians formerly lived, [5] \u2329below this old village about \u23297\u232a 5 miles passed Some monds on the L S. in a bend Where the \n Otteauze\n Indians formerly lived, This Situation I examined found it well situated for Defence \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 or 300 acres of Land Covered with mounds\u232a [6] \u00a0\u00a0 The flank came in & informed they heard two Guns to the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 the high land approaches in the 1st bend to the left, we camped on the S. S. below the point of an Island, G Drewyer brought in a \n Missourie Indian which he met with hunting in the Prarie \u00a0\u00a0 This Indian is one of the fiew remaining of that nation, & lives with the Otteauz, his Camp about 4 miles from the river, he informs that the 'great gangue' of the nation were hunting the Buffalow in the Plains. \u00a0\u00a0 h[i]s party was Small Consisting only of about 20 Lodges, [blank] miles furthr a nother Camp where there was a french man, who lived in the nation, This Indian appeared spritely, and appeared to make use of the Same pronouncation of the Osarge, Calling a Chief Inca\n [7]\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday July 28th 1804. Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded\non past a h. bottom prarie on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 back of these praries a high Ridge with Some Timbers on it & in the vallies near the prarie \u00a0\u00a0 above these praries the hills made in close & Steep to the River. \u00a0\u00a0 a nomber of high round knobs on those hills which are bare from Timber. Some Timber in the vallies \u00a0\u00a0 we passed the mouth of a Small Creek which comes in behind an Island from among those Ridges which is named Round Knob Creek. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 the land opt. on the S. S. is low, the Timber mostly Cotton wood. G. Drewyer came to the bank with the horses, brought in a Deer which he killed \u00a0\u00a0 The wind hard from the N. E. Detained us Some time \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on passt a high bank on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 thin Timbers on the N. S. G. Drewyer found three of the Zotaus Indians Dressing an Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 they were friendly and Gave him a part of it and one of them came with him in order to find the Boat. Drewyer killed one Deer & joined us \u00a0\u00a0 brought the Indian with him, where we was Camped on the north Side of the River in Timbered land below an Isld. & prarie.\u2014\n\n

[Floyd]

\u00a0

\nSatturday July 28th \u00a0\u00a0 Set out verry erley this morning \u00a0\u00a0 prossed on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek on the \u2329South\u232a North Side Called Beaver Creek\n [9] \u00a0\u00a0 is about\n20 yrds wide \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Low \u00a0\u00a0 that on the South is Prarie Land \u00a0\u00a0 Rain the\nfore part of the day \u00a0\u00a0 the Latter part Clear with wind from the North Est. \u00a0\u00a0 made 10 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Campt on the N. Side \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Low \u00a0\u00a0 that on the South is High prarie Land \u00a0\u00a0 ouer flanken partey Came with one Indian thay found on the South Side\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday, 28th. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early; had a cloudy morning: passed some beautiful hills and prairies, and a creek called Round-Knob creek, [10] on the north side; and high bluffs on the south. We encamped on the north side. Here two of our hunters came to us, accompanied by one of the Oto Indians. [11]\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday July 28th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 proceded on past a high Bottom prarie on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Some Timber on the Ridge back of those praries \u00a0\u00a0 above the Bottom prarie the hills make in close to the River verry high & Steep. \u00a0\u00a0 we passd. the mouth of a Small Creek on N. S. named Round Knob Creek. [12] \u00a0\u00a0 the wind Blew hard from N. E. \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer joined us at 11 oC with one Deer. [13] \u00a0\u00a0 we Came to a hi CLift or Buut one hun[dred] feet [14] \u00a0\u00a0 the Barge Struck a Sand Bare on the Side of the River on the Star Bord S. \u00a0\u00a0 inCampe on the north Side of the [river] at the foot of a iLand CaLd the BLuf iLand [15] \u00a0\u00a0 we Rowed 10 Miles that day. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunterers Comin and Brought one indian with [them &c?] [16]\n

\n

\nSaturday July 28th \u00a0\u00a0 The morning still continued Cloudy, we set out early, and passed a high bottom Priari on the North side; having on the back side of them high ridges with Trees growing on them. \u00a0\u00a0 above the bottom Priari the hills make in close to the River; and are very high and steep, we passed the Mouth of a Creek, laying on the North side of the River, which is called knob Creek, the wind blowing hard from the North East.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters came to us having One deer which he had killed, with him.\u2014

\n

\nThis hunter came to us opposite a remarkable high hill laying on the North side of the River, we took the meat on board, and proceeded on one Mile, when the boat struck a sand barr, on her larbourd Side, and all hands were obliged to jump out in the Water to prevent her from sinking, (the place the boat Grounded on being quick sand) \u00a0\u00a0 with much difficulty we got her off, we then proceeded one Mile, & encamp'd at the point of an Island, laying on the South side of the River.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters came in and brought an Indian with him of the Zoto Nation \u00a0\u00a0 distance come this day 10 Miles.\u2014\n\n\n\n
