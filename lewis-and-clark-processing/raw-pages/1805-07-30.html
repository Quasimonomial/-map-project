\n July 30, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Tuesday July 30th 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Capt. Clark being much better this morning and having completed my observations we reloaded our canoes and set out, ascending Jeffersons river. \u00a0\u00a0 Sharbono, his woman two invalleds and myself walked through the bottom of the Lard. side of the river about 4\u00bd miles when we again struck it at the place the woman informed us that she was taken prisoner. [1] here we halted untill Capt. Clark arrived which was not untill after one P.M. the water being strong and the river extreemly crooked. \u00a0\u00a0 we dined and again proceeded on; as the river now passed through the woods the invalleds got on board together with Sharbono and the Indian woman; I passed the river and continued my walk on the Stard. side. \u00a0\u00a0 saw a vast number of beaver in many large dams which they had maid in various bayoes of the river which are distributed to the distance of three or four miles on this side of the river over an extensive bottom of timbered and meadow lands intermixed. \u00a0\u00a0 in order to avoid these bayoes and beaver dams which I found difficult to pass I directed my course to the high plain to the right which I gained after some time with much difficulty and waiding many beaver dams to my waist in mud and water. \u00a0\u00a0 I would willingly have joined the canoes but the brush were so thick, the river crooked and bottoms intercepted in such manner by the beaver dams, that I found it uceless to attempt to find them, and therefore proceeded on up the river in order to intersept it where it came near the plain and woult be more collected into one channel. \u00a0\u00a0 at length about sunset I arrived at the river only about six miles from my calculation on a direct line from the place I had left the canoes but I thought they were still below me. I found the river was divided where I reached it by an Island and was therefore fearfull that they might pass without my seeing them, and went down to the lower point of the large island; here I discovered a small Island, close under the shore on which I was; I passed the narrow channel to the small island and examined the gravly bar along the edge of the river for the tracks of the men, knowing from the appearance of the river at this place that if they had passed they would have used the cord on the side where I was. I saw no tracks and was then fully convinced that they were below me. I fired my gun and hallooed but counld hear nothing of them. \u00a0\u00a0 by this time it was getting nearly dark and a duck lit on the shore in about 40 steps of me and I killed it; having now secured my supper I looked our for a suitable place to amuse myself in combating the musquetoes for the ballance of the evening. I found a parsel of drift wood at the head of the little Island on which I was and immediately set it on fire and collected some willow brush to lye on. I cooked my duck which I found very good and after eating it layed down and should have had a comfortable nights lodge but for the musquetoes which infested me all night. \u00a0\u00a0 late at night I was awakened by the nois of some animal runing over the stoney bar on which I lay but did not see it; from the weight with which it ran I supposed it to be either an Elk or a brown bear. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 the latter are very abundant in this neighbourhood. \u00a0\u00a0 the night was cool but I felt very little inconvenience from it as I had a large fire all night. Capt. Clark had proceeded on after I seperated from him and encamped on a islad. only about 2 miles below me [3] but did not hear the report of my gun nor of my hooping.\u2014I saw some deer and antelopes. [4]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n July 30th Monday 1805\n [5]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWe Set out 8 oClock and proceeded on 13\u00bd miles up the N. fork \u00a0\u00a0 the river verry rapid & Sholey \u00a0\u00a0 the Channel entirely Corse gravel [6] \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329passed the\u232a many Islands and a number of Chanels in different directions thro' the bottom &c. \u00a0\u00a0 passed the place the Squar interpretress was taken, one man with his Sholder Strained, [7] 2 with Tumers, we Camped on the Std. Side the evening Cool. Capt Lewis who walkd on Shore did not join me this evening\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n July 30th Tuesday 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fine pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded all the canoes eairly and Set out about 9 oClock A. M. and proceeded on up the North fork \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis and Several Inveleeds walked on Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed large bottoms covered with cotten timber & thick brush willow &C. \u00a0\u00a0 the River crooked rapid and full of Islands &C. \u00a0\u00a0 a bundance of beaver & beaver dams\nalong these bottoms. \u00a0\u00a0 thee currents of different kinds abound in these bottoms. \u00a0\u00a0 we dined at a Camp where the snake Indians had been camped 4 years ago, and was actacted by the Gross vauntaus. \u00a0\u00a0 2 or three of the Snake nation was killed, and Several Squaws taken prisoners \u00a0\u00a0 our Intrepters wife was one of them. She tells us that She was taken in the middle of the River as She was crossing at a Shole place to make hir ascape. \u00a0\u00a0 the rest all mounted their horses and cleared themselves as they do not wish to fite, neither were they Strong enofe for the Grossvauntaus. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters on Shore killed a Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 we peroceded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a verry large plain or prarie on L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 considerable of fine good flax on it, also wild onions or garlick. \u00a0\u00a0 passed high land on S. Side \u00a0\u00a0 we Came 13\u00bd miles this day and Camped on the Stard. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis did not join us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 these bottoms along the River are low and many beaver dams which causes\nponds in many places &C.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 30th. \u00a0\u00a0 We left our encampment at the forks, and proceeded on about 7 o'clock A. M. up the north branch. [8] This branch is about 60 yards wide and 6 feet deep, with a rapid current. We passed a number of islands. The valley continued on the south side all this day; but the spur of a mountain, about 5 or 6 miles from the forks came in close on the north side with very high cliffs of rocks. We encamped where it terminated, having made 13 miles and an half.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 30th July 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded the canoes eairly and Set out about 9 oClock and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis and Several men walked on Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed large bottoms of cotton timber. \u00a0\u00a0 the River crooked rapid and full of Islands. \u00a0\u00a0 the under bushes thick. \u00a0\u00a0 the currents abound. \u00a0\u00a0 the beaver pleanty. \u00a0\u00a0 a nomber of beaver dams behind the Islands &c. \u00a0\u00a0 we dined at the upper end of the bottoms close by a clear open prarie or plain. \u00a0\u00a0 at this place our Intrepters wife was taken prisoner 4 years ago by a war party of the grossvauntaus. \u00a0\u00a0 they took hir as She was attempting to make hir ascape by crossing a Shole place on the River, but was taken in the middle of it. \u00a0\u00a0 2 or 3 Indians killed at the Same time on Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 the rest of the Snakes made their ascape. \u00a0\u00a0 the day warm, and verry pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters killed a deer. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 the current verry Swift & rapids common. \u00a0\u00a0 passed beautiful large plains on L. S. and high land on the S. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 13\u00bd miles this day and Camped on the Stard. side. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis did not join us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 these bottoms are low & many beaver dams which causes pond &c.

\n

\nTuesday July 30th \u00a0\u00a0 A Clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded our Canoes early, and proceeded on our Voyage; about 9 o'Clock A. M., Captain Lewis and several of our party walked on shore.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on and passed large bottoms of Cotton timber, the River being Crooked, rapid, & full of Islands, the under wood thick & currants growing along the Shores in abundance.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Beaver here, \u2329and\u232a were seen by our party plenty, & We saw a number of their dams, laying behind the Islands & other places\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We halted to dine at the upper end of the bottoms, near which lay a Priari or plain, which was Clear & open, & without any bushes on it.\u2014

\n

\n Our Interpreters Wife the Indian Woman, related to us, that between 3 & 4 Years ago she was taken prisoner at the forks of the three rivers, by a Warr party of the Gros Vaunters or Big belley Indians, and that she had attempted to make her escape, with some others of her nation, but that she was retaken by them in the Middle of the Priari which lies near to us, that 3 of her nation was killed along the Shore, by the same party, that she was taken by but that the greater part, of the party that she was along with (Snake Nation) had made their escape;\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 This day was warm but yet pleasant, One of our hunters killed a deer which he brought to us.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on at 2 o'Clock P. M. and found the current running very swift, and a number of rapids, We passed beautiful large plains which lay on the South side of the River & high lands lying on the North side; Towards Evening we encamped on the North side of the River in a fine bottom of Timbered land, and came 13\u00bd Miles this day.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Country that we passed through this day, appeared to be rich & fertile, but it lay tumbling on the North side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis & the party that went with him, did not return to us this Evening\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The bottoms along the River this day lies low, and have ponds in them, occasioned by the Beaver Dams, which are very plenty\n\n\n\n\n
