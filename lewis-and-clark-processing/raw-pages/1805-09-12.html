\n September 12, 1805\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n September 12th Thursday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\na white frost \u00a0\u00a0 Set out at 7 oClock & proceeded on up the Creek, passed a Fork [1] on the right on which I saw near an old Indian encampment a Swet [NB: Sweat] house Covered wthh earth, at 2 miles assended a high hill & proceeded through a hilley and thickly timbered Countrey for 9 miles & on the Right [EC: hand side] of the Creek, passing Several branches from the right of fine clear water and Struck at a fork [2] at which place the road forks, one passing up each fork. The Timber is Short & long leaf Pine Spruce Pine & fur. [3] The road through this hilley Countrey is verry bad passing over hills & thro' Steep hollows, over falling timber &c. &c. \u00a0\u00a0 continued on & passed Some most intolerable road on the Sides of the Steep Stoney mountains, which might be avoided by keeping up the Creek which is thickly covered with under groth & falling timber Crossed a mountain 8 miles with out water & encamped on a hill Side on the Creek after Decending a long Steep mountain, [4] Some of our Party did not git up untill 10 oClock P M. I mad camp at 8 \u00a0\u00a0 on this roade & particularly on this Creek the Indians have pealed a number of Pine for the under bark which they eate at certain Season of the year, I am told in the Spring they make use of his bark [5] \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters Killed only one Pheasant this after noon. Party and horses much fatigued.\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday 12th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 a white frost. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded and Set out Soon after and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 Soon took the Mountains \u00a0\u00a0 came up and down Several Steep places \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several Small creeks and we descended a bad Step part of the Mout. and came down on the creek again and halted to dine \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters had killed this day 4 Deer and a pheasant \u00a0\u00a0 we dined and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 crossed 2 more creeks ascended up a mountain on a high ridge \u00a0\u00a0 a verry bad trail rough and\nrockey. [6] \u00a0\u00a0 we found no water nor place to Camp untill 10 oClock at night. \u00a0\u00a0 then descended a Steep part of the Mountain. \u00a0\u00a0 came down on the creek which we left this morning or at noon and we had came 17\u00bd miles this day. \u00a0\u00a0 and near Sd. creek where we could not find a level place to Sleep, and\nScarcely any feed for our horses\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 12th. \u00a0\u00a0 We started early on our journey and had a fine morning. Having travelled 2 miles we reached the mountains which are very steep; but the road over them pretty good, as it is much travelled by the natives, who come across to the Flathead river to gather cherries and berries. Our hunters in a short time killed 4 deer. At noon we halted at a branch of the creek, [7] on the banks of which are a number of strawberry vines, haws, and service berry bushes. At 2 we proceeded on over a large mountain, where there is no water, and we could find no place to encamp until late at night, when we arrived at a small branch, and encamped by it, in a very inconvenient place, \nhaving come 23 miles.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 12th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a white frost, and clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters Set out eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded up and Set out soon after Sunrise, and proceeded on a Short distance. \u00a0\u00a0 then took the mountains covred with pitch pine. \u00a0\u00a0 went up and down a nomber of bad hills and mot. \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several runs & about 1 oClock P. m. we descended a bad part of the mot. nearly Steep \u00a0\u00a0 came down on the creek a gain, and halted to dine. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters has killed this day 4 Deer and a fessent. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on crossed 2 more creeks, and assended a high rough mountain rockey & a verry rough trail to follow. \u00a0\u00a0 we proced. on along the ridge which was covred with pitch pine timber. \u00a0\u00a0 night came on and we had to go through the thickets of pine and over logs &c. untill about 10 oClock at in the evening before we could git any water. \u00a0\u00a0 then descended a Steep part of the mountain down on the Creek \u2329&\u232a which we left at noon, and Camped on the bank of the creek where we had Scarsely room to Sleep. \u00a0\u00a0 Came 17\u00bd miles this day. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw high Mountains to the South of us covred with Snow, which appears to lay their all the year round. \u00a0\u00a0 Scarsely any feed for our horses.

\n

\nThursday Septemr. 12th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning Clear weather with white frost & our hunters went out early to hunt; We loaded up our horses, and set out on our Journey, soon after sun rise, & proceeded on a short distance & took up \nto the Mountains, which were on their Tops cover'd with Pitch pine trees. \u00a0\u00a0 We then continued ascending & descending Mountains & bad hills & crossed several Runs.\u2014

\n

\nAbout 1 oClock P. M. we descended a bad part of the Mountains, which was nearly steep, & came down on the Creek which we had left,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 where we halted to dine & where our hunters came to us, & had killed 4 Deer & a \nPheasant which they brought to us, We halted for one hour & proceeded on, and crossed 2 Creeks, and ascended a high rough rockey mountain, & followed a very rough trail. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on along the ridge of one of these mountains which was covered with Pitch pine timber. \u00a0\u00a0 Night came on and we travelled in the dark, through thickets of pine Trees, & passed over logs & bad places untill about 10 o'Clock P. M. before we could get to a place where water was convenient to encamp at, which was at a steep part of the Mountain, which we descended down to a Creek, being the same which we \nhad left at noon. \u00a0\u00a0 We encamped on this Creek, where we had scarcely Room to lay down to Sleep, \u2329on\u232a having come about 17\u00bd Miles this day.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We found here, very little food for our horses, and saw Mountains this day which lay to the South of us covered with Snow, which lies on those Mountains during the whole Year.\u2014\n\n\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. \n Woodman Creek, Missoula County, Montana. Space, 5; Atlas map 69. (back)
\n
2. \n Grave Creek, Missoula County. Space, 6; Atlas map 69. (back)
\n
3. Short-leaf pine is probably lodgepole pine, Pinus contorta Dougl. ex Loud. Along the Jefferson River (see August 3, 1805) and earlier, the captains used the term short-leaf pine to refer to the limber pine. Now they are out of the range of limber pine, so short-leaf must refer to a new species, lodgepole pine. Little (CIH), 50-W, 56-W; Hitchcock et al., 1:125\u201326; information of Ralph S. Space, Orofino, Idaho. Long-leaf pine remains ponderosa pine. Spruce pine is Engelmann spruce as discussed in notes for September 9. Gass used the term on September 14 and Whitehouse on September 16, when the party was in an area where Engelmann spruce is the only logical tree of reference. Fir is either subalpine fir, Abies lasiocarpa (Hook.) Nutt., or Douglas fir, more likely the latter. Little (CIH), 7-W. (back)
\n
4. Some two miles below (east of) Lolo Hot Springs, Missoula County, near U.S. Highway 12. Space, 6; Peebles (LT), 3; Atlas map 69. (back)
\n
5. Ponderosa pine has edible underbark as described here. (back)
\n
6. The party passed along the north side of Lolo Creek, crossing Woodman and Grave creeks and a number of smaller streams, all in Missoula County, Montana. (back)
\n
7. They traveled up Lolo Creek, passing various branches, and nooned at Grave Creek, where the trail forks, in Missoula County, Montana. (back)
