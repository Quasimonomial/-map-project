\n \n 9th September Sunday 1804\n \n

\n

\nSet out at Sunrise and proceeded on passed the head of the Island on which we Camped, passed three Sand & willow Islands, the Sand bars So noumerous, it is not worth mentioning them, the river Shoal or Shallow wind S E \u00a0\u00a0 Came too and Camped on a Sand bar on the L. S. Capt Lewis went out to Kill a buffalow. I walked on Shore all this evening with a view to Kill a Goat or Some Prarie Dogs in the evening after the boat landed, I Derected my Servent York with me to kill a Buffalow near the boat from a numbr. then Scattered in the plains, I saw at one view near the river at least 500 Buffalow, those animals have been in view all day feeding in the Plains on the L. S. every Copse of timber appear to have Elk or Deer. D[rouillard]. Killed 3 Deer, I Kiled a Buffalow Y. [York] 2, R. Fields one.\n\n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 9th Sept. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at Sun rise & proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Island. Several gangues of Buffalow on the Sides of the hills on the South Side.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we halted took breakfast on the South Side opposite to the upper end of the Island. \u00a0\u00a0 we heared the prarie woolves bark on the hills \u00a0\u00a0 Capt.Clark went on Shore in order to kill or See Some of them &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed Several creeks & 2 Islands before dinner \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a high Bluff on S. S. & a Small creek at which place we took dinner. G. Drewyer killed 1 Buck & 2 fauns. R. Fields came to the Boat had killed one Buffalow. \u00a0\u00a0 passed red ceder on the in Several places along the Bluffs & Several creeks \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark joined us at dusk \u00a0\u00a0 had killed a Buffalow near where we camped on a Sand beach S. S. Capt. Clark informed us that the plains were allmost covered with Buffalow the most of this days walk. I saw Several on the bank.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 9th. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early, and passed two small creeks [9] on the north side, high bluffs on the south, and at one o'clock landed for dinner at a small creek [10] on the south side. One of our hunters [11] brought in a deer and two fawns. This day we saw several gangs or herds, of buffaloe on the sides of the hills: One of our hunters [12] killed one, and Captain Clarke's black servant [13] killed two. We encamped at sunset on the south side.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 9th Sept. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Several Gangs of Buffalow on the Side hills on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark walked on Shore \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several creeks. \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer killed a Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 R. Fields killed a Buffalow. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark killed a Bufalow.

\n

\nSunday September 9th \u00a0\u00a0 We sett off early this morning, and saw several Gangs of Buffalo which were on the side of some Hills, laying on the South side, of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Clark walked on shore & passed several Creeks. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, & encamp'd on the South side of the River in the Evening.\u2014\n\n\n\n

1. From the word \"halted\" to \"lower end of a Sand bar L. S.\" of this day's entry in the Field Notes (reverse of document 52) the writing appears to be in the hand of Private Joseph Whitehouse. 2. Either Spring or Pease creeks, in Charles Mix County, South Dakota, both nameless on Atlas map 20. Martha's Island and Pease Island on MRC map 34, of about 1890 do not correspond well in their relative positions to the islands shown on Atlas map 20, although they are in the same stretch of the river. One could be the Spear Island shown on Evans's map 1 (Atlas map 7). (back)

3. This point of observation is not marked on Atlas map 20. The creek may be Pease Creek or Campbell Creek, both nameless on the Atlas map. MRC map 34. (back)
