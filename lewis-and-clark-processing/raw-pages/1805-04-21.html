\n April 21, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Sunday April 21st 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out at an early hour this morning. Capt Clark walked on shore; the wind tho' a head was not violent. \u00a0\u00a0 the country through which we passed is very simelar in every rispect to that through which we have passed for several days.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We saw immence herds of buffaloe Elk deer & Antelopes. Capt Clark killed a buffaloe and 4 deer in the course of his walk today; and the party with me [1] killed 3 deer, 2 beaver, and 4 buffaloe calves. \u00a0\u00a0 the latter we found very delicious. I think it equal to any veal I ever tasted. the Elk now begin to shed their horns. \u00a0\u00a0 passed one large and two small creeks on the Lard. side, tho' neither of them discharge any water at present. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew so hard this evening that we were obliged to halt several hours. \u00a0\u00a0 we reached the place of incampment after dark, which was on the Lard. side a little above \n White earth river\n [2] which discharges itself on the Stard. side. \u00a0\u00a0 immediately at the mouth of this river it is not more than 10 yards wide being choked up by the mud of the Missouri; tho' after leaving the bottom lands of this river, or even sooner, it becomes a boald stream of sixty yards wide and is deep and navigable. \u00a0\u00a0 the course of this river as far as I could see from the top of Cut bluff, was due North. \u00a0\u00a0 it passes through a beatifull level and fertile vally about five miles in width. I think I saw about 25 miles up this river, and did not discover one tree or bush of any discription on it's borders. \u00a0\u00a0 the vally was covered with Elk and buffaloe. \u00a0\u00a0 saw a great number of gees today as usual, also some swan and ducks.\u2014

[Clark]

\n \n
21st of April Sunday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out early \u00a0\u00a0 the wind gentle & from the N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 the river being verry Crooked, I concluded to walk through the point, the Countrey on either Side is verry Similar to that we have passed, Saw an emence number of Elk & Buffalow, also Deer Antelopes Geese Ducks & a fiew Swan, the Buffalow is about Calveing \u00a0\u00a0 I killed a Buffalow & 4 Deer in my walk to day, the party killed 2 deer 2 beaver & 4 Buffalow Calves, which was verry good veele. I Saw old Camps of Indians on the L. Side, we passed 1 large & 2 Small Creeks on the L. Side \u00a0\u00a0 neither of them discharge any water into the river, in the evening the wind became verry hard a head, we made Camp at a late hour which was on the L. Side a little above the mouth of \n White Earth River which falls in on the Stad Side and is 60 yds. wide, several Mes. up

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 21st April 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a hard white frost last night. \u00a0\u00a0 froze water in the buckets Setting near the fire. \u00a0\u00a0 a Clear and pleasant morning, but verry chilly & cold. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on. Saw the hills and vallies on S. S. covered with buffaloe. Some calfs among them. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party clumb a Stump of a tree\nwhich had a Goose nest in the top of it \u00a0\u00a0 found four Eggs in it. \u00a0\u00a0 passed round the bottom covered with timber which we camped on last night. \u00a0\u00a0 about 10 oC. we halted & took breakfast. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed hills and round knobs on S. S. and a large bottom on N. S. Capt. Clark went on the S. S. to hunt. Came to us at dinner. \u00a0\u00a0 had killed 4 Deer in a bottom covered with Small timber \u00a0\u00a0 he attempted to kill a buffaloe Calf but could not git near Enofe without being discovered by them, the plain being So open. \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oClock clouded up \u00a0\u00a0 cold \u00a0\u00a0 the wind began to blow as usal. \u00a0\u00a0 we dined at a redish bluff on N. S. Saw large gang of buffaloe & calfs Elk also on the opposite Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 delayed about one hour & proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the mouth of a large Creek on the N. S. Called White Earth River. \u00a0\u00a0 it is about 15 yards wide at the mouth & Clear water & Gentle current. Camped on the South Side at a bottom \u00a0\u00a0 came 15 miles to day. Some of the men killed 2 buffaloe Calfs, & one Elk. We Saw this day 4 otter on a drift. Potts shot one of them in head but it Sunk. \u00a0\u00a0 the rest plunged in to the water and swam down the river, and Drewyer killed & Got one of them. \u00a0\u00a0 a Cool evening.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 21st. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on early; and had a fine clear morning, but cold: there was a sharp frost. We saw a great number of elk, buffaloe and deer on both sides of the river. About 12 the wind again rose and was disagreeable, but we continued our voyage. Two of our hunters went out this afternoon and caught three young buffaloe calves. We passed a small river called White Clay river on the North side and having gone 15 miles encamped on the South side.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday April 20th [5] \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we had pleasant Weather, in the night we had a frost, we sett out early, the Wind blowing from the Northwest. \u00a0\u00a0 the Water in the River fell one Inch \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and passed the River called Le Tear Black, [6] lying on the South side of the Mesouri, and Encamped on the North side of the River Mesouri, having went 16 Miles this day.\n\n\n

\n \n \n
\n
1. Including Potts and Drouillard, according to Ordway, each of them killing a buffalo calf. (back)
\n
2. Not to be confused with the present White Earth River, which they passed on April 16 without naming it. The present stream is Little Muddy River or Creek, in Williams County, North Dakota. The camp was in McKenzie County, nearly opposite present Williston. Mattison (GR), 58; Atlas maps 34, 47, 56; MRC map 59. The words \"White earth\" may be a later interpolation as are the same words in the last course of the course and distance table for this day. (back)
\n
3. Also given on Atlas map 35, in both captains' hands. (back)
\n
4. \n Clark has \"N.\" in his journal entry; Atlas map 35 agrees with Lewis. (back)
\n
5. The day is corrected from Saturday, but the date was not changed to April 21. The next entry should be April 22, a Monday as noted. (back)
\n
6. Since this is a misdated entry for April 21, Whitehouse is referring to the party's supposed White Earth River, in French \n La Terre Blanche\n . For the correct identification see the next day's entry. There are a number of course, distance, and campsite errors in these entries. (back)
