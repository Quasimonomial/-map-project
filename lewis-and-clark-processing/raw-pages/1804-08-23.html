\n August 23, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n23rd August \u2329Satturday\u232a Thursday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out this morning verry early, the two men R. Fields & Shannon did not Come up last night, I went out and Killed a fine Buck, J. Fields Killed a Buffalow, 2 Elk Swam by the boat whilst I was out and was not Killed, many guns fired at it \u00a0\u00a0 R. Fields Came up with the horses & brought two Deer, Collins Killed a Small doe, Several Prarie wolves Seen \u00a0\u00a0 Course West 4 Mls. to the mouth of a Small run between two Bluffs of yellow Clay \u00a0\u00a0 North 3\u00bc miles to the upper Pt. of Some timber in the bend to S. S. near where R. fields Killed the Buffalow [1] \u00a0\u00a0 passed the pt. of High Land on S. S at \u00bc of a mile, Capt. Lewis went out with 8 men & brought the buffalow to the river at this bend, C. Lewis Killed a Goose, wind blew hard \u2329I am obliged to make the next Corses Short on ackount\u232a of the flying Sands which rasies like a Cloud of Smoke from the Bars when the wind \u2329rise\u232a Blows, the Sand \u00a0\u00a0 being fine and containing a breat [great] perpotion of earth and when it lights it Sticks to every thing it touches \u00a0\u00a0 at this time the grass is white \u00a0\u00a0 S 48\u00b0 \u00a0\u00a0 3 miles to a point of willows on the S. S. haveing passed the Sand Island L. S \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the L S above the Island [2] \u00a0\u00a0 Saw an elk Standing on a Sand bar. Shields Shot it thro' the neck \u00a0\u00a0 10\u00bc [Ed: the day's mileage accumulation]

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 23rd \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men went out hunting, the wind\nfavourable from the South. G. Drewyer and Jo. Fields went out hunting on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passt. on round a bend \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark walked on Shore a Short time & killed a fine Buck, we halted to Breakfast, 2 Elk Swam the River close by the Boat \u00a0\u00a0 both of them we wounded. \u00a0\u00a0 at the same time Jo. Fields came to the Boat informed us that he had killed a Bull Buffelow, Capt. Lewis & myself & 10 more of the party went out Bucherd & Brought it to the Boat, Collins killed a faun Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 the 2 hunters on the South Side came to the Boat with 2 Deer which they had killed, \u00a0\u00a0 we then proceeded on past a Butiful bottom prarie N. S. which is verry large & wide \u00a0\u00a0 I walked about 1 mile & \u00bd in it when I went for the abo. ment. Buffelow, I Saw the beds & Signs of a great many more Buffelow But this was the first I ever Saw & as great a curiousity to me. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted in a bend opposite a Sand Isd. \u2329in Sd prarie\u232a \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew So hard that it detained us the most of the afternoon, So we pickled down our Buffelow meat, & jerked the venison, the Sand blew So thick from the Sand Island that we could not see across the River for a long time, towards evening the wind abated & we proceeded on to the head of sd. Sand Isd. & camped on S. S. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party wounded an Elk after we landed.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 23rd. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded early this morning with a fair wind. The river here becomes more straight than we had found it for a great distance below. Captain Clarke and one of the men killed a deer and a buffaloe, and some of the men were sent to dress and bring the buffaloe to the boat. [4] We stopped at a prairie on the north side, the largest and handsomest, which I had seen. Captain Clarke called it Buffaloe prairie. The men having returned, we again went on; but the wind changed and we were obliged to halt for the present. While we were detained here we salted two barrels of buffaloe meat. At five in the evening we proceeded some distance and encamped on the south side.\n\n

Clark walked on Shore a Short time and killed a fat Buck. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted to take Breakfast. \u00a0\u00a0 2 Elk Swam across the River close abv. the Boat \u00a0\u00a0 we wounded boath of them \u00a0\u00a0 Jo. Fields came to the Boat had killed a Bull Buffelow. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis & 10 men of the party went out & Brought it to the Boat. \u00a0\u00a0 John Collins killed a faun. \u00a0\u00a0 the 2 men on the S. S. \nwho had been with the horses came to the Boat with 2 Deer\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we halted in a bend to dine \u00a0\u00a0 the wind verry hard so we jurked our meat &c. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind fell towards evening \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on till dark & camped on the S. S.\u2014

\n

\nThursday August 23rd \u00a0\u00a0 We set off very early this morning, having previous to our departure sent 2 Men out a hunting; and Captain Clark walked along the Banks of the River, we proceeded on rowing, and passed round a bend of the River, a short time after passing this bend Captain Clark shot a buck Deer which was very fat, we halted with the boat & took Captain Clark on board, likewise the Buck; we proceeded on a small distance, and landed to take breakfast, whilst we were breakfasting 2 Elk swam across the River close above the Boat, \u2329one\u232a Some of our hands shot at them, & wounded them both, & we got them, One of hands that we had sent out this morning returned, he having shot a Buffalo Bull.

\n

\n Captain Clark and Ten Men went out in order to bring the Buffalo to the boat, which they did, One of our Men killed a fawn, which he brought likewise to us, The Men who were on the South side of the River with the horses came to us, having 2 deer which they had shot, We proceeded on and halted in a bend of the River to dine, The wind blowing hard we Jerked our Meat & overhauled several articles on board the boat. \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening the wind died away, We Proceeded on 'till dark, when we encamped on the South side of the River,\u2014\n\t\t\n\t\t\t\n\t\t\n\n\n

\n
1. The Field Notes state \"J. Field\" at one point and \"R. Field\" at another, whereas the Codex B entry indicates that Joseph, not Reubin, Field killed the buffalo; Whitehouse confirms that it was Joseph. This was the first buffalo, or bison, killed by a member of the party. Burroughs, 147. (back)
\n
2. In either Dixon County, Nebraska, or Clay County, South Dakota. The Missouri River has shifted considerably in this vicinity since 1804, and the mouth of the Vermillion River (Clark's \"White Stone River,\" August 24, 1804), above the camp in 1804, now reaches the Missouri below the campsite. The site would be a mile or so to the southeast of present Vermillion, South Dakota. Atlas map 17; Nicollet (MMR), 398\u201399; Warren map 10; MRC map 28; MRR map 80. (back)
\n
3. In either Dixon County, Nebraska, or Clay County, South Dakota, a mile or so southeast of Vermillion, Clay County. The shifting of the Missouri River over time makes the campsite difficult to determine. (back)
\n
4. \n Lewis led the party and Ordway was one of them. The first buffalo, Bison bison, taken by the expedition; see Clark's entry for the confusion about which one of the Field brothers killed it. (back)
