\n
\n \n

[Clark]

S. opposit the Commencement of the 1st Prarie, Called Prarie of the Arrows, [1] the river at this place about 300 yds. Wide \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Creek, Arrow Creek 8 yds. wide L. S. opposit Black bird C Small\n [2] \u00a0\u00a0 passed the head of the Isd. & a small Willow one to the L. S. (\u2609s merdn. altd. back obsvn. 37 00' 00[)] \u00a0\u00a0 N. 39\u00b0 W 2 Ms. to a pt. \u2329on S. S.\u232a of High Land on the L. Side opst. a pt. on St. S. River about 350 yds. wide at this pt. \u00a0\u00a0 a Wind from the S at 4 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 (Handson Sutn[)] [situation?] \u00a0\u00a0 on the High pt. a prarie & Small Lake below \u00a0\u00a0 N 32\u00b0 E 3\u00bd Ms. to a pt. on L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed an Isld. in the mid R\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 in passing up on the S. S. opsd. the Isd. the Sturn of the boat Struck a log which was not proceiveable \u00a0\u00a0 the Curt. Struck her bow and turn the boat against Some drift & Snags which [were] below with great force; This was a disagreeable and Dangerous Situation, particularly as immense large trees were Drifting down and we lay imediately in their Course,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Some of our men being prepared for all Situations leaped into the water Swam ashore with a roap, and fixed themselves in Such Situations, that the boat was off in a fiew minits, I can Say with Confidence that our party is not inferior to any that was ever on the waters of the Missoppie \u00a0\u00a0 we Crossed to the Island and Camped, [3] our hunters lay on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329\n Gibson verry nearly\u232a \u00a0\u00a0 the wind from the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 the river continue to rise Slowly \u00a0\u00a0 Current excessive rapid\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Countrey on the S. S. high bottom & Delghtfull land \u00a0\u00a0 that on the L. S. is up land or hills of from 50 to 100 foot higher than the bottom & a thinly wooded, Countrey, Lands tolerably Good; Comminced raining at 5 oClock and continued by intervales the greater part of the night. We discovered that one of our French hands had a Conpt. [complaint]\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We Commsd Doctering, I hope the Success in this case, usial to an [sentence incomplete]\n

\n 9th of June 1804 Satturday\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na fair morning, the River rise a little \u00a0\u00a0 we got fast on a Snag Soon after we Set out which detained us a Short time \u00a0\u00a0 passed the upper Point of the Island \u00a0\u00a0 Several Small Chanels running out of the River below a \u2329Bluff\u232a [NB: cliff of rocks called the arrow rock\n ] [4] & Prarie (Called the Prariee of Arrows) where the river is confined within the width of \u2329300\u232a [NB: 200] yds. \u00a0\u00a0 Passed a Creek of 8 yds. wide Called Creek of Arrows, this Creek is Short and heads in the Praries on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Creek Called Blackbird Creek S. S. and One Islands below & a Prarie above on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 a Small Lake above the Prarie\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 opposit the Lower point of the 2d. Island on the S. S. we had like to have Stove our boat, in going round a Snag her Stern Struck a log under Water & She Swung round on the Snag, with her broad Side to the Current expd. to the Drifting timber, by the active exertions of our party we got her off in a fiew Mints. without engerey and Crossed to the Island where we Campd. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters lay on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 the Perogue Crossed without Seeing them & the banks too uncertain to Send her over\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Some wind from the S accompanied with rain this evening\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Lands on the S. S. is a high rich bottom \u00a0\u00a0 the L. S. appears oven [open?] and of a good quallity runing gradually to from fifty to 100 foot.

\nSaturday June 9th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly \u00a0\u00a0 we got fast on a log \u00a0\u00a0 Detained us half an hour, Rain last night \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329oppisite\u232a we passed prarie & arrow Creek on South Side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 the land is high & verry good \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening we passed a prarie & a Small Lake below. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on an Island at left Side.\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 9th. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed the Prairie of Arrows, and Arrow creek on the \nsouth side. [6] This is a beautiful country, and the land excellent. The Missouri \nhere is only 300 yards wide, and the current very strong. Three miles farther, we passed Blackbird creek on the north side, and encamped. This day going round some drift wood, the stern of the boat became fast, when she immediately swung round, and was in great danger; but we got her off without much injury.\n\n\n\n

\nSatery 9 \u00a0\u00a0 Got on our way at the usal hour \u00a0\u00a0 roed. 7 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Stopd to take \ndinner at the End of a large Island above the arrow prarie \u00a0\u00a0 the distance from the latter to the River Charrotte River\n [7] is 14 miles \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters did Not Come \u2329in\u232a as the Storm was Great [illegible, crossed out] \u00a0\u00a0 the peirouge Could not Cross for them \u00a0\u00a0 Roed 15 Miles\u2014

\n

\nSaturday June 9th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we got under way at the usual hour, and rowed 7 Miles, when we took up in order to dine; which we did at the point of a large Island, [8] above a place called the Arrow Priari, This Island lies distant from Charotto River 14 Miles.

\n

\nThe day proving stormy, we were obliged to waite for our hunters who were on the opposite side of the River, and it being unsafe to venture across in the Pettiauger for them. \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped on this Island, the distance come this day being 15 Miles\n\n\n\n

\n
1. In the vicinity of the present town of Arrow Rock and Arrow Rock State Park in Saline County, Missouri. Arrow Creek is probably Pierre Fresne Creek, in Saline County. MRC map 9; MRM map 24. (back)
\n
2. Evidently later Richland Creek, in Howard County, Missouri. Coues (HLC), 1:18; MRM map 24. (back)
\n
3. Somewhat above Richland Creek and probably below the later village of Bluff Port, Howard County. MRC map 9; MRM map 25. (back)
\n
4. The name of the bluff, called Pierre \u00e0 Fl\u00e8che as early as 1723, supposedly derives from an Indian tradition. The present town of Arrow Rock and Arrow Rock State Park, both in Saline County, commemorate the name. After Fort Osage (see below, June 23, 1804) was abandoned in 1813, the post was moved to Arrow Rock. Coues (HLC), 1:18 n. 40; Missouri Guide, 357; MRC No. 9. Biddle apparently crossed out \"Bluff\" when he substituted \"cliff of rocks.\" (back)
\n
5. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
6. The prairie is in the vicinity of the town of Arrow Rock, Saline County, Missouri. \"Arrow\nCreek\" is probably Pierre Fresne Creek, Saline County. McKeehan's note: \"Prairies are natural meadows, or pastures, without trees and covered with grass.\" (back)
\n
7. \n Chariton River, Chariton County, Missouri. (back)
\n
8. There may be some confusion here with the campsite of the previous day. The camp today was on an island somewhat above Richland Creek and probably below later Bluff Port, Howard County, Missouri. (back)
