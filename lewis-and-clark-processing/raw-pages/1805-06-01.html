\n June 1, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Saturday June 1st 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe moring was cloudy and a few drops of rain. Set out at an early hour and proceeded as usual by the help of our chords. \u00a0\u00a0 the river Clifts and bluffs not so high as yesterday and the country becomes more level. \u00a0\u00a0 a mountain or a part of the N. Mountain appears to approach the river within 8 or 10 ms. bearing N. from our encampment of the last evening. [1] Capt C. who walked on shore today informed me that the river hills were much lower than usual and that from the tops of those hills he had a delightfull view of rich level and extensive plains on both sides of the river; in those plains, which in many places reach the river clifts, he observed large banks of pure sand which appeared to have been d[r]iven by the S W. winds from the river bluffs and there deposited. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 the plains are more fertile at some distance from the river than near the bluffs where the surface of the earth is very generally covered with small smothe pebbles which have the appearance of having been woarn by the agitation of the waters in which they were no doubt once immerced. [3] A range of high Mountains appear to the S. W. at a considerable distance covered with snow, they appear to run Westerly. [4] no timber appears on the highlands; but much more than yesterday on the river and Islands. \u00a0\u00a0 rockey points and shoals less freequent than yesterday but some of them quite as bad when they did occur. \u00a0\u00a0 the river from 2 to 400 yards wide, courant more gentle and still becoming clearer. \u00a0\u00a0 game is by no means as abundant as\nbelow; we killed one male bighorn and a mule deer today; saw buffalow at a distance in the plains particularly near a small Lake on Lard. side about 8 ms. distant. \u00a0\u00a0 some few drops of rain again fell this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed six Islands and encamped on the 7th; [5] they are all small but contain some timber. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind has been against us all day.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 I saw the choke cherry the yellow and red courant bushes; the wild rose appears now to be in full bloom as are also the prickley pear which are numerous in these plains.\u2014 [6] \u00a0\u00a0 We also saw some Indian Lodges of sticks today which did not appear to have been long evacuated.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 some coal appear in the bluffs.

[Clark]

\n \n
June 1st Satterday 1805
\u00a0
\n \n

\na Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at an early hour and proseeded on as usial with the toe rope \u00a0\u00a0 The Countrey appears to be lower and the Clifts not So high or Common, a mountain or a part of the north Mountain about 8 or 10 miles N. of this place, I walked on Shore to day found the \u2329high\u232a Plains much lower than we have Seen them and on the top we behold an extencive plain on both Sides, in this plain I observed maney [X: k]noles of fine Sand which appeared to have blown from the river bluffs and collected at these points [9] \u00a0\u00a0 Those plains are fertile \u00a0\u00a0 near the river a great no. of Small Stone, I observed at Some distance to the S. W. a high mountain which appears to bear westerly \u00a0\u00a0 The Cole appear as usial, more Cotton trees Scattered on the Shores & Islands than yesterday\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 no timber on the high land, the river from 2 to 400 yards wide & current more jentle than yesterday but fiew bad rapid points to day\u2014 the wild animals not So plenty as below \u00a0\u00a0 we only killed a ram & mule Deer to day, we Saw Buffalow at a distance in the plains, particularly near a Lake on the Lard. Side about 8 miles distant from the river\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We passed Six Islands and encamped on the 7th \u00a0\u00a0 all those Islands are Small but contain Some timber on them \u00a0\u00a0 The river riseing a little \u00a0\u00a0 Wind to day from the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 Som fiew drops of rain in the morning and also in the evening, flying Clouds all day

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n June 1st Saturday 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a Cloudy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at an eairly hour and proceeded on as usal with the toe rope. \u00a0\u00a0 the Country appears to be lower and the clifts not So high or common. \u00a0\u00a0 a Mountain or a part of the north Mountain about 8 or 10 miles N. of this place. more cotton trees Scatering along the river & Islands than yesterday. no timber on the high land. The river from 2 to 400 yards wide & current more jentle than yesterday. \u00a0\u00a0 but fiew bad rapids points to day. \u00a0\u00a0 the wild animels not So pleanty as below \u00a0\u00a0 we only killed a ram & mule deer to day. we Saw buffalow at a [d]istance in the plains. \u00a0\u00a0 perticelarly near a lake on the Lard. [s]ide about 8 eight miles off from the river. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed Six Islands and Camped on the 7th all night. all those Islands are Small but contain Some timber on them. The river riseing a little. wind to day from S. W. Some fiew drops of rain in the morning and also in the evening. flying clouds all day. Saw Several Indians Camps made of Sticks & Set up on end and do not appear to be long evacuated. The roses are in full bloom \u00a0\u00a0 we saw yallow berrys, red berry bushes great nombers wild or choke Cherries, prickly prairs are in the blossom \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw great nombers of them. Came 23 miles to day. \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 1st June, 1805. We embarked early. The morning was cloudy, but without rain. We passed through a more handsome country, than for some days past. It appears more level and there are some good bottoms on both sides of the river, but not large; also a number of beautiful small islands covered with cotton wood. We saw a number of mountain sheep. Yesterday our men killed three of them, that had remarkable large horns; one pair weighed 25 pounds. We passed a small river on the North side about 11 o'clock. [10] The water is not so rapid to day as usual, but continues high. In the afternoon we passed a creek [11] about 30 yards wide, and several small islands. We went 24 miles and encamped on a small island.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 1st June 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly & proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Steep clifts of white rocks which had villages of little birds, [12] built along the projecting rocks. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party killed a Mountain ram or Ibex which had fine horns. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed handsom bottoms of c. wood & box elder [13] timber on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw old Indian Camps at a bottom on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek [14] on N. S. & Straight bluffs. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Small Islands. \u00a0\u00a0 about one oC. P. M. we passed a beautiful large Island covered with large & Small timber Saw Some Elk on it. \u00a0\u00a0 the hills & bluffs are not So high on the river as they have been for Some time. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 oC. we halted to dine and air our goods &c. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind rose from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 the river bears to the South, handsom baron plains back from the river. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw Mountains a Short distance from the river on the N.S. and on the S. S. at a considerable distance up the river. \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oC. we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Islands covered with timber. \u00a0\u00a0 passd. a Straight clift of rocks Steep from the Surface of the water about 100 feet perpinticular. \u00a0\u00a0 passd handsom high plains on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 Came 24 miles & Camped on the 7th Isld. a Small Island covered with timber. \u00a0\u00a0 had passd. 2 little below

\n

\nSaturday June 1st \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we had Clear & pleasant Weather; we set off early, and proceeded on our Voyage, towing the Crafts as usual, and passed Steep Clifts of white Rock, which had a number of nests of some small birds built, along the projecting Rocks, as we pass'd.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our party, \u2329killed\u232a who was out hunting, killed a Mountain Sheep or Ibex, which had remarkable fine Horns.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on and passed \u2329a\u232a some handsome bottoms of land, having Cotton Wood & Box Elder Timber on them, lying on both sides of the River; and saw some old Indian Camps, in a bottom, on the South side of the River, We also passed, a Creek lying on the North side, and Bluffs and several Small Islands.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 About One o'Clock P. M. we passed a beautiful large Island, cover'd with large and small timber, and saw some Elk on it.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Hills and bluffs, are not so high along the River, as they have been for several days past.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 About 2 oClock P. M. we halted to dine, and to Air the Goods &ca. which were on board the Crafts, The wind rose here, and blew from the South East, the Course of the Mesouri running to the Southward, here we \u2329was\u232a saw Some Elk on the hills and Clifts.\u2014

\n

\nAnd there lies some handsome barren plains, which lay a small distance back from the River, We saw likewise here Mountains, which lay a short distance from the River, on the North side, and some Mountains, lying on the South side of the River, at a considerable distance up it. \u00a0\u00a0 About 3 oClock P. M. we proceeded on our Voyage, and passed a Creek lying on the North side of the River; and some Islands, cover'd with Timber and a Strait Clift of\nRocks, lying very steep from the Surface of the Water, they appeared to be 100 feet perpendicular, and some handsome plains, lying a small distance back, from the River on both sides of it.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We also passed 2 Islands which lay on each side of the River, We encamp'd in the Evening on the end of an Island, which was cover'd with timber, having come 24 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n\n\n
