\n June 12, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
Grand River \u00a0\u00a0 June 12th 1804 [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSir,
\nI have purchased from Mr Louiselle's Pattroon three hundred pounds of voyager's grease for which I am to pay by my A [account?]\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n 12th of June, Tuesday \u00a0\u00a0 We Set out early, passed thro: a verry bad bend N. 25\u00b0 W. 3\u00bd to a pt. L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 N. 70\u00b0 W. 2\u00bd ms to a pt. on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Sand bar\u2014N 60\u00b0 W 3 1/ ms. to a pt. on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed \n Plumb. C\n \n [2] at \u00bd a me. on L. S. and halted to Dine, and 2 Caussease [cajeux] Came Down from the Soux nation, we found in the party an old man who had been with the Soux 20 years & had great influence [hole] with them, we provld. [prevailed] on this old man Mr. [hole] Duriaur\n \n [3] to return with us, with a view to get Some of the Soux Chiefs to go to the U. S. \u00a0\u00a0 purchased 300 lb of Voyagers Grece @ 5$ [pr?] Hd. [4] \u00a0\u00a0 made Some exchanges & purchuses of Mockersons & found it Late & concluded to incamp. [5]\n

\n

\nThose people inform that no Indians are on the river, The Countrey on each Side of the river is good\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
12th of June, Tuesday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nSet out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed Some bad Placies, and a Small Creek on the L. S. Called \n plumb Creek\n at abt. 1 me. \u00a0\u00a0 at 1 oClock we brought too two Chaussies one Loaded with furs & Pelteries, the other with Greece buffalow grease & tallow \u00a0\u00a0 We purchased 300 lb. of Greese, and finding that old Mr. Durioun was of the party we questioned him untill it was too late to Go further and Concluded to Camp for the night, those people inform nothing of much information

\n

\nColcluded to take old Durion [X: (who went accg)] back as fur as the Soux nation with a view to get some of their Chiefs to Visit the Presdt. of the United S. \u00a0\u00a0 (This man being a verry Confidential friend of those people, he having resided with the nation 20 odd years) and to accompany them on [6]\n

\n
\n
\n Course & Distance June 12th\n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
N. 25\u00b0 W.3 \u00bdms. to L. S. passed Plumb C\n
N 70 W.2 \u00bdms. to pt. on S. S.
N. 60\u00b0 W.\n 3\n ms. to pt. on S. S.
\u00a09\u00a0
\n
\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday June 12th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairley this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair \u2329day\u232a morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed plumb Creek on South Side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 the plumbs [7] are plenty up sd. Creek. \u00a0\u00a0 near below Sd. Creek the land is high well Timbered & well Situated for a plantation. The Timber Soil &C. is verry excelent, we Camped on N. Side of the River. Several Frenchmen Stayed with us all night comming down the River in Several pearogues Loaded with peltry Bound to St. Louis. \u00a0\u00a0 came from the Shew nation. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 they passed the last winter their & had an amence Side of Bufloe Green Skins &C \u00a0\u00a0 we Got an old Frenchman [9] to go with us which could Speak Sevral languages, among the indians for a long distance.\u2014\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday June 12 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at the Usial ouer \u00a0\u00a0 the day Clear wind from the west \u00a0\u00a0 Came 4 miles \u00a0\u00a0 past a Creek on the S. Side Called \n Plumb Creek about 20 yads wide \u00a0\u00a0 the timber in this Bottoms is Cotten wood \u00a0\u00a0 2 miles when we met \u2329two\u232a 5 Cannoes from the \u2329Shoue\n Sue\u232a Soux nations Loaded with peltry and Greece \u00a0\u00a0 thay have been 13 mounthes up the missorea River \u00a0\u00a0 Delayed \u00bd day with the French, Bought Some tallow of them \u00a0\u00a0 ouer hunters Did not Rettern Last night \u00a0\u00a0 one French man hiard to go with us up the missorea who can Speak the Difernt [languages?] \u00a0\u00a0 encamped on the N. Side \u00a0\u00a0 the Land Good Bottom\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 12th.\n [10] We set out early, and proceeded until five o'clock in the afternoon, when we met five periogues loaded with fur and peltry from the Sioux nation of Indians. We remained with the people to whom these periogues belonged all night [11] and got from them an old Frenchman, [12] who could speak the language of the different nations of Indians up the Missouri, and who agreed to go with us as an interpreter.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTusday 12 \u00a0\u00a0 Left the Charrottoe Perarie and Saild. for the Grand River. \u00a0\u00a0 Met with 7 peirogues. \u00a0\u00a0 Loaded with peltry for Captn Chatto\n [13] in St Louis \u00a0\u00a0 Our men of Each Craft Exchangd. Blankets for Buffalow Robes & Mockisons \u00a0\u00a0 Sent One of Our Men Belonging to the white pierouge back that Belongd. to Captn Stodders Company of Artilery [14] \u00a0\u00a0 Incampd within \u2329thr\u232a two miles of the three point Island [15] \u00a0\u00a0 Roed 7 Miles that day\u2014

\n

\nTuesday June 12th \u00a0\u00a0 This Morning early we set off from the Charotto Priari, with a fair wind and fine weather, all hands being well and in high spirits; we found the current of the River still running strong, but are in hopes of soon getting to the Grand River. \u00a0\u00a0 At 10 oClock A. M. met 7 Pettiaugers, loaded with Peltry, belonging to Captain Choteau of Saint Louis. \u00a0\u00a0 our Men exchanged with those belonging to the Pettiaugers, some blankets for Buffalo Robes, & Mockasins; we put on board the Pettiaugers, one Man that we had with us, belonging to Captain Stoddards company of Artillery, who is going to Saint Louis, in the Evening we encamped two Miles from an Island called the three point Island; the distance we came this day, being 7 Miles\n\n \n

1. This passage in Lewis's hand, under the entries for June 25 and 26, 1804, is in the Field Notes (document 23). (back)
\n
2. Possibly later Bear Creek, Saline County, Missouri. MRM map 26. (back)
\n
3. \n Pierre Dorion, Sr., was born before 1750, probably in Quebec, and was in Cahokia, Illinois, in 1780. Clark perhaps had heard of Dorion before, for the trader had at least corresponded with George Rogers Clark in 1780. Within a few years of that date he had gone up the Missouri to the Yankton\n Sioux, where he married and settled down as a trader. He was employed by R\u00e9gis Loisel as an interpreter at Loisel's post at Ile aux Cedres. He took a delegation of Yankton chiefs to St. Louis for the captains and was later involved in various negotiations with Indians, serving for a time as a government subagent under Clark. He died sometime after 1811. Munnick (PD); Speck, 150\u201386; Irving (Astor), 117\u201320; Osgood (FN), 55 n. 1. (back)
\n
4. In the Codex A entry of this date Clark or someone else has interlined \"buffalow grease & tallow\" over \"Greece.\" Perhaps this was a base material for making pemmican. Nute, 54. (back)
\n
5. \n Clark is not entirely clear which side of the river the camp was on, but Ordway says they camped on the north side. This would place them in south-central Chariton County, Missouri, in the vicinity of where Missouri Highway J ends. MRC map 10. (back)
\n
6. On this day, according to Whitehouse, a man belonging to Captain Amos Stoddard's artillery company was sent back down the river, presumably with Dorion's boat. No other journalist mentions the event. This man may have been John Robertson, or Robinson, who was with the party at River Dubois but appears in no record or roll of the expedition proper. See Appendix A. (back)
\n
7. Any number of possibilities of Prunus sp.; see Clark's entry for June 10. (back)
\n
8. Meaning the Sioux Indians, probably the Yankton Sioux . (back)
\n
9. \n Pierre Dorion, Sr.\n (back)
\n
10. \n Gass has no entry for June 11; the party remained in their camp of June 10. (back)
\n
11. In south-central Chariton County, Missouri. (back)
\n
12. \n Pierre Dorion, Sr.\n (back)
\n
13. \n Ren\u00e9 Auguste Chouteau or Jean Pierre Chouteau; no one else mentions that these men \nwere Chouteau employees. (back)
\n
14. No other journal keeper mentions this event. The man was a member of \nCaptain Stoddard's artillery company, from which several men were assigned to the expedition. He may have been John Robertson, or Robinson. See Clark's entries for this day, and Appendix A. (back)
\n
15. Not mentioned by any other journal keeper. A nameless triangular island appears on \nlater maps in the right location on the Saline County, Missouri, side of the river. They camped in south-central Chariton County, Missouri. (back)
