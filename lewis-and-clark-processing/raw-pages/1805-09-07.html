\n September 7, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n September 7th Saturday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nA Cloudy & rainie Day \u00a0\u00a0 the greater Part of the Day dark & Drisley we proceedd on down the river thro a Vallie \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Small Runs on the \u2329right left\u232a [NB: right] & 3 creeks on the \u2329right\u232a left \u00a0\u00a0 The Vallie from 1 to \u2329three\u232a 2 miles wide the Snow top mountains to our left, open hilley Countrey on the right. Saw 2 horses left by the Indians \u00a0\u00a0 Those horses were as wild a Elk. One of our hunters Came up this morning without his horse, in the course of the night the horse broke loose & Cleared out\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we did not make Camp untill dark, [1] for the want of a good place, one of our hunters did not join us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 he haveing killed an elk packed his horses & could not overtake us\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday 7th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a cloudy cold morning. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our hunters Stayed out all last night. \u00a0\u00a0 we set out eairly and proceeded on down the creek. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunter who Stayed out last night over took us \u00a0\u00a0 had lost his horse. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed over narrow plains larg pine and cotton timber along the creeks \u00a0\u00a0 high mountains a little to the Lard. Side which is covred thick with Snow. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our hunters killed two Deer. Some of the other hunters killed 1 goose 1 crain Several pheasants and a hawk. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several Small creeks and runs \u00a0\u00a0 we had Several Showers of rain. \u00a0\u00a0 this valley gitting wider. \u00a0\u00a0 the plain Smooth & dry. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 18 miles this day and Camped [2] on the bank of the Creek.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 7th. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early in a cloudy cool morning; and our hunters went on as usual. We proceeded down the creek, [3] and in our way we were met by a hunter, who had not come in last night, and who had lost his horse. We halted at 12 o'clock, and one of the hunters killed 2 deer; which was a subject of much joy and congratulation. Here we remained to dine, and some rain fell. On the south of this place there are very high mountains covered with snow and timber, and on the north prairie hills. After staying here 2 hours we proceeded on down the creek; found the country much the same as that which we had passed through in the forenoon; and having travelled about 20 miles since the morning, encamped [4] for the night. The valley is become more extensive, and our creek has increased to a considerable river. Some rain fell in the afternoon, and our hunters killed two cranes [5] on our way.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 7th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a cloudy cold morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly, and \nproceeded on down the creek. [6] \u00a0\u00a0 our hunter came up who Stayed out last night. \u00a0\u00a0 had lost his horse. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on over a plain. \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms narrow and considerable of timber large pine and cotton along the creek \u00a0\u00a0 high mountains [7] on the Side of the creek which are covred with pitch pine. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the highest are covred thick with Snow. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our hunters killed 2 Deer, which revived us. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the hunters killed a goose & a crain Several fessents and a hawk. \u00a0\u00a0 Several Small Showers of rain in the course of the day. \u00a0\u00a0 the valley gitting wider the creek larger. \u00a0\u00a0 the plain Smooth and dry. \u00a0\u00a0 the Soil verry indifferent. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came 18 miles this day and Camped on the bank of the Creek. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed Several creeks and branches [8] which run into the main creek this day. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our hunters did not join us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 a little rain. \u00a0\u00a0 our course this day is generally N. west.

\n

\nSaturday September 7th \u00a0\u00a0 We had a cold, Cloudy morning.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early and proceeded on, we went down the Creek that we had encamped on. \u00a0\u00a0 The hunter that was out last night returned to us, he had lost his horse, & had killed no game. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and crossed a plain. \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms along the Creek are narrow, and had a considerable quantity of Pine & Cotton Wood trees growing on it. \u00a0\u00a0 On the South side of this Creek, lies high Mountains, which are covered with Pitch Pine. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters came to us, and had killed 2 deer, Some others of the Hunters also came to us, & had killed 1 Goose 1 Crane several ferrets [9] & a hawk all of which they brought with them.\u2014

\n

\nOur party seemed revived at the success that the hunters had met with, however in all the hardship that they had yet undergone they never once complained, trusting to Providence & the Conduct of our Officers in all our difficulties. \u00a0\u00a0 We had several small showers of Rain in the course of the day. \u00a0\u00a0 As we pursued our Journey we found the Valleys getting wider & the Creek larger & the plain smooth & dry, but the Soil very indifferent. \u00a0\u00a0 We came 18 Miles this day, and encamped on the bank of the same Creek, that we had encamped on Yesterday. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed during this days Journey several Creeks, & branches which run into the creek where we now are.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters did not join us this evening, our course during this day has been generally North West.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. \n Clark apparently mislabeled or drew in incorrectly some streams on Atlas map 68 above the camp of September 6. The stream labeled \"West Fork of Clarks River\" (today's West Fork Bitterroot River) is too far north according to his course and distance table (here found with the entry of September 9), which is more instructive for the day's route than the text. The true West Fork would be the first major stream on the east side beyond the camp of September 6, between the words \"Hills\" and \"Pine\" on the map (\"Creek on the left\" in the second course for the day and mislabeled \"Nez Perce\" by Coues). The creek in the next course would be either McCoy Creek or Tin Cup Creek, the latter according to Coues's interlineation. Coues seems to be correct in identifying the creek in the next course as Rock Creek; it is the one Clark drew in as West Fork of Clarks River. Just above that (unnamed on the map) is present Lost Horse Creek, called Little Horse Creek by Coues. The party camped southwest of Grantsdale, Ravalli County, Montana, on the east side of the Bitterroot River. (back)
\n
2. On the Bitterroot River, southwest of Grantsdale, Ravalli County, Montana. (back)
\n
3. The party went down the East Fork Bitterroot River to the junction with West Fork Bitterroot River (West Fork Clark's River to the party) and on down the Bitterroot (Clark's) River, Ravalli County, Montana. (back)
\n
4. Southwest of Grantsdale, Ravalli County, on the east side of the Bitterroot. (back)
\n
5. Probably a sandhill crane, Grus canadensis.\n (back)
\n
6. The party went down the East Fork Bitterroot River to the junction with West Fork \nBitterroot River (West Fork Clark's River to the party) and on down the Bitterroot (Clark's) River, Ravalli County, Montana. (back)
\n
7. The Bitterroot Mountains on the Montana-Idaho border. (back)
\n
8. Including McCoy, Tin Cup, Rock, and Lost Horse creeks, in Ravalli County. See Clark's entry for the day. (back)
\n
9. The copyist's error. He meant \"fessents\" as in the first entry, for pheasants, otherwise \ngrouse. (back)
