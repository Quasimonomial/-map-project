\n May 19, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Sunday May 19th 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe last night was disagreeably could; we were unable to set out untill 8 oclock A. M. in consequence of a heavy fogg, which obscured the river in such a manner that we could not see our way; this is the first we have experienced in any thing like so great a degree; there was also a fall of due last evening, which is the second we have experienced since we have entered this extensive open country. \u00a0\u00a0 at eight we set out and proceeded as yesterday by means of the cord principally, the hills are high and the country similar to that of yesterday. Capt Clark walked on shore with two of the hunters and killed a brown bear; notwithstanding that it was shot through the heart it ran as it's usual pace near a quarter of a mile before it fell. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party wounded a beaver, and my dog as usual swam in to catch it; the beaver bit him through the hind leg and cut the artery; it was with great difficulty that I could stop the blood; I fear it will yet prove fatal to him. \u00a0\u00a0 on Capt. Clark's return he informed me that he had from the top of one of the adjacent hights [1] discovered the entrance of a large stream which discharged itself into the Missouri on the Lard. side distant 6 or seven miles; from the same place he also saw a range of Mountains, bearing W. distant 40 or 50 miles; they appeared to proceed in a S. S. W. direction; the N. N. E. extremity of these mountains appeared abrupt. [2]\n

\n

\nThis afternoon the river was croked, rappid and containing more sawyers than we have seen in the same space since we left the entrance of the river Platte. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. C. in the course of his walk killed three deer and a beaver, I also walked on shore this evening a few miles and killed an Elk, a buck, and a beaver. \u00a0\u00a0 the party killed and caught 4 other beaver & 3 deer.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 19th Sunday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na verry cold night, the murckery Stood at 38 at 8 oClock this morning, a heavy dew which is the 2d I have Seen this Spring. The fog (which was the first) was So thick this morning that we could not Set out untill the Sun was about 2 hours up, at which time a Small breeze Sprung up from the E. which Cleared off the fog & we proceeded on by means of the Cord \u00a0\u00a0 The hills are high & rugged the Countrey as yesterday\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 I walked on Shore with two men \u00a0\u00a0 we killed a \u2329brown\u232a white or grey bear; not withstanding that it was Shot through the heart it ran at it's usial pace near a quarter of a mile before it fell. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis's dog was badly bitten by a wounded beaver and was near bleading to death\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 after killing the Bear I continued my walk alone, & killed 3 Deer & a Beaver; finding that the Perogues were below I assended the highest hill I could See, from the top of which I Saw the mouth of \n M. Shell R\n \n [5] & the meanderings of the Missouri for a long distance. I also Saw a high mountain in a westerley direction, bearing S. S W. about 40 or 50 miles distant, in the evening the river was verry Crooked and much more rapid & Containing more Sawyers than any which we have passed above the River Platte \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis walked on Shore this after noon & killed an Elk, Buck & a Beaver, I kiled three Deer at dinner, the hunters killed three other Deer to day \u00a0\u00a0 Several beaver also killed. We Camped on the Stard. Side in a bottom of Small Cotton wood [6]\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 19th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a heavy diew fell last night. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the party caught a beaver. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off about 7 oC \u00a0\u00a0 Clear and pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 about 10 oC. A. M. we killed a young brown bear, on the S. Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 passed pitch pine hills on each Side of the River and timbred bottoms. Semon Capt Lewiss dog got bit by a beaver. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters on Shore killed a Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 about one oC. we halted to dine on N. S. at a bottom of c. wood timber. Capt Clark killed three deer. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a willow Island near the N. Shore \u00a0\u00a0 passed high pitch pine & ceeder hills as usal. \u00a0\u00a0 passed bottoms on each Side covered with c. w. timber. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came about 18 miles this day & Camped [7] on a timbred bottom on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 [illegible words] place Capt Lewis killed an Elk. Some other of the hunters killed 3 deer & 3 beaver to day.\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 19th. \u00a0\u00a0 The morning was foggy and there was some dew. The river is handsome and the country mountainous. We made 20\u00bc miles and encamped on the North side in a small bottom.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 19th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a heavy diew fell last night. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off as usal and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 passed pitch pine hills on each Side of the river. \u00a0\u00a0 about 10 oClock we killed a Small female brown bear on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we took on board the meat & Skin and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 about 1 oC. we halted to dine at a bottom on the N. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark killed 3 Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a handsom willow Island near the N. S. of River. \u00a0\u00a0 passed pitch pine & ceeder hills as usal, & bottoms of timber on each Side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came about 18 Miles and Camped at a bottom on the N. Side, where Capt. Lewis killed an Elk & Some of the men killed 3 Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the hunters killed 3 beaver to day.\u2014

\n

\nSunday May 19th \u00a0\u00a0 A heavy dew fell last night, and this morning was clear and pleasant, we set off early, as usual; and proceeded on, and passed on each side of the River, hills cover'd with Pitch pine, about 10 o'Clock A. M. some of our party killed a small female brown bear on the South side of the River, we stopped for a short time; and took on board the Meat, and Skin of \nthis bear, we then proceeded on till about One o'Clock, when we halted to dine in a bottom laying on the North side of the River, Captain Clark who had been on shore hunting, join'd us here; having killed 3 Deer, which was brought to us, by a party sent after them,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 At 2 o'Clock P. M we proceeded on our Voyage, and passed a handsome Island with Willows growing on it, lying on the North side of the River, & hills lying on both sides of the River; \nthe growth on which was Pitch pine and Cedar, and fine bottoms of timber, we proceeded on till Evening, and encamped at a bottom lying on the North side of the River, Captain Lewis who had been out hunting with a party of our Men since we dined returned to us here having killed One Elk 3 deer and 3 Beaver.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came 18 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n\n\n
