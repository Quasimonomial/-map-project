\n June 6, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
Wednesday the 6th of June 1804 [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMended our Mast this morning and Set out at 7 oClock, under a Jentle Braise from the S, E by S \u00a0\u00a0 N 28\u00b0 W 3\u00bd miles to a hill on St Sd. pass:g the N: belge of the Island Called Split rock Island, the river rose last night a foot \u00a0\u00a0 the Countrey about this Isd. is delightfull \u00a0\u00a0 large rush bottom of rushes below on the St. Side \u00a0\u00a0 N 49\u00b0 W, 1\u00bd Ms. to the mouth of Split rock \u2329Creek\u232a River\n [2] [blank] yds. wide on the Starboard Side opod. the pt. of a Isd: passed a place in the projecting rock Called the hole \u00a0\u00a0 thro' the rock, a \u2329Small\u232a round Cave pass thro the Pt. of rock's \u00a0\u00a0 West 1\u00bd ms. to a pt. on Std. Sd. opposit a Clift of rocks abt 200 foot \u00a0\u00a0 N 31\u00b0 W. 4 ms \u00bd to a pt. on L: Side \u00a0\u00a0 passed \n Saline Creek\n [3] on the L. Side \u00a0\u00a0 a large Salt Lick & Spring 9 me. up the Creek, one bushel of water will make 7 lb. of good Salt

\n

\n(Information) \u00a0\u00a0 Took Meridian altitude of \u2609 Limb. 37\u00b0 6' 0\" equat to [blank] of Lattidude.

\n

\non this Creek [Saline], So great a no of Salt Springs are on it that the water is brackish \u00a0\u00a0 N 51\u00b0 W to a Belge of an Isd\n [4] on the S. Sd. at 3 ms. Passed a Willow Isd. in Middle, Some wind in the after part of to day from the S E, (the Banks are falling in greatly in this part of the river) as also is one Side or the other in all the Course, we assended on the North Side of the Isd. and finding that the perogues Could not Keep up Camped 2 hs. by Sun. on the Sd Sd [5] \u00a0\u00a0 the land below this is good.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n June 6th Wednesday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMended our Mast this morning &, Set out at 7 oClock under a jentle breise from S. E. by S \u00a0\u00a0 passed the large Island, and a Creek Called Split rock Creek at 5 ms. on the S. S. psd. a place to the rock from which 20 yds we. \u00a0\u00a0 this Creek takes its name, a projecting rock with a hole thro: a point of the rock, at 8 ms. passed the mouth of a Creek Called \n Saline or Salt \u2329Creek\u232a R on the L. Sd. this \u2329Creek\u232a River is about 30 yds. wide, and has So many Licks & Salt Springs on its banks that the Water of the Creek is Brackish, one Verry large Lick is 9 ms. up on the left Side \u00a0\u00a0 the water of the Spring in this Lick is Strong as one bushel of the water is said to make 7 lb. of good Salt \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Isd. & Several Small ones, the water excessivly Strong, So much So that we Camped Sooner than the usial time to waite for the pirogue, The banks are falling in Verry much to day \u00a0\u00a0 river rose last night a foot.

\n

\n Capt. Lewis took meridean altd. of Suns U. L. with the octant above Split Rock C. & made the altitude 37\u00b0 6' 00 \u00a0\u00a0 error of octt. as useal 2\u00b0 0' 0\" + The Countrey for Several miles below is good, on the top of the high land back is also tolerable land \u00a0\u00a0 Some buffalow Sign to day [6]\n

\n

\nI am Still verry unwell with a Sore throat & head ake

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWednesday June 6th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we mended our mast this morning and Set out at 7 o.C. with a gentle wind up the River. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a place in the projecting Rock the hole of the Split Rock River. \u00a0\u00a0 a little above we passed Split Rock Creek. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 the Country around is Delightful \u00a0\u00a0 Good Timber. I Saw one handsome Run from under the Clifts of Rocks, passed Saleen Creek on South Side of the River. Salt has been made their &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped on North Side of the River, [words erased and crossed out, illegible]

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nwensday June 6th 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out 6 oclock after ouer mast mended \u00a0\u00a0 4 miles past a Creek on the N Side Called Rock Creek \u00a0\u00a0 on the Loer Side Blow Cliftes 3 miles past Sallin Creek \u2329Big Lick\u232a on the South Side \u2329Below\u232a Cliftes on the Loer Side \u00a0\u00a0 water good the fore part of the day the Latter part Strong \u00a0\u00a0 came 18 miles \u00a0\u00a0 ouer hunters Kild one Deer \u00a0\u00a0 encampet on the\nN Side \u2329the midel way of Isd.\u232a\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nOn the 6th, we passed Saline creek on the south side; and on the 7th the river of the Big Devil\n [9] on the north; and Big Goodwoman's creek on the same side, where we encamped.\n\n\n

\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n[W]ednesday 6th June 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek called led creek [10] on S. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek on N. Side called little good woman creek. \u00a0\u00a0 the country is good Soil rich and well timbred &c. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the North Side.\u2014

\n

\nWednesday June 6th \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early this morning, and proceeded on, & passed a Creek called Lead Creek, laying on the South side of the River; also passed another Creek, called little good Woman Creek, The soil during this days travel appeared rich, and the Country well timbered, in the Evening we encamped on the North side of the River,\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. \n Biddle's notation at the top of this sheet of the Field Notes (document 19), reads \"June 6 to June 9th.\" (back)
\n
2. A literal translation of the French name, Roche Perc\u00e9e, now Perchee Creek, in Boone County, Missouri. Thwaites (LC), 1:41 n. 2; MRC map 8. (back)
\n
3. \n Petite Saline Creek, so called from salt deposits in the vicinity, enters the Missouri River in northwest Moniteau County, Missouri. Coues (HLC), 1:16 n. 35; MRC map 8. (back)
\n
4. Evidently the island later called Rocher Island and still later Terrapin Island; it is no longer on maps. Nicollet (MMR), 358; MRC map 8; MRM map 21. (back)
\n
5. In Boone County, probably a little downstream from where Interstate Highway 70 crosses the Missouri River. MRC map 8. (back)
\n
6. The first mention of the buffalo, Bison bison, in the journals. They did not actually shoot one until August 23, 1804. Hall, 2:1108\u201310. (back)
\n
7. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
8. Contrary to Ordway's wording, there seems to be only one stream here, Perchee Creek, Boone County, Missouri. (back)
\n
9. \n Moniteau Creek on the Howard-Boone county line, Missouri. \"Big Devil\" must refer to \nthe antlered or horned figures on a nearby rock noted by Clark this day (June 7, 1804). (back)
\n
10. All the other journals indicate that they passed Lead Creek (Gass's\n Mine Creek, present Rock Creek or Mud Creek, Cole County, Missouri) on June 5. The same is true of Little Good Women Creek (present Bonne Femme Creek, Boone County, Missouri). (back)
