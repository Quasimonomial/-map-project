\n June 20th, Wednesday\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out after a heavy Shower of rain and proceeded on the Same Course of last night \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large butifull Prarie on the S. S. opposit a large Island, Calld Saukee Prarie, a gentle breese from the S. W. Some butiful high lands on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Som verry Swift water to day, I saw Pelicans to day on a Sand bar, my servant\n York nearly loseing an eye by a man throwing Sand into it, we came too at the lower Point of a Small Island, [4] the party on Shore we have not Seen Since we passed Tiger R\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Land appeard verry good in each Side of the River to day and well timbered, we took Some Loner [lunar] observations, which detained us untill 1 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 a butifull night but the air exceedingly Damp, & the mosquiters verry troublesom

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 20th. \u00a0\u00a0 At five in the morning we continued our voyage, passed Tiger creek, a large creek that flows in from the north, and encamped on an island. The land along here is good on both sides of the river.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWendy 20th \u00a0\u00a0 Rain came on as we was a goeing to start in the morning \u00a0\u00a0 Shortly after Got fair \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters [9] Came to the bank of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 the[y] Killd. a bear brought the Skin \u00a0\u00a0 left the Meat as it was poor \u00a0\u00a0 the Currant was Strong \u00a0\u00a0 towd Our boat Untill we Came to the head of the Strong watter Island whare the watter run So Rappid that the men of the french Peirouge Could not make headway by Roeing Or poleing \u00a0\u00a0 the[y] had to jumpd. out and push her through the water \u00a0\u00a0 Incampd On the point of and Islanand Calld. Strong water point \u00a0\u00a0 Roed. 12 Miles

\n

\nWednesday June 20th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning as we were preparing to start a Rain came on which detained us, for some time, in about an hour the weather got clear, and our hunters who had crossed the River, early this morning came to the opposite bank of the River having killed a bear, but it proving very poor they brought only the Skin with them, the Meat being unfit for use, we started having our boat to tow, the current of the River being so strong that we found it impossible either to Row or pole her. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on 'till we came to the head of strong Water Island, where the River ran so strong, that the Canadians who were in a Pettiauger could make no headway either by Rowing or poling, but were forced to jump into the River and push her through the water, We encamped on the point of an Island called strong Water point. \u00a0\u00a0 We towed our boat 12 Miles this day.\n\n\n\t\n

\n
1. Apparently Crooked River, in Ray County, Missouri. In Codex A Clark indicates that they passed this stream on June 19, though it is not in his courses and distances for that day. Gass and Ordway both say the expedition passed Tiger River on the twentieth, while Biddle's\n History places the event on the nineteenth, presumably following Codex A. Evidently the June 19 camp was near the mouth of that stream on the opposite side, either above or below, wherever the mouth was in 1804. MRC map 12, made about 1890, shows what may be an old channel of Crooked River, but the two mouths were not far apart. It seems most probable that they camped on the nineteenth just beyond Sheep Nose Point, with the mouth of Crooked River in sight, and passed the stream early the next morning. Coues (HLC), 1:27\u201328 and nn. 60, 61; Osgood (FN), 59 n. 8. (back)
\n
2. \n Clark interlined \"and a large Isd.\" and indicated its insertion here. Obviously they passed a willow island and a large island, the latter perhaps the later Lexington Island. Osgood (FN), 234; MRC map 12. (back)
\n
3. Some figures written diagonally at the bottom of this entry appear to be \"270 W.\" (back)
\n
4. Perhaps later Wolf Island, a few miles below present Wellington, Lafayette County, Missouri. MRC map 13. (back)
\n
5. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. The creek is apparently present Sniabar River, near Wellington, Lafayette County, Missouri. See entry for June 21. (back)
\n
6. Apparently Crooked River, Ray County, Missouri. Clark indicates that the party passed this stream on June 19; see his entry for this day for a discussion of this matter. (back)
\n
7. Mentioned only by Ordway this day, it is probably wild crab, Pyrus ioensis (Wood) Bailey. (back)
\n
8. The camp was apparently on Wolf Island, below Wellington, Lafayette County, Missouri. (back)
\n
9. Apparently John Shields and John Collins, but Clark says only that they had not joined the party for two nights and were spotted on shore. Nobody else mentions these hunters on this date. (back)
