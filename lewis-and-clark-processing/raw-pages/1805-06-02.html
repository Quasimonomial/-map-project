\n June 2, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Sunday June 2ed 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe wind blew violently last night and was attended by a slight shower of rain; the morning was fair and we set out at an early hour. \u00a0\u00a0 imployed the chord as usual the greater part of the day. \u00a0\u00a0 the courant was strong tho' regular, and the banks afforded us good toeing. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind was hard and against us yet we proceded with infinitely more ease than the two precedeing days. The river bluffs still continue to get lower and the plains leveler and more extensive; the timber on the river increases in quantity; the country in all other rispects much as discribed yesterday. I think we are now completely above the black hills\n [NB: see note of May 29] [1] \u00a0\u00a0 we had a small shower of rain today but it lasted only a few minutes and was very moderate. Game becomeing more abundant this morning and I thought it best now to loose no time or suffer an opportunity to escape in providing the necessary quantity of Elk's skins to cover my leather boat which I now expect I shall be obliged to use shortly. Accordingly I walked on shore most of the day with some of the hunters for that purpose and killed 6 Elk 2 buffale 2 Mule deer and a bear. \u00a0\u00a0 these anamals were all in good order we therefore took as much of the meat as our canoes and perogues could conveniently carry. \u00a0\u00a0 the bear was very near catching Drewyer; it also pursued Charbono who fired his gun in the air as he ran but fortunately eluded the vigilence of the bear by secreting himself very securely in the bushes untill Drewyer finally killed it by a shot in the head; the [NB: only] shot indeed that will conquer the farocity of those tremendious anamals.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 in the course of the day we passed 9 Islands all of them small and most of them containing some timber.\u2014

\n

we came too on the Lard. side in a handsome bottom of small cottonwood timber opposite to the entrance of a very considerable river; [2] but it being too late to examine these rivers minutely to night we determined to remain here untill the morning, and as the evening was favourable to make some obsevations.\u2014

[Clark]

\n \n
\n June 2nd Sunday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n June 2nd Sunday 1805. we had a hard wind & a little rain last night. this morning fair. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at an eairly hour. \u00a0\u00a0 wind from S. W. Some little rain to day \u00a0\u00a0 wind hard a head. \u00a0\u00a0 the Country much like that of yesterday, as described. Capt. Lewis walked on Shore, himself and the hunters killed 6 Elk a bear and 2 mule Deer, and 2 buffalow, which was all in good order \u00a0\u00a0 a beaver also killed this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 passed 9 Islands to day. \u00a0\u00a0 the current Swift but regular. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped [5] on the Larboard Side at the mouth or at the forks of the river. \u00a0\u00a0 the current & Sizes of them we could not examine this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair night. \u00a0\u00a0 the Captains took Some Lunr. observations, of moon & Stars\u2014. Came 18 miles to day.\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 2nd. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early in a fine morning. The hills come close on the river, but are not so high nor so broken, as we found them a short distance lower down. This forenoon we passed two creeks, [6] one on each side, and several islands covered with cotton wood; but there is not a stick of timber to be seen any where upon the hills. Some of the hunters killed a brown bear in a small botton on the South side, and having come 18 miles, we encamped just above the bottom on the same side, at the mouth of a large river.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 2nd June 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off as usal & proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 about 9 oC. Some of the hunters killed a buffalow and an Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 passed high bluffs on each Side, high plains, narrow bottoms and Islands. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek [7] on the N. S. and one on the S. Side [8] \u00a0\u00a0 about 12 oC. killed another Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 about 1 oC. we halted to dine at a bottom of timber on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the men killed another buffaloe. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind high from the N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 clouded up. \u00a0\u00a0 the current is not So Swift yesterday & to day as it has been Some time past. \u00a0\u00a0 we git along verry well with the towing lines. \u00a0\u00a0 a Small Sprinkling of rain. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 oC. we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed Several Islands of cotton wood bluffs & high land \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening the hunters killed a yallow bear in a bottom of cotton wood on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came 18 miles & Camped at a fork of the river. \u00a0\u00a0 we could not determine which was the Missourie. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters killed 6 Elk in all to day. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw a high mountain [9] to the west of us. \u00a0\u00a0 one hunter man Shot a large beaver this evening.

\n

\nSunday June 2nd \u00a0\u00a0 A Clear pleasant morning, we set off early; and proceeded on our Voyage. \u00a0\u00a0 About 9 o'Clock A. M. some of our hunters killed a buffalo, and an Elk, we proceeded on, and passed high bluffs lying on each side of the River, also high plains, narrow bottoms of land, & Islands, on both sides; and two Creeks, One on the North, and the other on the South side of the River, about 11 o'Clock A. M. one of our hunters killed another Elk, at 12 o'Clock A. M. we halted to dine on a bottom, lying on the North side of the River, Where one of our party killed a Buffalo, The wind blew hard from the North west at this place, and the Sky became Cloudy.\u2014

\n

\nThe current of the Mesouris not running so swift, these two days past; as it had done for some time before; so that we made good head way, with towing the Crafts along,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 about One o'Clock P M, we had some small\nsprinkling of rain\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 oClock P. M. we proceeded on, and passed several Islands, lying on both sides of the River; having Cotton wood on them.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 and bluffs and high land.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening, our hunters killed a Yellow Bear in a bottom, on the South side of the River, We encamped at a\nplace where the Mesouri River forked, the officers being at a loss which fork was the Mesouri River, The Hunters that were out this day, returned to us, having killed 4 more Elk, and in the Evening one beaver,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We saw a high Mountain lying to our West. \u00a0\u00a0 We came 18 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n
