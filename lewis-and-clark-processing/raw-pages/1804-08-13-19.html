[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 13th \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on under a gentle Breese\nfrom Souhard \u00a0\u00a0 we passed the mouth of the Mahar Creek\n [6] below an Island\nS. Side of the Missouri R. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on to the lower point of another Island N. S. opposite to which we Camped on S. Side Near the Mahar Village, I and 3 [7] more of the party went out to the Village or to the place where it formely Stood. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 we passed through high Grass in the low prarie & came to the Mahar Creek on our way. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded along Creek till we came to 3 forks which came in near together below the Village. \u00a0\u00a0 we crossed the North branch and proceded along the South branch which was verry fatigueing for the high Grass Sunflowers & thistles [9] &C all of which were above 10 feet high, a great quantity of wild peas [10] among those weeds, we broke our way through them till we came to where their had been a village of about 300 Cabbins called the Mahar village. \u00a0\u00a0 it was burned about 4 years ago immediately after near half the Nation died with the Small pox, which was as I was informed about 400, we found none of the natives about the place \u00a0\u00a0 they were out hunting the Buffelow, we ascended the hill above the village on which was all the Graves of the former, I Saw the grave also where the Grand chief of the Punckhas\n [11] was buried about the Same time the Mahars were &.C. &.C\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on the hill about 5 miles from the Boats. \u00a0\u00a0 we Struck up a fire \u00a0\u00a0 the Musquetoes verry troublesome, we were in Great want of warter but found none,\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 13th. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded this morning with a fair wind; and at 2 landed on a sandy beach, near the Maha village, on the south side of the river. A sergeant and one man were sent to the village, who did not return this day.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMonday Augt. 13th \u00a0\u00a0 Arived. at the fish camp Neer the Mahars Village at 4 oclock this day \u00a0\u00a0 the Commanding Officer Sent a Serjt. & 4 Men with a white flagg, to the Village to Invite them to Come to a treaty, but the[y] found no Indians at the Village \u00a0\u00a0 Returnd. Nixt day after 12 Oclock\u2014

\n

\nMonday August 13th \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early this morning and continued on, at 4 oClock P. M we arrived at a Camp, near the Mahaw Village when the commanding Officer, sent a Serjeant and four Men, with a White flag to the \nvillage, to Invite them to come to a Treaty.\u2014

\n

\nThe Serjeant & party proceeded on to the Mahaw Village but found no Indians there, we continued here this day, waiting for their return.\u2014\n\n\n

\n
1. \n Mackay established this post, named for the reigning king of Spain, Charles IV, in November 1795, and wintered there. From here his companion, Evans, made his journey up the Missouri to the Mandan villages. The site was in Dakota County, Nebraska, southeast of Homer and south of Omaha Creek, but has not been discovered by researchers. Nasatir (BLC), 1:98\u2013108; Atlas maps 7 and 16; MRC map 27; MRR maps 74, 75. (back)
\n
2. This is perhaps the best-known Omaha village. It was called \"Big Village,\" or Tonwontonga. Fletcher & La Flesche, 86, 99; Champe; Steinacher & Carlson, 29\u201336. It is in Dakota County, about one mile north of present Homer and six and a half miles south of Dakota City, on or near U.S. Highway 77. Appleman (LC), 335; Atlas map 16; MRC map 27; MRR map 75. (back)
\n
3. In either Dakota County, Nebraska, or Woodbury County, Iowa, and a few miles south of present Dakota City. Atlas map 16; MRC 27; MRR map 75. (back)
\n
4. One of the few references to \"Carrn.,\" who is otherwise \"E. Cann\" and \"Cane\" (see July 4 and August 25, 1804). Presumably he was one of the French engag\u00e9s, although he is not mentioned under that name in the list of boatmen of May 26, 1804. See Appendix A. (back)
\n
5. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
6. \n Omaha Creek, north of the Dakota-Thurston county line, Nebraska. (back)
\n
7. According to Clark the detachment consisted of Ordway, Cruzatte, George Shannon, William Werner, and the mysterious \"Carrn,\" for whom see Clark's entry for this date and Appendix A. (back)
\n
8. \n Ordway's route this day cannot be determined with great accuracy, but he seems to have gone up Omaha Creek and then crossed it to what he conceived to be forks of the creek,\nperhaps Fiddlers, Wigle, and South Omaha creeks. He then apparently camped on a hill near the Omaha village for the night. The village he discusses in this entry is Tonwontonga (see Clark's entry for this day). The rest of his discussion about the village, its surroundings, and\nthe Omaha people is apparently taken from Clark's entry of August 14 (see notes there). (back)
\n
9. Probaby the common sunflower, Helianthus annuus L., and an unknown thistle, Cirsium sp. (back)
\n
10. An unidentified legume with pea-like characteristics. (back)
\n
11. No information seems to be available to identify a chief of the Poncas buried here. The statement may be unreliable or inaccurate. (back)
