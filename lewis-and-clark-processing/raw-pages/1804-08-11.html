\n August 6th Monday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 at 12 oClock last night a Violent Storm of wind & rain from the N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 one perogue (\n Bapteest Le Joness\n [La Jeunesse] Patroon) [2] lost her Colours \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early & proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Island on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 back of this Island \n Rivie de Soldiert\n \n [3] \u00a0\u00a0 Come in on the S. S.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the Solder's River is about the Sise of Nodaway 20 yd. wide at the mouth, passed two remarkable places, where the River had once \u2329river\u232a Passed\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We have every reason to belive that one man has Deserted Moses B: Reed\n \u00a0\u00a0 he has been absent three Days and one french man we Sent to the Indian Camps has not joined us, we have reasons to beleve he lost himself in attempting to join us at the \n Council Bluff\u2014\n \u00a0\u00a0 we are deturmind to Send back 4 men to take reede Dead or alive, also hunt \n La Liberty\n and to meet us at the Mahar nation as Soon as the order is executed.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 6th \u00a0\u00a0 a violent storm came up about 12 oClock last night of wind & rain from N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Isld. on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 back of this Isld. comes in a Creek Called the \n Soldiers R.\n [5] on N. S. as big as the Nardaway R. at the mouth \u00a0\u00a0 passed round bends & Sand bars & a prarie on S. S. & Groves of cottenwood on the bank, on N. S. near the River is a Spit of cottenwood timber but praries back of that Generally.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 M. B. Reed who went back on 4th ult has not returned yet nor La Liberty who went for the Indians has not returned \u00a0\u00a0 we expect he got lost attempting to come to us from the nation but we have all reason to think that Reed has deserted. \u00a0\u00a0 we came 20\u00bd miles this day & camped [6] on the S. S. of the River, George Drewyer Joined us this afternoon & fetched 2 Deer & one faun &.C.\u2014\n\n

[Gass]

\n Monday 6th. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded at an early hour this morning, after a stormy night of wind and rain; passed a creek on the north side, at the back of an island, called Soldiers creek and encamped on the south side.\n\n

\nMonday August 6 \u00a0\u00a0 This morning being fair, we got under way at Sunrise, and passed an Island lying on the So. West side of the River, we rowed 12 Miles by Dinner, the hunters brought 3 deer to the River \u00a0\u00a0 we encamp'd on the So West side of the River in the evening having row'd 18\u00be Miles this day.\u2014\n\n\n\n\n

2. The only reference to La Jeunesse as \"patroon,\" that is, foreman of the French boatmen. Clark may have intended to refer to Baptiste Deschamps, the actual patroon, or he may only have meant that La Jeunesse was in charge of the one pirogue at this time. See Appendix A. (back)
\n
3. The mouth of Soldier River, in Harrison County, Iowa, has apparently moved about considerably over the years, just as the Missouri has changed course. See Atlas map 14. It is questionable that there were any white soldiers on this stream before Lewis and Clark. The name probably derives from the French trader's term soldier for members of Indian warrior societies, who often kept order in camp and protected traders. Petersen, 464\u201365; Hodge, 2:614\u201315; Nicollet (MMR), 388; Warren map 6; MRC maps 24, 25; MRR maps 68, 69. (back)
\n
4. The courses and distances give the only information on this day's camp, other than that on Atlas map 14. It was apparently on the starboard side, in Harrison County, Iowa, roughly halfway between the Soldier and Little Sioux rivers. MRC map 24; MRR maps 69, 70. (back)
