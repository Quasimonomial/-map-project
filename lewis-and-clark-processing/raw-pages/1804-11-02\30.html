\n November 2, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n 2nd Novr. 1804 Friday\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis returned to the Village & I fixed on a place for to build a fort and Set to work [1] \u00a0\u00a0 Cap Lewis returned in the eveng with 11 bushels of Corn, the Ricarre Chief Set out for his Village accompanied by Several mandans\u2014

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n 2nd November Friday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThis morning at Day light I went down the river with 4 men to look for a proper place to winter \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded down the river three miles & found a place well Supld. with wood, & returned, Capt. Lewis went to the village to here what they had to Say & I fell down, and formed a camp near where a Small Camp of Indian were huntig \u00a0\u00a0 Cut down the Trees around our Camp, in the evening Capt. Lewis returned with a present of 11 bushe[l]s of Corn, our recaree Chief Set out acccompanied by one Chief [NB: of Mandans \u2329& Minetarees\u232a] and Several Brave men, [NB: of Minitarees & Mandans\n ] \u00a0\u00a0 he Called for Some Small article which we had \u2329given\u232a [NB: promised] but as I could not understand him he Could not get [NB: (afd he did get it] [NB: leave out this] [2] \u00a0\u00a0 the wind from the S. E. a fine day\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 many Indians \u2329to view us\u232a to day\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Friday November 2nd 1804\n [3]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThis morning early we fixed on the site for our fortification which we immediately set about.

\n

\nThis place we have named Fort Mandan in honour of our Neighbours. \n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 2nd Nov. \u00a0\u00a0 a cloudy morning. Capt. Clark went with Some men down the Bottom to look for a place to Build our huts. \u00a0\u00a0 they Returned Shortly \u00a0\u00a0 had found a Good place a Short distance down where their was an Indian camp in a Grove of large cottonwood Timber. \u00a0\u00a0 Sevral Indians at Sd. camp \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis & Several of the party [4] went in a pearogue up to the 1st village of the Mandans in order to Git corn. \u00a0\u00a0 we droped down a Short distance farther to a body & Bottom of large Timber where we commenced falling Timber, and fixing a camp close by the place where we intend for to build. \u00a0\u00a0 picthed our tents & laid the foundation of one line of our huts, which consisted of 4 Rooms 14 feet Square. \u00a0\u00a0 the other line will be the Same \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis returned. \u00a0\u00a0 brought us 10 or 12 bushels of Good corn. \u00a0\u00a0 we find the cottonwood Timber will Split Tollorable well, and as their is no other building timber in this bottom we expect to Split punchin to cover the huts with. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our french hands is discharged & gone down the river.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 2nd. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis, myself and some of the men, went up to the first village of the Mandans, who gave us some corn. Captain Clarke and the rest of our party, having dropt half a mile lower down the river, began to clear a place for a camp and fort. We pitched our tents and laid the foundation of one line of huts.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 2nd November \u00a0\u00a0 Began the works of the fort \u00a0\u00a0 the weather Continued pleasant for 14 days during which time all the men at Camp Ocepied thair time dilligentntly in Building their huts and got them Made Comfertable in that time to live in\u2014

\n

\nFriday November 2d \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we began to build the Fort, having pleasant weather for 14 days, during which time all hands were busily employed in building of Huts to winter in, they compleated them in that time and made them comfortable to live in.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. The site of Fort Mandan, where the Corps of Discovery remained until April 1805, is in McLean County, North Dakota, about fourteen miles west of Washburn. The actual site has been washed away by the Missouri and lies at least partially underwater. Appleman (LC), 341; North Dakota Guide, 204; Atlas map 29; Warren map 88; MRC, map 51. (back)
\n
2. \n Biddle has made considerable emendations here to get it to read differently, perhaps for his History. He finally concluded: \"leave out.\" (back)
\n
3. \n Lewis's note from Codex O, though not concerned with astronomy. (back)
\n
4. Including Gass, according to Gass. (back)
