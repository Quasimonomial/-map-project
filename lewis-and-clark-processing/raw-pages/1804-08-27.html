\n August 27, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n27th August Monday, this morning the Morning Star was observed to be very large, G Drewyer Came up and informed that he Could neither find Shannon or the horses, he had walked all night\u2014 we Sent Shields & J. Fields back to look for Shannon & the horses and to Come up with us on the river above at the grand Callemet or River KaCure\n [1] & we Set out under a Gentle Breeze from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Bluff at 7 mes. Several mile in extent of white Clay Marl or Chalk, under this bank we discovered Large Stone resembling lime incrusted with a Substanc like Glass which I take to be Cabolt, also ore, [2] three mes above this Bluff we Set the Prarie on fire, to let the Soues Know we wished to see them \u00a0\u00a0 at two oClock an Indian Swam to the Perogue, we landed & two other Came \u00a0\u00a0 they were boys, they informed us that the \n Souex\n were Camped near, on the R Jacke \u00a0\u00a0 one Maha boy informed us his nation was gorn to make a peace with the Pania's [Pawnee] \u00a0\u00a0 we Send Sjt. Pryor & a frenchman with the Interptr. Mr. Durion to the Camp to See & invite their Great Chiefs to Come and Counsel with us at the Callemet Bluffs [blank] Mile abov on L. S.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we proceed on 1\u00bd miles farther & Camped S S. [3]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
27th August Monday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nThis morning the Star Calld. the morning Star much larger than Common \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer Came up and informed that he Could neither find Shannon nor horses, we Sent Shields & J Fields, back to hunt \u2329Sha\u232a Shannon & the horses, with derections to Keep on the Hills to the Grand Calumet above on River Ka cure\n [NB: quecure].

\n

\nWe Set Sail under a gentle Breeze from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 at 7 miles passed a white Clay marl or Chalk Bluff \u00a0\u00a0 under this Bluff is extensive I discovered large Stone much like lime incrusted with a Clear Substance which I believe to be Cabalt, also ore is imbeded in the Dark earth, resembling Slate much Softer\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 above this Bluff we had the Prarie Set on fire to let the Souix See that we were on the river, as a Signal for them to Come to it.

\n

\nat 2 oClock passed the mouth of \n River Jacque\n , or Yeankton \u00a0\u00a0 one Indian at the mouth of this river Swam to the Perogue, we landed and two others came to us, those Inds. informed that a large Camp of Soues, were on R. Jacque near the mouth. \u00a0\u00a0 we Sent Sergt. Pryor & a Frenchman with Mr. Durioin the Souis interpeter to the Camp with derections to invite the Principal Chiefs to councel with us at a Bluff above Called the Calumet\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 two of those Indians accompanied them and the third continued in the Boat Showing an inclination to Continue, this boy is a Mahar, and inform that his nation, were gorn to the Parnias to make a peace with that nation.

\n

\nWe proceeded on about one and a half miles and in Camped on a bar makeing out from the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew hard from the South. \u00a0\u00a0 a Cool & Pleasent evening, The river has fallen verry Slowly and is now low.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 27th \u00a0\u00a0 we heard G. Drewyer hollow before day this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 the pearogue went over eairly for him \u00a0\u00a0 he had not found the horses. \u00a0\u00a0 2 other men went out to hunt them, 2 of the party caught 12 fine cat fish last night, we Set off at Sun rise under a gentle Breeze from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw a mink [8] under the bank \u00a0\u00a0 we Sailed on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Chalk Bluff on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 in the Same Bluff their is a vane of Black Sulpheras clay which has the appearance of Slate or Stone coal & Some kinds of mineral Substance, we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 the Sand blew thick till abt. 3 o.C. P. M. \u00a0\u00a0 passed the Mouth of a large creek called River [pt.?] Shark\n Jaque\n [9] on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 here we See a likely young Indian of the Mahar nation. \u00a0\u00a0 he told us that their camp was near; their is considerable of cottonwood Timber about this place, while we were halted here 2 more young Indians came to us. \u00a0\u00a0 one a Mahar the other a Siowee. Sergt. pryor & 2 more [10] went from the Boat with 2 of the Indians out to their Camp to invite them in to See us especially the Chiefs, the other Indian came along and went with us, we proceeded on till dark and Camped on a large Sand beach on the N. S.\n\n\n

\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 27th. \u00a0\u00a0 Got under way at sunrise, and passed white bluffs on the south side. At 2 we stopped for dinner, and an Indian of the Mahas nation, who lives with the Sioux came to us here, at the mouth of the Sacque river; [11] and while we remained here two more came in. A sergeant with our old Frenchman and another man went with two of the Indians to their camps, and the other went with us in the boat. We encamped on a sand beach on the north side.\n\n

\n \n

\t\nMonday 27th \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer came to us this morning \u00a0\u00a0 2 men Sent out for to hunt the horses. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at Sun rise under a gentle Breeze from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a chalk Bluff on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 in this Bluff is Mineral Substance &c\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we passed the Mouth of a large Creek called River Jaque on the N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 here we Saw 3 Indians [12] they informed us that their Camp was near & Sergt. pryor & 2 men went \u00a0\u00a0 we then proceeded on until dark & camped on a large Sand beach on the N. Side.

\n

\nMonday August 27th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning early George Drewyer our hunter came to us, two of our Men were sent out to hunt the horses, We set out at sunrise, with a gentle breese from the South East, and proceeded on, we passed a Chalk bluff, laying on the South side of the River; this bluff had also Mineral substance on it. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and passed the Mouth of a large Creek, called the River Jacque, lying on the North side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 Here we met with 3 Indians, who informed us that their Camp lay near us. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis sent Serjeant Pryor & 2 Men to find them out & bring them to us.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on our way with the boat &ca till dark, and encamped on a large Sand barr, lying on the North side of the River\u2014\n\n\n

1. For \n L'Eau qui Court\n , or Niobrara River, see below, September 4, 1804. (back)
\n
2. Here Clark gets a good view of the Niobrara Formation, an impure chalk or marl, which later became famous for the fossil skeletons of marine reptiles and remains of foraminifera (one-celled animals) which it contains in abundance. The glassy substance is probably the coating of a concretion or nodule which is common in the Niobrara. Clark's references to cobalt and ore are wishful thinking as no metallic ores have been discovered here. (back)
\n
3. In Yankton County, South Dakota, between the mouth of the James River and the present town of Yankton. Atlas map 18; MRC map 30; MRR maps 82, 83. (back)
\n
4. The origin of the name Rivi\u00e8re aux Jacques is uncertain. It is now the James River, one of the principal streams of eastern North and South Dakota, locally known as \"Jim River.\" Atlas map 18; MRC map 30. (back)
\n
5. The head of the James, in Wells County, North Dakota, is nowhere near the headwaters of the Mississippi, Red, or Minnesota rivers. (back)
\n
6. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
7. \n Lewis's undated observation from Codex O, immediately following the previous one; the location seems to be near the campsite of August 27. (back)
\n
8. \n Ordway is the only writer who mentions seeing a mink, Mustela vison. (back)
\n
9. \n James River, Yankton County, South Dakota. (back)
\n
10. \n Pierre Dorion, Sr. and another Frenchman with him. (back)
\n
11. \n Gass's version of the Rivi\u00e8re aux Jacques, now James River, Yankton County, South Dakota. (back)
\n
12. The Indians were Yankton\n Sioux, the first plains nomads the party had met. (back)
