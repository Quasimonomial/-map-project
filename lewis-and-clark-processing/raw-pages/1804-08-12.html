\n August 12, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark] [1]

\n \n
\u00a0

\n12th August Sunday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 a South wind \u00a0\u00a0 We Set out early \u00a0\u00a0 the river wider than usial, and Shallow, at 12 we halted in a bend to the left to take the Meridian altitude, & Dine, & Sent one man across where we took Dinner yesterday to Step off the Distance across Isthmus, he made it 974 yards, and the bend around is 18\u00be miles above this bend about 4 miles, a yellow & Brown Bluff Comnuces and Continus 3 or 4 miles on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 this Bluff has Some Sand Stone, Some rich Black mole [mold] mixed with yellow Clay, [2] a fiew Red Ceeder [3] on the tope, which is \u2329about\u232a, from 20 to 150 foot high \u00a0\u00a0 the hill Still riseing back, I think may be estemated at 200 foot \u00a0\u00a0 on the top is timber, the wind for a few hours this evening was hard and from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 In the evening about 5 oClock Cap. L. & My Self wen on Shore to Shoot a Prarie wolf [4] which was barking at us as we passed [5] This Prarie Wolf barked like a large fest [feist] and is not much larger, the Beaver is verry plenty, not with Standing we are almost in Sight of the Mahar Town\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Cought a verry Large Catfish this morniong, prepared the Indian present which we intend given to the \n Mahars. \u00a0\u00a0 P. Wiser apt. Cook to Serjt: Floyds Squad from to day [6]\n

\nSet out early under a gentle Breeze from the South \u00a0\u00a0 the river wider than usial and Shallow \u00a0\u00a0 (1) at 12 oClock we halted to take a meridian altd. of the Sun & Sent a man back or I may Say across to the Bind of the river where Capt. Lewis took the Mdn. altitude yesterday, to Step off the distance, he made it 974 yards across, the Distance arround the bend is 18\u00be miles\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 about 4 miles above the bend on the L. S. is the Commencement of a Bluff which is about 4 miles extending on the river, of yellow and brown Clay \u00a0\u00a0 in Some parts in it near the river a Soft Sand Stone is inbeded on the top (which is from 20 to 150 feet above the water, & rises back) is Covered with timber, a fiew red Ceider is on this Bluff, the wind Comes round to the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 a Prarie Wolf Come near the bank and Barked at us this evening, we made an attempt but could not git him, this Animale Barkes like a large feste Dog. Beever is verry Plenty on this part of the river. I prepare Some presents for to give the Indians of the \n Mahars\n nation. Wiser apt. Cook & Supentdt. of the Provisions of Sergt. Floyds Squad. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on a Sand Island in a bend to the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Musquitors verry troublesom untile the wind rose. \u00a0\u00a0 at one or 2 oClock

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 12th \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on round a bend which was 18 miles Round by water & only little better than half a mile a cross by land, S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we Sailed on with a S. E. wind. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Red Ceeder [11] Bluff on S. S. abot. 200 feet high \u00a0\u00a0 the course bearing North,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the land on the N. S. is low \u00a0\u00a0 the Timber cottenwood & large willows, & Subject to overflow, (Grapes &.C. in abundance[)] \u00a0\u00a0 we heard a prarie woolf bark on the bank N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 it resembled our Indian dogs.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on a Sand bar S. Side of the Missourie River,\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 12th. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked and got under way before day light. The musketoes last night were worse than I ever experienced. We went round a bend, of eighteen miles, the neck of which was only 974 yards across; passed high bluffs of yellow clay on the south side of the river and low land on the north; and encamped on a sand island.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday Augt 12th \u00a0\u00a0 the Morning was fair a Sharp Breese of wind Blew from the South \u00a0\u00a0 Sald. 21 Miles from 12 Oclock the 11th to 12 Oclock this day and Gaind. 914 [12] yards on a direct Cource. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the W. S. \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329roe\u232a 21 Miles\u2014

\n

\nSunday August 12th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we Started early with a fair wind from the South, We found by one o'Clock P. M. that we had sailed 21 Miles from 12 oClock the 11th to 12 o'Clock, this day and only gained 914 Yards on our direct course.\u2014

\n

\nWe continued Sailing 'till the evening and encamped on the South side of the River, having Sailed 21 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n
