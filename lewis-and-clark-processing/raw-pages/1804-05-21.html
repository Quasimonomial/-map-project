\n May 21, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
Monday 21st May
\u00a0
\n \n

\nDine with Mr. Ducete & Set out from St. Charles at three oClock after getting every matter arranged, proceeded on under a jentle Breese, at one mile a Violent rain with Wind from the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 we landed at the upper point of the first Island on the Stbd Side & Camped, [1] Soon after it commenced raining & continued the greater part of the night; 3 french men got leave to return to Town, and return early (refur to Fig:, 2.)

\n

\n25st refured to fig: 2 [2] Left St: Charles May 21st 1804. Steered N. 15\u00b0 W 1\u00be Ms \u00a0\u00a0 N 52\u00b0 W to the upper point of the Island and Camped dureing a rain which had been falling half an hour, opposit this Isd. Coms in a Small creek on the St. Sd. and at the head one on the Ld. Side [3] rains powerfully.

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 21st 1804 Monday\u2014
\u00a0
\n \n

\nAll the forepart of the Day Arranging our party and prcureing the different articles necessary for them at this place\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Dined with Mr. Ducett and Set out at half passed three oClock under three Cheers from the gentlemen on the bank and proceeded on to the head of the Island (which is Situated on the Stbd Side) 3 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Soon after we Set out to day a hard Wind from the W. S W accompanied with a hard rain, which lasted with Short intervales all night, opposit our Camp a Small creek coms in on the Lbd Side\u2014

Monday May 21st 1804,\n
\u00a0

\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nMonday 21st May 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 Some rainy. \u00a0\u00a0 we took on board Some more provision bread &c. \u00a0\u00a0 about 4oClock P.M we Set out from this place. \u00a0\u00a0 fired our bow peace [5] and gave three cheers, and proceeded on in good heart, about [blank] miles and Camped on the North Side. \u00a0\u00a0 2 frenchman went back to the village. \u00a0\u00a0 two of our men Stayed at St. Charls in order to come on with the horses

\n

\nMonday May 21st \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we had some Rain, part of this day was employed in taking in Provisions &ca.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 about 4oClock P.M. we took our departure from Saint Charles, (a number of the Inhabitants had assembled to see us set off) we fired our Swivel, from the Bow of our boat; and gave them three Cheers, which they returned; we then proceeded up the River about 3 Miles, and came to, on the North side of said River where we encamped. \u00a0\u00a0 We sent two of our hands (Canadians) back to Saint Charles, in Order to bring on two of our Men, that were left with horses at that place. \u00a0\u00a0 We found the current of the River very rapid, the Banks steep, & the bottom very miry. \u00a0\u00a0 The course of the River running due West from its mouth this place. \u00a0\u00a0 The country lying level, and very fertile.\u2014\n\n

3. This first creek is probably either Ducket or Little Ducket Creek, in St. Charles County , both probably named for Fran\u00e7ois Duquette. The second is probably Creve Coeur Creek, in the opposite shore in St. Louis County . MRC map 2. (back)
