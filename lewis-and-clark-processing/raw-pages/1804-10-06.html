\n October 6, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n 6th of October Satturday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Cold Wind from the N. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw many large round Stones near the middle of the River \u00a0\u00a0 passed an old Ricara village of 80 Lodges Picketed in \u00a0\u00a0 those lodges in nearly an octagon form, 20 to 60 feet Diameter \u00a0\u00a0 Specious [spacious] Covered with earth and as Close as they Can Stand, [1] a number of Skin Canoes [2] in the huts, we found Squashes of 3 different Kinds growing in the Village \u00a0\u00a0 Shields Killed an Elk Close by\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Magpy is common here, we Camped off the mouth of Otter Creek on the S. S. [3] this Creek is 22 yds. wide & heads near the R. Jacque,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 contains much water.

[Clark]

6th October Satturday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\na cool morning wind from the North \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed a willow Island (1) Situated near the S. Shore at the upper point of Som timber on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 many large round Stones near the middle of the river, those Stones appear to have been washed from the hills \u00a0\u00a0 (2) passed a village of about 80 neet Lodges covered with earth and picketed around, those loges are Spicious of an Octagon form as close together as they can possibly be placed and appear to have been inhabited last Spring, from the Canoes of Skins Mats buckets [5] found in the lodges, we are of appinion they were the recrereis \u00a0\u00a0 we found Squashes of 3 Different Kinds growing in the Village, [6] one of our men killed an Elk Close by this Village, I saw 2 wolves in persute of another which appeared to be wounded and nearly tired, we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 found the river Shole \u00a0\u00a0 we made Severl. attempts to find the main Channel between the Sand bars, and was obliged at length to Drag the boat over to Save a league which we must return to get into the deepest Channel, we have been obgd to hunt a Chanl. for Some time past the river being devided in many places in a great number of Chanels, Saw Gees, Swan, Brants, & Ducks of Different kinds on the Sand bars to day, Capt Lewis walked on Shore Saw great numbers of Prarie hens, [7] I observe but fiew Gulls or Pleaver in this part of the river, The Corvos or Magpye is verry Common in this quarter

\n

\nWe Camped on a large Sand bar off the mouth of \u2329Beaver or\u232a [8]\n Otter Creek on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 this Creek is about 22 yards wide at the mouth and contains a greater perpotion of water than Common for Creeks of its Sise

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday 6th Oct. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed Black Bluffs on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 high land, hilley & plains on boath Sides of the River \u00a0\u00a0 no Timber only in the Bottoms on the River.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 passd a Timbered Bottom on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men went out hunting\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 at 1 oClock we halted at an old Rickree Village on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 took dinner. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters came to us \u00a0\u00a0 had killed a fat Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 we found at this village Some Squashes. \u00a0\u00a0 the Rick Rees left it last Spring. \u00a0\u00a0 their village was built verry close compact, & covered each Sepperate house with Earth. \u00a0\u00a0 we Saw Several canoes made of Buffalow hides which would carry 2 men & considerable baggage, also Some baskets \u00a0\u00a0 we took Several of them & Some of the Squashes &.C\u2014&.C\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis & one hunter went out hunting in a handsome Bottom covered with Timber on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek on the South Side [9] \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on a large Sand beach on N. S. Capt. Lewis & the hunter joined us. \u00a0\u00a0 brought no Game with them.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the 2 Capt. & 2 more knockd for \nbow pack [10]\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 6th. \u00a0\u00a0 We continued our voyage early, and had a clear day; passed bluffs on the south side and a bottom covered with timber on the north. About 11 we passed a handsome bottom, where a band of the Rees lived last winter. They had left a number of round huts covered with earth, some of their water craft made of buffaloe hides, and some garden truck, such as squashes. We proceeded on and passed a small creek on the south side; a handsome bottom on the north; and encamped on a sand beach on the north side.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 6th Oct. \u00a0\u00a0 Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 clear & pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 oC we were passing a bottom covered with timber on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 2 of our hunters went out and killed 1 Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 in this bottom a band of the Rick a rees lived last winter. \u00a0\u00a0 they left a nomber of round huts covered with earth, and Some water crafts made out of buffaloe hides. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a creek on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on a Sand beach on N. S.

\n

\nSaturday October 6th \u00a0\u00a0 We set off early this morning, having clear pleasant Weather and continued on 'till 11 oClock A. M. and passing by a bottom covered with heavy Timber, one of our Hunters went on shore, and killed an Elk in this bottom, where we found a Band of the Rick ARees Indians had lived, during the last Winter, They had left a number of round huts, which was cover'd with Earth, and some Water Crafts made out of buffalo Hides\u2014

\n

\nWe stopped and took the Man & Elk on board, and proceeded on, and passed a Creek lying on the South side of the River, and in the Evening We encamped on the bank lying on the North side of the River.\u2014\n\n\n\n

\n \n \n
\n
1. \n Clark is describing the earth lodge characteristic of the sedentary tribes of the Missouri. The area is shown on Atlas map 25. (back)
\n
2. Probably \"bullboats,\" buffalo skins stretched over a hemispherical frame. Though hard to steer, they were handy for crossing the river and could be carried by one person. (back)
\n
3. Later known as Swan Creek, in Walworth County, South Dakota. There is an Otter\nCreek a few miles farther down the Missouri, but its location does not correspond with that shown on Atlas map 25. The campsite would now be inundated by the Oahe Reservoir. Mattison (OR), 76\u201377; MRC map 44. (back)
\n
4. In Codex C the distance is 2\u00bd miles, with the fraction possibly crossed out. (back)
\n
5. \n Biddle apparently added the letters \"uc\" in red in the middle of this word, blotting out Clark's first spelling. (back)
\n
6. Varieties of Cucurbita pepo L., pumpkins and squashes, and possibly C. maxima Duchesne, hubbard and turban squashes. Fernald, 1349. Gilmore lists eight types of pumpkins and squashes among the Omahas. Gilmore (UPI), 65\u201366. (back)
\n
7. Perhaps the same as the \"grouse\" of October 7, below, that is, the sharp-tailed grouse. See April 15, 1805, below, where Lewis notes that grouse are also called \"prairie hens.\" (back)
\n
8. \n Biddle apparently crossed out \"Beaver or\" with his usual red ink. (back)
\n
9. Perhaps Four Bears Creek, Dewey County, South Dakota, mentioned by other enlisted men but not by Clark. (back)
\n
10. This final sentence does not make sense. The words \"knockd,\" \"bow,\" and \"pack\" may not be correct. The other journals are no help. (back)
