\n August 4th \u00a0\u00a0 at 7 oClock the heavens darkened and a violent wind from the N W. Suckceeded which lasted about \u2329half\u232a an hour, with a little rain. Set out this morning early thro a narrow part of the [channel], the whole Channel Confined in Some parts between the (1) Sand on one Side & the bank on the other (which is washing in) within 200 yards, this Chanl. Crouded with Snags. \u00a0\u00a0 at 1\u00bd m: passed an old tradeing house L. S. where one of our Crew passed 2 years \n P. C\n [Pierre Cruzatte] tradeing with the Mahar; & Ponies\u2014 [3] \u00a0\u00a0 above 1 me. a (3) Creek [4] Coms in opsd. a large bad (2) Sand bar \u00a0\u00a0 this (3) Creek is the outlett of 3 ponds, which recved ther water from the Smaller Streams running from the hills on the L. S, Great qts. of Gees, passed in the next bend L. S. an out let to the Pond, Butifull bottom Prarie on both Sides of the river, Pumey [pumice] Stone is found on the Sides of the river of various Sizes. [5] Wind a head. Reed the man who went back to the Camp of last night for his Knife has not Come up this evening\u2014 [6] \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped at a pt. on the L. S. at a Beaver house. [7] \u00a0\u00a0 1 Buck Killed to daye. [8]\n

\n August 4th Satturdaye\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out early\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 (at 7 oClock last night we had a Violent wind from the N W \u00a0\u00a0 Som little rain Succeeded, the wind lasted with violence for one hour \u00a0\u00a0 after the wind it was clear Sereen and Cool all night.) \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed thro betwen Snags which was quit across the Rivr \u00a0\u00a0 the Channel Confined within 200 yards one Side a Sand pt. S S. the other a Bend, the Banks washing away & trees falling in constantly for 1 mile, abov this place is the remains of an old Tradeing establishment L. S. where Petr. Crusett one of our hands Stayed two years & traded with the \n Mahars\n \u00a0\u00a0 a Short distance above is a Creek (3) the out let of Three Ponds comunicateing with each other, those Ponds or rether Lakes are fed by Springs & Small runs from the hills. \u00a0\u00a0 (2) a large Sand Island opposit this Creek makeing out from the L. Point, from the Camp of last night to this Creek, the river has latterly Changed its bed incroaching on the L. Side, in this Sand bar I Saw great Nos. of wild gees\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Creek on the L. S about 3 miles above the last \u00a0\u00a0 both of those Creek's are out lets from the Small Lake which reive their water from the Small Streems running from the high land\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 great ma[n]y Pamey Stones on the Shore of various Sises \u00a0\u00a0 the wind blew hard\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 \n Reed\n a man who went back to Camp for his knife has not joined us. \u00a0\u00a0 we camped at a Beaver house on the L. S.\u2014 one Buck Killed to day\u2014

\n Saturday 4th \u00a0\u00a0 at 7 oClock last night, a violent wind from the N. W. & thunder & rain which lasted about an hour then ceased blowing but hard rain followed, all Set out eairly this morning \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on through a narrow part of the River which is filled with Snags & logs \u00a0\u00a0 the River in many places is confined within 200 yards. \u00a0\u00a0 (Reed went back for knife) \u00a0\u00a0 we passed an\nOld Tradeing house where one of our party passd. 2 years \n P. C\n . [10] trading with the Mahar, & ponies.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 above that we passd. a Small Creek [11] which comes in behind a large Sand bar \u00a0\u00a0 this Creek is the outelet of 3 ponds which make in from towards the hills.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the Bottom and high praries extends along Both Sides of the River the most of this days march, Some young Groves of cotton wood on the points \u00a0\u00a0 we Road 10\u00bd miles and Camped [12] on the S. S. on a high bank of willows.\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 4th. \u00a0\u00a0 We were early under way this morning, and had a fair day. We passed a creek on the south side, which came out of ponds. One of our men [14] went out this morning and did not return: another came to us and brought a deer. We encamped on the south side.\n\n

\nSaturday Augst 4th \u00a0\u00a0 The morning was Clear \u00a0\u00a0 passd. Several Indian Old Camps On the W. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one was Calld. the hat as the Indian died there namd the hat [15] \u00a0\u00a0 Campd. on the East Side \u00a0\u00a0 Roaed 17 ms.\u2014

\n

\nSaturday August 4th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we set off early, having fine Clear weather, and passed several old Indian Camps lying on the So West side of the River, which were called the hat, it being the name of an Indian, who had died there of that name; in the Evening we encamped on the No. East side of the River, having rowed 17 Miles this day\n\n\n

4. Probably Fish Creek, Blair. Atlas map 14; MRC map 24. (back)

5. Several exposures of volcanic ash (pumice), long called the Pearlette Ash, have been observed along the Missouri River bluffs north of Omaha, and one near the mouth of Ponca Creek, south of Blair, was recorded by Miller. Although they were long considered to represent a single ash fall, recent studies have shown that there are at least three in eastern Nebraska and western Iowa, with ages between .6 and 2.0 million years. At present (1984) no ash beds exposed in this reach of the river have been dated. Miller; Reed & Dreeszen; Boellstorff; Izett. The pumicestone could also be one of several varieties of rock produced by burning of coal beds in North Dakota and carried to this point by floods. The rocks are not volcanic. Information of Robert N. Bergantino, Montana Bureau of Mines and Geology. (back)
