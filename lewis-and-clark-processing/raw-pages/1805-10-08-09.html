[Clark]

\n October 8th \u2329Monday\u232a Tuesday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\t\nA Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 loaded our Canoes which was unloaded last night and Set out at 9 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 passed 15 rapids four Islands and a Creek on the Stard Side at 16 miles just below which one canoe in which Serjt. Gass was Stearing and was nearle turning over, She Sprung a leak or Split open on one Side and Bottom filled with water & Sunk on the rapid, the men, Several of which Could not Swim hung on to the Canoe, I had one of the other Canoes unloaded & with the assistance of our Small canoe and one Indians Canoe took out every thing & \u2329got\u232a toed the empty Canoe on Shore, [2] one man Tompson a little hurt, every thing wet perticularly the greater part of our Small Stock of merchindize, had every thing opened, and two Sentinals put over them to keep off the Indians, who are enclined to theave haveing Stole Several Small articles \u00a0\u00a0 those people appeared disposed to give us every assistance in their power dureing our distress\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We passed Several Encampments of Indians on the Islands and those near the rapids in which places they took the Salmon, at one of Those Camps we found our two Chiefs who had promised to accompany us, we took them on board after the Serimony of Smokeing\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday 8th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we delayed here Sometime changing the officers canoes &C. \u00a0\u00a0 hid a canister of Powder by a broken top tree. \u00a0\u00a0 about 9 oClock we Set out and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Some Indians horses on the Side of the hills on Stard. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Several bad rapids \u00a0\u00a0 took in Some water by the waves. \u00a0\u00a0 passed Some clifts of rocks and barron hills on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 about 12 OClock we halted at Some Indian Camps about 6 lodges of well looking Savages who had Several Small canoes and catch considerable of Sammon. \u00a0\u00a0 we bought some from them. \u00a0\u00a0 2 dogs also. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on a Short distance and halted at Some more Camps at the foot of an Isld. and rapids where we bought Some more Sammon and Some white roots &C. \u00a0\u00a0 then proceed on \u00a0\u00a0 descended a rockey rapid at the foot of an Island where was Several Indian Camps. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes Struck a rock in the middle of the rapid and Swang round and Struck an other rock and cracked hir So that it filled with water. \u00a0\u00a0 the waves roared over the rocks and Some of the men could not Swim. \u00a0\u00a0 their they Stayed in this doleful Situation untill we unloaded one of the other canoes and went and released them. \u00a0\u00a0 2 Indians went in a canoe to their assistance also. \u00a0\u00a0 we got the men and the most of the baggage Safe to Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 a fiew articles lost \u00a0\u00a0 one tommahawk and a fiew light things. \u00a0\u00a0 we put the baggage out and Camped [3] on the Stard. Side at high plains. \u00a0\u00a0 a number of Savages visited us this evening\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 had Come about 18 miles to day\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 8th. \u00a0\u00a0 At 9 o'clock in a fine morning we continued our voyage down the river: passed three islands and several rapids; and at noon stopped at some Indian lodges, of which there are a great many along the river. At 2 we proceeded on again. In the evening, in passing through a rapid, I had my canoe stove, and she sunk. Fortunately the water was not more than waist deep, so our lives and baggage were saved, though the latter was wet. We halted and encamped here to repair the canoe, after coming 18 miles. At this place there are some lodges of the natives on both sides of the river; a \nnumber of whom keep about us, and we get some fish from them. Two chiefs [4] of the upper village joined us here, and proposed to go with us, until we should meet with white people; which they say will be at no great distance.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 8th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair day. \u00a0\u00a0 we dilayed loading &c. \u00a0\u00a0 burryed a canister of powder the Northe Side of a broken toped tree. \u00a0\u00a0 about 9 oClock we Set out and proceeded on down the River. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Some Indian horses on the Side of the hills \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Several bad rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 took Some water in the canoes by the waves dashing over the Sides. \u00a0\u00a0 the current rapid the most part of the way some places deep. \u00a0\u00a0 passed clifts of rocks and bare hills on each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 about 12 oClock we Came to Some Indian Camps, on the South Side. \u00a0\u00a0 only 4 or 5 lodges of well looking Indians & Squaws. \u00a0\u00a0 they had Several Small canoes, and catch considerable quantitys of Sammon. \u00a0\u00a0 we purchased Some from them by giving them a fiew green or blue or red beeds, and tin &c. \u00a0\u00a0 the day warm. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the men bought 2 dogs from them. \u00a0\u00a0 they have a great many horses feeding along the Shores and have a nomber of Small canoes. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on a Short distance further down came to Some more Indian Camps at the foot of an Isl. & rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted a Short time, bought Some more Sammon and Some white roots. \u00a0\u00a0 then proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 a Short distance further down 2 chiefs came with us. \u00a0\u00a0 as we were descending a rockey rapids at the foot of an Island on which was Some Indian Camps, one of the canoes Struck a rock and wheled round then Struck again and cracked the canoe and was near Spliting hir in too. \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329thrung\u232a throwed the Stearsman [5] over board, who with difficulty got to the canoe again, but She soon filled with water, and hang on the rocks in a doleful Situation. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the men on board could not Swim, and them that could had no chance for the waves and rocks. \u00a0\u00a0 an Indian went in a Small canoe to their assistance. \u00a0\u00a0 our little canoe went also and took out Some of the loading, and \ncarried it to Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 we unloaded one of the other canoes and went in the rapid and took the loading all out of the canoe which was Stove and got all to Shore below the rapid, and Camped, at dark. \u00a0\u00a0 examined found everry thing wet which was in the canoe that was Stove. \u00a0\u00a0 Some Small articles lost. \u00a0\u00a0 a nomber of the natives visit us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 we have come about 18 miles this day before the Sad axident hapened to us

\n

\nTuesday October 8th \u00a0\u00a0 We had a fair day, We delayed for to load one of our Canoes & burying a Cannister of powder; which we did on the North side of a broken top Tree, About 9 o'Clock A. M. we set out, & proceeded on with our Canoes down the River on our Voyage; we saw a number of the Indians horses feeding on the side of hills, as we passed along, We also passed over several bad rapids, where our Canoes took in Water, occasion'd by the Waves dashing over the sides of them.\u2014

\n

\nThe Current of the River has run rapid the most part of the way this day, & in some places we found the River very deep. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed by large Clifts of rocks & naked hills lying on both sides of the River.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 About 12 oClock A. M. we arrived at some Indian Camps, which lay on the South side of the River, in which we saw some well looking Indian Men & Squaws; there was only 5 lodges at this place, and they had several small Canoes, which were tied to the Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 These Indians employ themselves in catching of Salmon and catch considerable quantity of them \u00a0\u00a0 They were part of the flatt head Nation.\u2014

\n

\nWe stopped with our Canoes some time, & purchased from these Indians some Salmon for which we gave them round Blue & Green beads, & some small pieces of Tin. \u00a0\u00a0 some of our party also purchased from those Indians 2 dogs for trifles\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Those Indians had a number of fine horses, which we saw feeding along the Shore.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The day proved warm. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on but a short distance down the River, and came to another Indian camp; This Camp was situated at the lower end of an Island, where lay some rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 We halted here for a short time, & purchased some more Salmon and white bread Roots.\u2014

\n

\nThese Indians were also belonging to the flatt head Nation.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on and went but a short distance, and took in the Indians that set out this morning by land. \u00a0\u00a0 we then continued on our Voyage, and as we were descending a rocky rapid at the foot of an Island on which were some Indian camps, One of our Canoes struck a rock, and wheeled round, where she again Struck \u2329a\u232a another rock and Cracked the bottom of it, & was near splitting in two; & threw the Man who was steering her overboard, but he with great difficulty got to her again\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 This Canoe soon filled with water & hung on the rock in a perilous situation. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the Men on board of her, could not swim; & those Men that could, had no chance of saving themselves, the Waves ran so high, and the current was so rapid, that they must have been dashed against Rocks that lay below them a short distance & in all probability must have drownded.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 It was very fortunate for those Men, that an Indian who saw their situation from the Island we passed last, came to their assistance with a small Canoe & One of our Canoes went also, & took out some of the loading & landed it safe on the Shore.\u2014

\n

\nOur officers had one of the other Canoes unloaded and the Men went, and took out the remainder of the loading from on board that Canoes, which had been stove; and got it all on the Shore below the rapid, They experienced much difficulty in unloading the Canoe in the Rapids, the Current being so very Swift & strong. \u00a0\u00a0 We found the whole of the loading got out of that Canoe, wet & we lost some small Articles of the loading.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Our party were all rejoiced at the fortunate escape that the Men made that were in the Canoe, & think that nothing but the Interference of providence was the occasion. \u00a0\u00a0 In the Evening a number of the Natives came to Visit us, & behaved with a great deal of friendship to us all, they continued some time with us & took a friendly leave & departed for their Camps. \u00a0\u00a0 These Indians also belonged to the Flatt-head Nation.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came about 18 Miles this day before the accident happen'd to the Canoe,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 & encamped where we unloaded the Canoes with the baggage.\u2014\n\n\n\n

1. \"Colters Creek\" on Atlas map 72, after John Colter of the party, now Potlatch River in Nez Perce County, Idaho. In 1899 a Jefferson peace medal, perhaps from the expedition, was discovered near the mouth of Potlatch River. It appears that the peace medal, supposedly at the American Museum of Natural History, New York City, has been lost for some time. Wheeler, 2:123\u201324; Cutright (LCIPM), 164\u201365. (back)
\n
2. They remained at this site until October 10, as indicated on Atlas map 72. It was on the north side in Nez Perce County, below the confluence of the Potlatch and Clearwater rivers, a few miles from present Spalding. The party's camp was near Arrowbeach site which is located a little over half a mile below the mouth of Potlatch River. The site was excavated in the late 1960s, but it is now essentially destroyed. While the site occupation spanned at least the last three thousand years, it was probably a seasonal village during the last fifteen hundred years. Toups; Ames. (back)
\n
3. This camp, where they stayed until October 10, is on the north side of the Clearwater, Nez Perce County, Idaho, below the confluence of the Potlatch and Clearwater rivers, a few miles from Spalding. (back)

4. \n Twisted Hair and Tetoharsky. (back)

5. Perhaps Thompson, who Clark says was \"a little hurt.\" (back)
