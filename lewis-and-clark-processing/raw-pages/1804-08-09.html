\n9th Augt Thursday 1804 [3] \u00a0\u00a0 The fog of this morning detained us untill \u00bd passed 7 oClock at which time we left our moreing and proceeded on under a gentle Breeze from the S. [Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 9th\n [6] \u00a0\u00a0 a foggy morning, which detained us till past 7 oClock at which time we Set out under a gentle Breeze from S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed Round Several points of high wood land \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark & Sgt. Floyd went out hunting on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 came 11 miles by 12 o.C. where the River had formerly Cut across a bend \u00a0\u00a0 Said to be 15 mes. Round & a very Short distance a cross where it had Broke through a narrow Stripe of woods on each side of the River, the old Channel in the above mentiond bend is ponds & Islands. \u00a0\u00a0 the hills are a Great distance from the River this Several days. \u00a0\u00a0 the land on the River is low chiefly covered with Cottenwood & Grape vines &.C. \u00a0\u00a0 the Grapes are verry pleanty on the River for this Several days. Capt. Clark killed a Turkey & Joined us towards evening. \u00a0\u00a0 we camped on S. S. of the River \u00a0\u00a0 The Musquetoes more troublesome than ever.\u2014\n\n

\n \n

\nThursday augt the 9th \u00a0\u00a0 Set out at 7 oclocks a, m, \u2329we could see about us the\u232a after the fague was Gon which is verry thick in this Cuntrey \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark and my Self went out on the South side \u00a0\u00a0 passed a verry Bad place in the River whare the water is verry Shellow \u00a0\u00a0 mad 17 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Campd. on the South Side at prarie\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 9th. \u00a0\u00a0 The fog was so thick this morning, that we could not proceed before 7, when we went on under a gentle breeze, and having advanced eleven miles, came to a place where the river by cutting through a narrow neck of land, reduced the distance fifteen miles. Captain Clarke and one of the men went out to hunt and killed a small turkey. [7] We encamped on the south side, where we found the musquetoes very troublesome.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\nThursday Augt 9th \u00a0\u00a0 the Morning was foggy \u00a0\u00a0 Cleard Up at 8 Oclock \u00a0\u00a0 the Wind blew south \u00a0\u00a0 had Good Sailing for better than 14 Miles\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Camped On the E. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Roaed & Saild 20 miles

\n

\nThursday August 9th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we set out in a fog which cleared up at 8 oClock A. M. \u00a0\u00a0 the Wind blew from the South; we set sail & went 14 Miles, when the wind died away, we then took to our Oars and rowed. \u00a0\u00a0 in the evening we encamped on the North [8] side of the River having rowed & Sailed 20 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n
