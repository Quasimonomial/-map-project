\n May 7, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Tuesday May 7th 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nA fine morning, set out at an early hour; the drift wood begins to come down in consequence of the river's rising; the water is somewhat clearer than usual, a circumstance I did not expect on it's rise. \u00a0\u00a0 at 11 A. M. the wind became so hard that we were compelled to ly by for several hours, one of the small canoes by the bad management of the steersman filled with water and had very nearly sunk; we unloaded her and dryed the baggage; at one we proceed on the wind having in some measure abated. \u00a0\u00a0 the country we passed today on the North side of the river is one of the most beautifull plains we have yet seen, it rises gradually from the river bottom to the hight of 50 or 60 feet, then becoming level as a bowling green. \u00a0\u00a0 extends back as far as the eye can reach; on the S. side the river hills are more broken and much higher tho' some little destance back the country becomes level and fertile. \u00a0\u00a0 no appearance of birnt hills coal or pumicestone, that of salts still continue. [1] \u00a0\u00a0 vegitation appears to have advanced very little since the 28th Ulto.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we continue to see a great number of bald Eagles, I presume they must feed on the carcases of dead anamals, for I see no fishing hawks [2] to supply them with their favorite food. \u00a0\u00a0 the water of the river is so terbid that no bird wich feeds exclusively on fish can subsist on it; from it's mouth to this place I have neither seen the blue crested fisher [3] nor a fishing hawk. \u00a0\u00a0 this day we killed 3 Buffaloe 1 Elk & 8 beaver; two of the Buffaloe killed by Capt Clark near our encampment [4] of this evening wer in good order dressed them and saved the meat, the Elk I killed this morning, thought it fat, but on examineation found it so lean that we took the tongue marrowbones and Skin only.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 7th Tuesday, 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nA fine morning \u00a0\u00a0 river rose 1\u00bd Inches last night, the drift wood beginning to run \u00a0\u00a0 the water Something Clearer than usial, the wind became verry hard, and at 11 oClock one Canoe by bad Stearing filled with water, which detained us about 3 hours, had a Meridian altitude, the Latd. from which is 47\u00b0 36\u00b0 11\" 6/10 \u00a0\u00a0 The Countrey on the North Side of the Missouri is one of the handsomest plains we have yet Seen on the river \u00a0\u00a0 the plain rises from the river bottom gradually. The Hills on the South Side is high & uneavin. \u00a0\u00a0 no appearance of Coal or burnt hills, that of Salts Still appear; vegitation appears to be Slow, I walked on the bank to day and Shot 2 beaver, in the evening Killed two Buffalow in tolerable order which we Saved and Camped on the Lard Side. \u00a0\u00a0 8 beaver, 3 buffalow & an Elk killed to day

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 7th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 clear pleasant and warm. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind rose from the East. \u00a0\u00a0 we Sailed verry fast untill about 12 o.C. one of the canoes filled with water, but we got it Safe to Shore, and halted for the wind to abate at a bottom on s. s. Some men went out to hunt. \u00a0\u00a0 two beaver was caught last night by Some of the party, & shot five more at this place. \u00a0\u00a0 about 4 oClock we proceeded on as the wind had abated Some. Saw large gangs of buffaloe on each side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 handsom plains on the N. S. Capt. Clark and one hunter walked on Shore on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening they killed \n2 buffaloe in a bottom of timber on S. S. where we Camped for the night and dressed the 2 buffaloe. Came 15\u00bd miles this day,\n\n\n

\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 7th. \u00a0\u00a0 We again set out early and went on very well till 12 when it began to blow hard, and being all under sail one of our canoes turned over. Fortunately the accident happened near the shore; and after halting three \nhours we were able to go on again. Having this day made sixteen miles we encamped on the South side.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 7th May 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 clear and pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind rose from the East. \u00a0\u00a0 we Sailed on untill about 12 oC. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind rose So high that one of the cannoes filled with water \u00a0\u00a0 we got it Safe to Shore, and halted for the wind to abate on the S. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Some men went out to hunt. \u00a0\u00a0 two beaver was caught by Some of the men last night and Shot five [6] more at this \nplace. \u00a0\u00a0 about 4 oC. we Set off and proceeded on verry well. \u00a0\u00a0 passed handsom plains and bottoms on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 rough hills & ridges & bottoms on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw large gangs of buffaloe on each Side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 Came 15\u00bd miles to day, & Camped on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark and one hunter killed two buffaloe, which we found to be good meat to Eat.

\n

\nTuesday May 7th \u00a0\u00a0 This day the weather Clear & pleasant, we set off early, Shortly after the wind blew from the East, we set our Sails, and continued on, Sailing till about 12 oClock A. M. at which time the wind rose so high, that one of our Canoes filled with water, we got the Canoe to the shore, and stopped on the South shore for the wind to abate, some of the party caught \n2 Beaver during last night, a party of our Men went out to hunt. \u00a0\u00a0 They shot five more Beaver at this place. \u00a0\u00a0 About 4 oClock P. M. we set out again and proceeded on, and passed by some handsome plains; & bottoms lying on the North side, and Rough hills, ridges & bottoms on the South side of the River, We saw large Gangs of Buffalo as we passed on both sides of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 In the Evening we stopped and encamped on the South side of the River\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Clark who had went out hunting from the place we stopped at this day; returned to us here, with one of the hunters, they had killed 2 Buffalo, which was brought to our Camp, they were in good order and their flesh good eating.\u2014\n\n\n
