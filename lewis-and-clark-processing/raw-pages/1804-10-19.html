\n October 19, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n 19th of October Friday 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early under a gentle Breeze from the S.E. \u00a0\u00a0 more timber than Common in the bottoms \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Pond on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 I walked out on the high land L. Side and observed great numbers of Buffalows, I counted in view at one time 52 gangues of Buffalow & 3 of Elk, besides Deer & goats &c. \u00a0\u00a0 all the Streems falling from the hills or high lands So brackish that the water Can't be Drank without effecting the person making use of it as Globesalts\u2014, [1] I saw in my walk Several remarkable high Conocal hills, one 90 feet, one 60 and others Smaller\u2014 [2] \u00a0\u00a0 the Indian Chief Say that the Callemet Bird [3] live in the hollows of those hills, which holes are made by the water passing from the top & &. \u00a0\u00a0 I also Saw an old Village fortified Situated on the top of a high Point, which the Ricarra Chief tels me were Mandans, [4] we Camped on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 I Killed a Deer & Saw Swans &c. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters Killed 4 Elk and 6 Deer to Day

[Clark]

19th October Friday 1804.
\u00a0
\n \n

\na fine morning \u00a0\u00a0 wind \u2329hard\u232a from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out early under a gentle Breeze and proceeded on verry well, more timber than Common on the banks on this part of the river\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Pond on the S. S.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 I walked out on the Hills & observed Great numbers of Buffalow feedeing on both Sides of the river \u00a0\u00a0 I counted 52 Gangues of Buffalow & 3 of Elk at one view, all the runs which come from the high hills which is Generally about one or 2 miles from the water is brackish and near the Hills (the Salts are) [6] and the Sides of the Hills & edges of the Streems, the mineral salts appear \u00a0\u00a0 I saw Som remarkable round hills forming a Cone at top one about 90 foot one 60 & Several others Smaller, the Indian Chief Say that the Callemet bird live in the holes of those hills, the holes form by the water washing thro Some parts in its passage Down from the top\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 near one of those noles, on a point of a hill 90 feet above the lower plane I observed the remains of an old village, [NB: high, strong, watchtower &c.] which had been fortified, the Indian Chief with us tels me, a party of Mandins lived there, Here first saw ruins of Mandan nation \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on & Camped on the L. S. [7] opposit the upper of those Conocal hills\u2014

\n

\nour hunters killed 4 Elk 6 Deer & a pelican, I saw Swans in a Pond & Killed a fat Deer in my walk, Saw above 10 wolves. This day is pleasent

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 19th Oct. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear & pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men out hunting on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 a gentle breeze from the South \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Timbred bottom on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 2 men went out hunting on N. S. & in a fiew minutes killed an Elk. \u00a0\u00a0 we took it on board & proceeded on untill abt. 1 o.C. when we halted for dinner on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the hunters that went out this morning joined us \u00a0\u00a0 had killed 2 Elk. Capt. Clark & 2 more hunters went out hunting on S. S. Sailed on. Saw an abundance of droves of Buffaloe on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passd. a Bottom on N. S. where we took on board 2 of the hunters who had killed 3 deer\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening our hunters all joined us. \u00a0\u00a0 had killed 4 more Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 the Skins were all Given out to the party &.C.\n\n\n\n

\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 19th. \u00a0\u00a0 Early this morning we renewed our voyage, having a clear day and a fair wind: passed a creek on the south side. While out hunting yesterday I saw about three hundred goats, and some buffaloe. Deer are not so plenty here as lower down the river; but elk, buffaloe and goats, are very numerous. Four hunters went out to day and in the evening returned with 7 deer and three elk. We encamped on the north side.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday October 19th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning being clear, we sent our Hunters out, and proceeded on our Voyage with a fair Wind; we passed a Creek, lying on the South side of the River, as we passed along we saw a great quantity of \nBuffalo, Elk, and deer in gangs, We came too in the Evening, and encamped on the North side of the River, our hunters returned, and brought with them 3 Elk & 7 deer\u2014which they had killed this day\n\n

1. Glauber's Salts, the decahydrate form of sodium sulfate, used as a laxative. (back)
\n
2. They appear on Atlas map 28 in Morton County, North Dakota; the highest is probably the \"Sugarloaf\" of Evans's map 5 (Atlas map 11), present Sugarloaf Butte. MRC map 49; MRY map 133. (back)
\n
3. The golden eagle. (back)
\n
4. The village may be the Eagle Nose site set atop a flat-topped eminence known today as Eagle Nose Butte or Birds Bill Hill, in Morton County. It figures prominently in Mandan folklore. Atlas map 28 calls it a \"hunting camp fortifed on a hill.\" Mattison (OR), 148; Will, 313; Bowers (MSCO), 162, 197 199, 252; MRC map 49. The location of the camp is discussed in n. 7. (back)
\n
5. The location of the camp is discussed in n. 7. The distance is 3 \u00bd in Codex C. (back)
\n
6. We have changed Clark's square brackets around this passage to parentheses. (back)
\n
7. The letters are uncertain in Codex C, but are clearly \"L. S.\" in the Field Notes. Atlas map 28 seems to indicate a campsite a little above the upper hill; aside from possible error by the map copyist, Clark's use of the word \"opposite\" is not always precise. The camp would be in Morton County, a few miles upstream from the village of Huff; it is now inundated by Oahe Reservoir. MRC map 49. (back)
