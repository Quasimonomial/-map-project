[Clark]

\n September 2nd Monday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na Cloudy Mornin, raind Some last night \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early and proceeded on up the [EC: Fish\n ] Creek, Crossed a large fork from the right and one from the left; and at 8 [EC: 7\u00bd] miles left the roade on which we were pursuing and which leads over to the Missouri; and proceeded up a West fork [EC: of Fish Creek\n ] without a roade proceded on thro' thickets in which we were obliged to Cut a road, over rockey hill Sides where our horss were in pitial [1] danger of Slipping to Ther certain distruction & up & Down Steep hills, where Several horses fell, Some turned over, and others Sliped down Steep hill Sides, one horse Crippeled & 2 gave out. with the greatest dificuelty risque &c. we made five [EC: 7\u00bd] miles & Encamped on The left Side of the Creek in a Small Stoney bottom [2] \u00a0\u00a0 after night Some time before the rear Came up, one Load left, about 2 miles back, the horse on which it was Carried Crippled. Some rain at night

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 2nd Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a cloudy wet morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded our horses and Set out about 7 oClock, and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 the way we had to go was verry bad \u00a0\u00a0 Some places thick bushes and logs to pass over. \u00a0\u00a0 other places rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 our course about N. E. \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several creeks or large Spring runs the water of which was verry cold. Saw a nomber of large beaver dams and beaver ponds on this creek. \u00a0\u00a0 the pine and balsom fir timber tall and Strait, and pleanty. Some of the pine is large enofe for canoes or boards &C. \u00a0\u00a0 considerable of common alder along the creeks and runs. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on up the creek \u00a0\u00a0 passed through verry bad thickets where we were oblidged to cut a road for our horses to pass through. \u00a0\u00a0 handsom tall strait pine and balsom fir and a little cotton intermixed. \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several branches. Some places muddy. \u00a0\u00a0 we call this place dismal Swamp. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 Several beaver dams in it &C. Some places Steep along the edge of the mountains and verry rough and rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 the mountains make close on each Side of the creek and high covred with pine. \u00a0\u00a0 this is a verry lonesome place. Some of our weak horses fell backward climeing the Steep rockey hills, and mountains. \u00a0\u00a0 we had considerable of trouble this day carring Several of the horses loads up the steep rockey mont. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the horses gave out So that he could not carry his load. \u00a0\u00a0 we went 13 miles this day and Camped [4] in a thicket near the creek. \u00a0\u00a0 it was about six miles through the thicket which we call the dismal Swamp. \u00a0\u00a0 nothing killed this day by the hunters only a fiew fessents. [5] \u00a0\u00a0 no game of any kind to be Seen in these mountains.\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 2nd. \u00a0\u00a0 The morning was cloudy. We set out early; proceeded up the creek, and passed some part closely timbered with spruce and pine. We went on with difficulty on account of the bushes, the narrowness of the way \nand stones that injured our horses feet, they being without shoes. In the forenoon we killed some pheasants [6] and ducks, and a small squirrel. In the afternoon we had a good deal of rain, and the worst road (if road it can be called) that was ever travelled. The creek is become small and the hills come close in upon the banks of it, covered thick with standing timber and fallen trees; so that in some places we were obliged to go up the sides of the hills, which are very steep, and then down again in order to get along at all. In going up these ascents the horses would sometimes fall backwards, which injured them very much; and one was so badly hurt that the driver was obliged to leave his load on the side of one of the hills. In the low ground there are most beautiful tall straight pine trees of different kinds, except of white pine. [7] Game is scarce; and a small quantity of dried salmon, which we got from the natives is almost our whole stock of provisions. A son of our guide joined us to day and is going on. We went 13 miles and encamped; [8] but some of the men did not come up till late at night.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 2nd Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a wet cloudy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded our horses and Set out about 7 oClock and proced on. \u00a0\u00a0 the road bad \u00a0\u00a0 Some places thick bushes and [logs?] to cross. \u00a0\u00a0 other places rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 went about a N. E. course up the abo. ment. [9] Creek. \u00a0\u00a0 Crossed Several large Spring runs. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw a nomber of large beaver dams & ponds \u00a0\u00a0 the pine and bolsom fer timber verry pleanty and thick up this Creek \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the Pine is large enofe for boards [several words illegible] \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on through a bad thicket of tall Strait pitch pine bolsom fer & cotton timber \u00a0\u00a0 we were obledged to cut a road for the horses to go and some places verry Steep and rockey. \u00a0\u00a0 we followed the creek up, crossed a nomber of fine Spring branches and waided the creek a nomber of times. \u00a0\u00a0 the mountains on each Side of the Creek is verry Steep and high. \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms on the Creek narrow and Swampy a nomber of beaver dams. \u00a0\u00a0 we Call this place dismal Swamp, and it is a lonesom rough part of the Country. \u00a0\u00a0 we were obledged to climb Several hills with our horses, where it was So Steep and rockey that Some of the horses \nwhich was weak and their feet Sore, that they fell back \u00a0\u00a0 3 or 4 fell over backwards and roled to the foot of the hills. \u00a0\u00a0 we were then obledged to carry the loads up the hills and then load again. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the horses gave out So that his load was left a little before night. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came 13 miles this day and Camped in a thicket of pine and bolsom fir timber near the Creek. \u00a0\u00a0 2 of the men came up with their horses and loads after dark. \u00a0\u00a0 this horrid bad going where we came up this creek which we Call dismal Swamp was six miles and we are not out of it yet, but our guide tells us that we will git on a plain tomorrow. \u00a0\u00a0 Several fessons killed this day, but no other kind of game Seen by our hunters.\u2014

\n

\nMonday September 2nd \u00a0\u00a0 A cloudy wet morning, We set out with our horses & proceeded on our Journey, and about 7 o'Clock A. M. we passed some very bad Roads, some being plains with very thick bushes, and low slashes to cross, other places, very rockey, we went a North east course up the Creek above mention'd. \u00a0\u00a0 We recrossed the same Creek near a large spring run,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 where we saw a number of high beaver dams. \u00a0\u00a0 We saw also Pitch pine & Balsam fir Trees in great abundance, on the Tops of the Mountains, We passed through a bad thicket of tall strait Pitch pine, balsam fir, & Cotton wood Timber \u2329so\u232a which grew so close to each other that we were obliged to cut a road for our horses to pass through.\u2014

\n

\nSome of those places were very steep & Rockey.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We continued our course up the side of the Creek already mentioned this day, and crossed a number of fine Spring branches, and waided in the Creek a number of times. \u00a0\u00a0 The Mountains that lies on both sides of this Creek are very steep & high; the bottoms on this Creek narrow & swampey, And had a vast number of beaver dams, on them \u00a0\u00a0 We named this place Dismal swamp, & it was certainly a lonesome rough looking part of the Country.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We were obliged to Climb several hills with our horses, where the hills was so steep & rockey, \u2329which\u232a some of the horses that was weak & had sore feet fell backward with their loads; and rolled down to the foot of those hills, we were obliged to carry the loads of our horses, on our backs up many of the hills, & then load them again. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our horses gave out, so that we were obliged to leave his load.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came about 13 Miles this day, & encamped in a thicket of pine trees, and balsam fir timber; near the Creek.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Two of our Men came up with their horses & loads after it was dark.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came about \n6 Miles through this bad road, all of which way was very bad travelling, and we are informed by our guide, that we have still further to go, before we get to the plains, which he says will be tomorrow, Our Hunters killed several Pheasants this day, but saw no other kind of Game.\u2014\n\n\n\n
