\n June 3, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
June Sunday 3rd 1804 [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nthe fore part of the day fair \u00a0\u00a0 I attempted to take equal alltitudes, & M[eridian] Altitudes, but was disapointed, the Clouds obscured the Sun, took the D. of \u2609 & \u263d : Capt Lewis & George Drewyer went out & Killed a Deer, We Set out at 5 oClock P M \u00a0\u00a0 Cloudy & rain, West 5 Ms. to the mo. of Murrow Creek\n [2] Lb Sd. \u00a0\u00a0 a pt. St. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Keeping along the Lbd Side 1 Ms., passed the mouth of a Creek on Lbd Side 3 ms., I call Cupboard, Creek, [3] mouths behind a rock which projects into the river, Camped in the mouth of the Creek aforesaid [Moreau River], at the mouth of this Creek I saw much fresh Signs of Indians, haveing Crossed \u00a0\u00a0 2 Deer Killed to day. I have a verry Sore Throat, \u2329great\u232a & am Tormented with Musquetors & Small ticks.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n June 3rd Sunday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe forepart of the day fair \u00a0\u00a0 Took meridional altitude of \u2609's U: L with the Octant and Glass Horrison adjusted back observation. \u00a0\u00a0 the instrument gave 38\u00b0 2' 00\"\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 it was Cloudy and the Suns disk much obsured, and Cannot be Depended on.

\n

\nWe made other Observations in the evening after the return of Capt Lewis from a walk of three or four ms. round\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We Set out at 5 oClock P. M. proceeded on five miles to the mouth of a Creek on the L. S. 20 yds. wide Called Murow, passed a Creek at 3 ms. which I call \n Cupbord Creek as it Mouths above a rock of that appearance. Several Deer Killed to day\u2014 at the mouth of the Murow Creek I Saw much Sign of war parties of Inds. haveing Crossed from the mouth of this Creek. I have a bad Cold with a Sore throat. Near West 5 Miles\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday June 3d 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we lay at the osage R. the four part of the Day. \u00a0\u00a0 the Hunters [5] killed five Deer while we delayed at this place. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at 5 oC. in the afternoon and proceeded along the Clifts. \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped at Morow Creek on S. Side.

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday June 3d 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out at 4 oclock P. m \u00a0\u00a0 the for part of the day Clear \u00a0\u00a0 the Latter part Clouday with thunder and Rain wind from Est. Capt Lewis and G. Drureay\n [6] went hunting \u00a0\u00a0 Kild one Deer & Grown hog \u00a0\u00a0 4 miles to River murrow on the South Saide \u00a0\u00a0 it is about 30 yardes wide and High Cliftes on the Loer Side of it \u00a0\u00a0 3 hundrered yardes up the River Cliftes \u00a0\u00a0 encamped at the mouth on the South Side \u00a0\u00a0 ouer hunters Kild one Deer\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 3rd. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis, with one of the men went out and killed a deer. At five in the afternoon we embarked, and having proceeded six miles, encamped at the mouth of Marrow creek on the south side.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday 3rd June 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 Several men out a hunting. \u00a0\u00a0 our officers takes observations &c.\u2014 [7]\n

\n

\nSunday June 3rd \u00a0\u00a0 This day being clear, several of the Men was sent out a hunting; Captain Lewis & Captain Clark observ'd, and found the Mouth of \nthe Grand Osage River, to be in Latitude 38\u00b0 31 6 North.\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. Biddle placed the heading \"June 3 to 5th\" at the top of document 17 in the Field Notes. (back)
\n
2. The Moreau River, the party's camp for the night, enters the Missouri in Cole County, Missouri, just east of Jefferson City, the present state capital. It is probably named for one of several Frenchmen named Moreau residing in Missouri. Fig. 14, a sketch map on the reverse of document 18 of the Field Notes, appears to show the Missouri upstream from the Osage River, without naming most of the streams. MRC map 6; McDermott (WCS), 147. (back)
\n
3. Apparently Rising Creek, in Cole County, some two miles east of the mouth of Moreau River. MRC map 6. (back)
\n
4. \n Lewis's observation from Codex O. (back)
\n
5. Including Lewis and Drouillard, according to Clark. (back)
\n
6. \n Drouillard. (back)
\n
7. \n Whitehouse fails to mention that the party moved several miles upstream this day. They encamped at Moreau River, Cole County, Missouri, east of Jefferson City. (back)
