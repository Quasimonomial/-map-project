\n June 8, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n June 8th Friday [1]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out at Daylight \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on the Course of last night S 87\u00b0 W 3 ms \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Willow Island, from the Point of last Course S 81\u00b0 W. 3 ms. to a pt. on S. S: passd a [blank] Isd. in the middle of the river, passd a run [2] on the Ld S: above a pt. of rocks 3 ms. on which thir is a number of Deer Licks, N 88\u00b0 W. 3 Ms. to a pt L S: N. 83\u00b0 W 2 ms. to the Mo of Mine River, [3] psd an Isd.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 This river is 90 yards wide & navagable for Perogues about 90 Ms. \u00a0\u00a0 I went out on the L S. about 4 ms. below this R. and found the Countrey for one mile back good Land and well watered \u00a0\u00a0 the hills not high with a gentle assent from the river, well timbered with oake, walnit Hickory ash, &c. \u00a0\u00a0 the land Still further back becoms thin and open, with Black & rasp Berries, [4] and Still further back the Plains Commence, The french inform that Lead ore is found on this river in Several places, it heads up between the Osagees & Kansas River the right hand folk [fork] passes in a Short distance of the Missourie at the antient Little Ozages Village [5] \u00a0\u00a0 our hunter [6] Killed, 2 Deer, after Stayingd one hour at the mouth of this River, Cap Lewis went out & proceede on one Mile & came in, he fount the land in the point high and fine \u00a0\u00a0 Course N. 64\u00b0 W 1 Ms. to a pt. on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 N. 80\u00b0 W to the Lower pot a Id. on L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a \u2329large\u232a Small Isd. in the m: R. at (3 Ms.) \u00a0\u00a0 met 3 men on a Caussee from R Dis Soux, [7] above The Mahar\n [Omaha] Nation loaded with fur. \u2329N 39 W 3\u00bd Ms. to pt on S. S. opsd. a Prarie\u232a \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the Lower point of an Id. L. S. called Rock Island the Mills, [8] here I found Kegs an Pummey [pumice] stone, and a place that fur or Skins had been burred [buried] by the hunters \u00a0\u00a0 our Hunters Killed 5 Deer, Some rain, the Countrey on the S. S. is Verry fine\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
8th of June, Friday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out this morning at Daylight \u00a0\u00a0 proceeden on the Course of last night \u00a0\u00a0 Passed two willow Islands & a Small Creek above a Rock point on the L. S. at 6 miles on which there is a number of Deer Licks, passed the \n Mine River at 9 ms. \u00a0\u00a0 this river is about 70 yards wide at its mouth and is Said to be navagable for Perogues 80 or 90 ms. \u00a0\u00a0 the \u2329main\u232a [NB: West] [9] branch passes near the place where the Little osage Village formerly Stood on the Missouries, & heads between the Osarge & Kansias Rivers, the left hand fork [10] head with nearer Branches of the Osage River, The french inform that Lead Ore has been found in defferent parts of this river, I took Sjt. Floyd and went out 4 Ms. below this river, I found the land Verry good for a Mile or 1 \u00bd Ms. back and Sufficiently watered with Small Streams which lost themselves in the Missouries bottom, the Land rose gradeuelly from the river to the Summit of the high Countrey which is not more that 120 foot above High Water mark, we joined the Boat & Dined in the point above the mouth of this River, Capt. Lewis went out above the river & proceeded on one mile, finding the Countrey rich, the wedes & Vines So thick & high he came to the Boat\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on passed an Island \u2329about\u232a and Camped at the lower point of an Island on the L. S. Called the Island of mills [NB: mills] about 4 ms. above Mine River \u00a0\u00a0 at this place I found Kanteens, Axs, Pumey Stone & peltrey hid & buried (I suppose by some hunters) none of them (except the pumey Stone) was teched by one of our party, our hunters Killed 5 Deer to day, Commenced raining Soon after we Came too which prevented the party Cooking their provisions\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 our Spies inform that the Countrey they passed thro: on S. S. is a fine high bottom, no water.

\nThis day we met 3 men on a Cajaux from the River of the Soux above the \n Mahar nation \u00a0\u00a0 those men had been hunting 12 mo: & made about 900$ in pelts. & furs \u00a0\u00a0 they were out of Provesions and out of Powder. \u00a0\u00a0 rained this night\n\n\t\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday June 8th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at 4. OC. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a run on left Side above a point of rocks on 3 miles on which their is a nomber of Deer licks, 3 miles to the mo. of mine River, this River is navagable for Perogues\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we met 3 Frenchman in 2 canoes from R. des Soud above this Nation loaded with fur. \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped on the Lower point of an Island called mills Island. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters killed 5 Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 Some rain \u00a0\u00a0 the Country on the right is verry fine\u2014

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n\nFriday June 8th \u00a0\u00a0 Set out erley this morning \u00a0\u00a0 the day Clear wind from the west \u00a0\u00a0 Came 5 miles \u00a0\u00a0 past 2 Canoes Lasht to Gather Loaded with Bever Skins otter Skins from the Littel River\n [11] \u00a0\u00a0 mean thay ar 30 day coming from that place 5 miles past the mouth of the Big River mine \u00a0\u00a0 it is about 100 and 50 yardes wide a butifull \u2329S\u232a River on the South Side \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Good first Rate Land well timberd \u00a0\u00a0 this River is navagbl for Som hundreds miles aperintley \u00a0\u00a0 water Strong \u00a0\u00a0 past Several Isd. Came 10 miles \u00a0\u00a0 ouer hunters Kild 5 Deer \u00a0\u00a0 encamped on \u2329an\u232a the Loer pint of an Isd and on the South Side of the River\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 8th. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked and proceeded five miles, when we met four canoes loaded with fur and peltry: and passed the Mine river on the south side, which is 150 yards wide. The land here is also good and well timbered.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 8th \u00a0\u00a0 Got on our way at the usal hour \u00a0\u00a0 had strong Watter to Goe throug \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329We had\u232a the white pierogue had hard Crossing the River to bring the Meat from the hunters, Druyer Killd 5 deer before 12 Oclock. \u00a0\u00a0 met two Connooes loaded with furr from the Zotte River\n [12] neer the Mandens. \u00a0\u00a0 Got to the \u2329imun\u232a Amens River\n [13] at three Oclock but did not remain at it any times \u00a0\u00a0 Roed. 16 mile

\n

\nThursday June 8th \u00a0\u00a0 We started early this Morning, and found the current of the River very strong against us, we sent a Pettiauger to bring the bear meat to the boat, but the hands had very great difficulty in crossing the River to us. \u00a0\u00a0 The hunters being left, they proceeded a hunting; and one of them (G Drewer) killed five deer by 12 oClock A. M. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on and met with Two canoes loaded with Furr, which had come from Oto River, near the Zotto\n [14] nation. \u00a0\u00a0 At 3oClock P. M. got to the River L'mine or lead River and remained there but a short time. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on & encamped on the North side of the River Mesouri, the distance come this day being 16 Miles\n\n\n

3. The Lamine River reaches the Missouri River in Cooper County. The name is thought to derive from the report in 1714 by Sieur de Bourgmont that the Indians mined leads on its banks. See fig. 14. Missouri Guide, 359; MRC map 9. (back)

4. Probably Rubus occidentalis L., black raspberry, one of the most common raspberries in Missouri. A number of other Rubus species, however, blackberry and raspberry, also occur along the Missouri River. Steyermark, 835\u201336. (back)
\n
5. The fork is the Blackwater River, which meets the Lamine in northwest Cooper County. The Little Osage village on the Missouri River is identified as the Plattner site, which is about three miles northeast of the town of Malta Bend, in Saline County. It was occupied in the mid-eighteenth century. Chapman (AM), 6\u201338. (back)
