\n September 8, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n September 8th Sunday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early and proceeded on through an open vallie for 23 miles \u00a0\u00a0 passed 4 Creeks [1] on the right Some runs on the left, The bottoms as also the hills Stoney bad land. Some pine on the Creeks and mountains, an partial on the hills to the right hand Side. \u00a0\u00a0 two of our hunters came up with us at 12 oClock with an Elk, & Buck\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the wind from the N. W. & Cold. The foot of the Snow mountains approach the \u2329Creek\u232a River on the left Side. Some Snow on The mountain to the right also [2] \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on down the Vallie which is pore Stoney land and encamped on the right Side of the river [3] \u00a0\u00a0 a hard rain all the evening we are all Cold and wet. \u00a0\u00a0 on this part of the river on the head of Clarks River I observe great quantities of a peculiar Sort of Prickly peare [4] grow in Clusters ovel & about the Size of a Pigions egge with Strong Thorns which is So birded [bearded] as to draw the Pear from the Cluster after penetrateing our feet. Drewyer killed a Deer. I killed a prarie fowl \u00a0\u00a0 we found 2 mears and a Colt \u00a0\u00a0 the mears were lame, we ventered to let our late purchase of horses loose to night.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSunday 8th Sept 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy and cold. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly and proceeded on down this creek which is now verry large. \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Smooth plain \u00a0\u00a0 no timber except along the bank of the creeks. Saw Snow on the mount to our left. \u00a0\u00a0 high barron hills to our right. [5] \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 oClock we halted to dine at a branch \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters joined us. \u00a0\u00a0 had killed an Elk, and a Deer which they brought with them. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind cold from the N. W. & Showers of rain, and a little hail. \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Smooth plains in this valley. \u00a0\u00a0 the Mountains are rough on each side and are covred with pine and on the tops of which are covd. with Snow. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters found 2 Stray horses and a handsom colt, which they took along with them \u00a0\u00a0 we marched 20 odd miles this day and Camped [6] on a Smooth bottom near the creek, where we had fine feed for our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters joined us \u00a0\u00a0 had killed one Deer.\u2014\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 8th. \u00a0\u00a0 The morning was wet, and we proceeded on over some beautiful plains. One of our hunters had remained out all night, at noon we halted and they all came in, having killed an elk and a deer. At 2 we proceeded on again, and had a cold, wet and disagreeable afternoon, but our road or way was level along the valley. Having travelled 20 miles, we encamped and our hunters came in, one [7] of whom had killed a deer, and another had caught two mares and a colt, which he brought with him.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 8th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy and verry chilley and cold. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly and proceeded on down this large creek or Small River. \u00a0\u00a0 passed over Smooth dry plains. \u00a0\u00a0 no timber only along the River, which is large pitch pine \u00a0\u00a0 the bottoms wide. \u00a0\u00a0 we crossed Several creeks. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Snow on the Mountains to our left. \u00a0\u00a0 high barron hills to our right. \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 oClock we halted to dine at a branch \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters all joined us. \u00a0\u00a0 had killed an Elk & and a Deer, Which they brought with them. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind from N. W. chilley and cold. \u00a0\u00a0 the Snow lays thick on the mot. a little to our left. \u00a0\u00a0 we delayed and let our horses feed about 2 hours, and proceeded on down the valley. \u00a0\u00a0 had Several Small cold Showers of rain & a little hail. \u00a0\u00a0 passed over level Smooth plains in this valley. \u00a0\u00a0 the Mountains are rough on each Side and are covered with pine and the tops of which are covered with Snow. \u00a0\u00a0 Some \nplaces appear to lay thick. \u00a0\u00a0 one of our hunters found & caught 2 horses and a handsom Colt. \u00a0\u00a0 we take them along with us though the horses are lame. \u00a0\u00a0 we expect that to be the reason that the natives left them in these bottoms. \u00a0\u00a0 we travveled 20 odd miles this day and Camped at the Creek and Smooth bottom where was fine feed for our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters all joined us \u00a0\u00a0 one of them had killed a Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 crossed Several branches in course of the day.\u2014

\n

\nSunday Septemr. 8th \u00a0\u00a0 A cold chilly Cloudy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out early and proceeded on our Journey.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We went down this large Creek or small River, we passed over smooth dry plains, but no timber to be seen excepting what grew along the Creek, which were large Pitch Pine trees.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The bottom land along this large Creek were wide & the Soil very rich.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We crossed several small Creeks, which made into the large Creek which we left this morning.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We saw Snow lying on Mountains, which lay to the South of us, & high Barren hills, which lay to the North likewise of us.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 About 11 o'Clock A. M. we halted to dine at a Creek.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Our hunters all joined us here, they had killed an Elk & one Deer, which they brought to us. \u00a0\u00a0 The \nWind has been from the N West during this day & the Air chilly & Cold.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Snow still continues on the Mountains, a small distance to the South of us. \u00a0\u00a0 We delayed about 2 hours to let our horses feed, we then proceeded on down the Valley & had several small showers of rain accompanied with hail.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We passed over level smooth plains in this Valley, the Mountains on each side of which, are rough, and are cover'd with Pine Trees, the Tops of which are Covered with Snow & the snow appears to lay thick in many places on these Mountains.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our hunters came across 2 Strange horses & a Colt, which he caught.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We took those horses & Colt along with us, the horses were lame & we expected that the Natives had left them in this Valley, on that account. \u00a0\u00a0 We came 25 Miles this day as near as we can guess, and encamped at a Creek, on a smooth bottom of land. \u00a0\u00a0 We found here fine food for our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 Our hunters joined us in the Evening. \u00a0\u00a0 They had killed only one deer which they brought to us.\u2014\n\n\n
