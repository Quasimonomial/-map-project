\n Course & Distance &c. Sept. 13th 1805\n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
S. W.\u00a0\u00a02miles up the Said Creek through an emencely bad road,
\nrocks, Steep hill sides & fallen timber inumerable \u00a0\u00a0 The
\nSnow toped mountains at a long distance from S W to S E
\nnone else to be Seen in any other Directions to hot Springs
\non the right. Those springs come out in maney places in the
\nrocks and nearly boiling hot
S. 30\u00b0 W.\u00a0\u00a03miles to the creek \u00a0\u00a0 passed a round about of 3 miles to our
\nleft of intolerable road timber &c as usial \u00a0\u00a0 halted to noon it
\n& wate for Capt. Lewis who lost his horse
S. 30\u00b0 W.\n \u00a0\u00a07\n miles over a mountain & a Dividing ridge of flat gradey land
\nto a Creek from the left passing thro a glade of \u00bd a mile in
\nwidth, keeping down the Creek 2 mile & Encamped. The
\nCountry as usial except the Glades which is open & boggey,
\nwater Clare and Sandey. Snow toped Mountains to the S E. at
\nthe head of this Creek which we call [blank] Creek. The after
\npart of the day Cloudy. I killed 4 Pheasents & Shields killed
\na Black tail Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 a horse found in the glades left lame by
\nSome Indians &c.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n September 13th Wednesday [NB: Friday] 1805.
\u00a0
\n \n

\t \t\na cloud morning \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis and one of our guides lost their horses, Capt Lewis & 4 men detained to hunt the horses, I proceeded on with the partey up the Creek at 2 miles passed Several Springs which I observed the Deer Elk &c. had made roads to, and below one of the Indians had made a whole to bathe, I tasted this water and found it hot & not bad tasted \u00a0\u00a0 The last [blank] \u00a0\u00a0 in further examonation I found this water nearly boiling hot at the places it Spouted from the rocks (which a hard Corse Grit, and of great size the rocks on the Side of the Mountain of the Same texture[)] \u00a0\u00a0 I put my finger in the water, at first could not bare it in a Second\u2014 [2] \u00a0\u00a0 as Several roads led from these Springs in different derections, my Guide took a wrong road and took us out of our rout 3 miles through intolerable rout, after falling into the right road I proceeded on thro tolerabl rout for abt. 4 or 5 miles and halted to let our horses graze as well as waite for Capt Lewis who has not yet Come up, The pine Countrey falling timber &c. &c. Continue. This Creek is verry much damed up with the beaver, but we can See none, dispatched two men back to hunt Capt Lewis horse, after he came up, and we proceeded over a mountain to the head of the Creek which we left to our left and at 6 miles from the place I nooned it, we fell on a Small Creek from the left which Passed through open glades Some of which \u00bd a mile wide, [3] we proceeded down this Creek about 2 miles to where the mountains Closed on either Side crossing the Creek Several tmes & Encamped.

\n

\nOne Deer & Some Pheasants killed this morning, I shot 4 Pheasents of the Common Kind except the taile was black. [4] The road over the last mountain was thick Steep & Stoney as usial, after passing the head of Travelers rest Creek, the road was verry fine leavel open & firm \u00a0\u00a0 Some mountains in view to the S E & S W. Covered with Snow. [5]\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nFriday 13th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 we got all our horses up except one which Capt. Lewis rode we could not find, and a colt also. \u00a0\u00a0 we then loaded our horses and proceeded on a Short distance and came to a warm Spring [6] which run from a ledge of rocks and nearly boiled and issued out in several places \u00a0\u00a0 it had been frequented by the Savages. \u00a0\u00a0 a little dam was fixed and had been used for a bathing place. \u00a0\u00a0 we drank a little of the water and washed our faces in it. \u00a0\u00a0 a handsome green on the creek near this Spring. \u00a0\u00a0 we had Some difficulty here in finding the direct trail. \u00a0\u00a0 we went round a bad way came on the trail again and halted to dine at or near the head of Sd. Creek at a beaver dam. \u00a0\u00a0 then proceeded on ascended a high rough mountain \u00a0\u00a0 over took the hunters who had killed a deer. 2 of them sent back to look for Capt. Lewises horse. \u00a0\u00a0 we crossed the dividing ridge [7] and a number of Spring runs and found it to be only about half a mile from the head Spring of the waters running East to the head Spring of the waters runing west. \u00a0\u00a0 each heading in an open marshy Swamp which is level and full of Springs. \u00a0\u00a0 we came on a creek running west on which we Camped.\u2014 [8]\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 13th. \u00a0\u00a0 A cloudy morning. Capt. Lewis's horse could not be found; but some of the men were left to hunt for him and we proceeded on. When we had gone 2 miles, we came to a most beautiful warm spring, the water of which is considerably above blood-heat; and I could not bear my hand in it without uneasiness. There are so many paths leading to and from this spring, that our guide took a wrong one for a mile or two, and we had bad travelling across till we got into the road again. At noon we halted. Game is scarce; and \nour hunters killed nothing since yesterday morning; though 4 of the best were constantly out, and every one of them furnished with a good horse. While we remained here, Captain Lewis and the men, who had been left with him, came up; but had not found the horse. At 2 o'clock we proceeded on again over a mountain, and in our way found a deer, which our hunters had killed and hung up. In a short time we met with them, and Capt. Lewis sent two back to look for the horse. We passed over a dividing ridge to the waters \nof another creek, and after travelling 12 miles we encamped on the creek, up which there are some prairies or plains.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 13th Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy. \u00a0\u00a0 we got our horses up all but the one Capt. Lewis rode and a colt which our young Indian rode. \u00a0\u00a0 we hunted Some time for them but could not find them. \u00a0\u00a0 then all but 2 or three loaded the horses and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 a Short distance passed a warm Spring, which nearly boiled where it Issued out of the rocks \u00a0\u00a0 a Short distance below the natives has dammed it up to bathe themselves in, and the water in that place is considerable above blood heat. \u00a0\u00a0 it runs out in Sundry places and Some places cooler than others. \u00a0\u00a0 Several of us drank of the water, it has a little sulpur taste and verry clear. \u00a0\u00a0 these Springs are very beautiful to See, and we think them to be as good to bathe in &c. as any other ever yet found in the United States. \u00a0\u00a0 a handsom green or Small meadow on the creek near Sd. Springs. \u00a0\u00a0 a little above we could not git along the Indian trail for the timber which had been blown down in a thicket of pine &c. \u00a0\u00a0 So we went around a hill came on the trail again and proceeded on untill about 11 oClock and halted to dine and let our horses feed on the main fork of the creek where was Several beaver dams. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis and the men who Stayed back to hunt their horses joined us, but had not found them \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters gone on a head \u00a0\u00a0 the mountains rough and rocks which appear above the timber like towers in Some places. \u00a0\u00a0 the day proved pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on assended a high mountain, over took the hunters. \u00a0\u00a0 they had killed a Deer. \u00a0\u00a0 2 of them Sent back after Capt. Lewiss horse. \u00a0\u00a0 we crossed the dividing ridge found it only about half a mile from the head Spring of the water running East to a branch running west. \u00a0\u00a0 each heading on an open Swamp, which is level and full of Springs. \u00a0\u00a0 Came [blank] miles this day and Camped on the branch running west where we had good feed for our horses.

\n

\nFriday Septemr. 13th \u00a0\u00a0 We had Cloudy weather; We got up all our Horses, but the one that Captain Lewis had rode & a Colt which was rode by the Young Indian, who attended our Interpreter; which we had got from the Snake Nation of Indians

\n

\nThe men all turned out to hunt for this horse & Colt, but returned to us without success. \u00a0\u00a0 We on the return of our Men loaded our horses with our Goods & baggage excepting 3 which we left for Men, to ride & seek the lost horses, we proceeded on our way a short distance when we came to a Warm spring, where the water was nearly boiling hot, where it issued out of the Rocks. \u00a0\u00a0 We found a short distance below that place a dam, which the Natives had made in Order to stop the Water, that they might have a bathing place. \u00a0\u00a0 the water at this Bath was considerable above blood heat, this bath run out at different places, some of which was considerable cooler than others.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Several of our party drank of the Water that was in this Bath, it had strongly the taste of Sulphur, & was very clear. \u00a0\u00a0 The same kind of Sulphurous springs are to be found near this place, & has a handsome appearance, Our officers were of opinion that those Springs were very healthy to bathe in; Near this spring run lies a very handsome Creek, with a very handsome Meadow lying \nalong it, & this Meadow lay near to the Spring, & a small distance above it\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We could not get along the Indian trail, for the timber that had been blown down in a thicket of Pine & other Trees.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We went round this falling timber, and round a hill, and got into the road again. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on our Journey 'till about 11 o'Clock A. M. when we halted to dine & let our horses feed which was on the Main fork of the Creek,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 where we saw several beaver dams, Captain Lewis & the Men that staid behind to hunt the horses joined us, but they had not found them. \u00a0\u00a0 Our hunters went on ahead to hunt, 'till the evening. \u00a0\u00a0 The Mountains we found this day were very rough, and Rocks, which appear above the timber like Towers.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The day proved very pleasant, and we proceeded on, & ascended a high mountain; & overtook our hunters who had killed a deer, Captain Lewis sent back 2 of these hunters, to hunt for his Horse & the Colt which was lost\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and crossed the dividing ridge, & found it only about half a mile from the head of a spring where the Water run an east Course, to a branch of Water which run a West course, each heading in an Open Swamp which lies level & abounds with Springs. \u00a0\u00a0 We came only 18 Miles this day & encamped on the branch which run a West course; where we found good Grass for our Horses.\u2014\n\n\n

The springs emerge from granitic rocks of the Cretaceous-age Idaho baholith very near the batholith's contact with rocks of the Precambrian Belt Group. The large size or texture of the crystals in the granite is indicative of the rock having cooled slowly at a great depth in the earth.
