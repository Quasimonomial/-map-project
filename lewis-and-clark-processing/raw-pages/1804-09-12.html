\n September 12, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n
\n
\n Course Distance &c. 12 th of Septr. 1804.\n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n
N. 45\u00b0 W.4miles to a point of woods on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed an Isd Trouble-
\nsom Island\n [1] in the middle of the river \u00a0\u00a0 at the upper pt of this
\nIsland the river was so crouded with Sand bars that we found
\ngreat dificulty in getting the boat over, she turned on the Sand
\n4 times and was verry near turning over. \u00a0\u00a0 we camped on the
\nL. S. near a village of Prarie Dogs
\n
\n

\nSome rain all day to day & Cold\u2014

\n

\nI walked on Shore \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Several foxes Several Villages of Prarie dogs, and a number of Grouse [2]\n

\n

\n Septr. 12th Wednesday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early \u00a0\u00a0 a Dark Cloudey morning wind from the N W. \u00a0\u00a0 cold \u00a0\u00a0 passed, (a village of Little Squerals or Prarie dogs opsd. Camp on the N. Side.) [3]\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
(12th)
\u00a0
\n \n
\n
\n Course Distance & reffrs. Septr. 12th\n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
N.45\u00b0 W.\n 4\n miles to a point of wood on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Passed an Island in the
\nCenter of the river and Several Sand bars (1) on which we
\nfound great dificuelty in passing the water being verry Shallow
\u00a0\n 4\n \u00a0
\n
\n

\n \n Septr. 12th Wednesday 1804\n \n

\n

\na Dark Cloudy Day the wind hard from the N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed (1) a Island the middle of the river at the head of which we found great dificuelty in passing between the Sand bars \u00a0\u00a0 the water Swift and Shallow, it took \u00be of the day to make one mile, we Camped on the L. S. opsd. a Village of Barking Prarie Squriels

\n

\nI walked out in the morn:g and Saw Several Villages of those little animals, also a great number of Grous & 3 foxes, and observed Slate & Coal mixed, [4] Some verry high hills on each Side of the river. [5] \u00a0\u00a0 rains a little all day.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWednesday 12th Sept. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind shifted Since last night in to the North. Set off as usual \u00a0\u00a0 proceded on Slowly. \u00a0\u00a0 the current Swift & wind a head.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Clark walked on Shore S. S. Sergt. Gass & Newman with him.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we had Some difficulty owing to the river being Shallow. \u00a0\u00a0 the Boat wheeled Several times and creened on hir Side So that we were obledged to Spring out and hold hir from oversetting. \u00a0\u00a0 we hunted for the channel & were forced to turn back some distance & take another channel.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We Camped on S. S. [6] \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis went out found another village of little Dogs in a Bottom prarie above the Island we called Troublesome Isd.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 12th. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out as usual and had a cloudy day; passed a long range of black bluffs on the south side, and an island covered with timber, which is all the timber that can be seen from this place. The country round is all hills and prairie. Captain Clarke, myself and another [7] went out to hunt, and did not return till after dark. The boat had much difficulty in passing on account of the sand bars and strong current, and did not make to day more than four miles. [8]\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Wednesday 12 Sept. 1804\n [9] \u00a0\u00a0 Clouday. \u00a0\u00a0 R. Fields continued on with the horse. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a long range of black bluffs on the S. S. and an Island covered with timber. \u00a0\u00a0 that is all the wood that is to be Seen at this place. \u00a0\u00a0 all the \ncountry is hills and praries. \u00a0\u00a0 at 12 oclock Capt. Clark\n Newman and Sergt. Gass went a hunting. \u00a0\u00a0 those in the barge had a Great deal of trouble to Git along the Sand bars, their was So many and the current So rapid that we did not come more than 4 miles. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped after dark on the S Side.

\n

\nWednesday Septemr 12th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning Cloudy, the Man who had the horse on shore, continued on with him. \u00a0\u00a0 We set out about 8 o'clock A. M. and passed a long range of black bluffs, lying on the South side of the River; and an Island covered with timber, which is the only place here that had wood on it. \u00a0\u00a0 The country here being Priaries and hills. \u00a0\u00a0 At 12 oClock A. M. Captain Clarke and two of our Serjeants landed, in order to go a hunting. \u00a0\u00a0 The Men who were in the barge, had a great deal of trouble to get her along, their being so many sand Barrs in the way, and the current running, so rapid against us, so that we did not come more than 4 Miles this day. \u00a0\u00a0 We came too after dark, & encamped on the South side of the River,\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. Evidently later Durex, or Hiram Wood, Island. Atlas map 20; MRC map 35; MRY map 37. (back)
\n
2. The sharp-tailed grouse, a Lewis and Clark discovery although they apparently never gave a full description of it. They sent a live specimen to Jefferson from Fort Mandan in April 1805, but it did not survive the journey. Cutright (LCPN), 81, 121, 375\u201377. (back)
\n
3. The prairie dog village, not the camp, was on the north side. The camp was apparently a short distance north of the Lyman-Gregory county line, South Dakota. Atlas map 20; MRC map 36. (back)
\n
4. \n Clark mistakes the Pierre Shale, which is very rich in organic matter and dark gray to black in color, for slate and coal. (back)
\n
5. Including the Bijou Hills in Brule County, east of the river. Nicollet (MMR), 411; MRC map 36. (back)
\n
6. Apparently a short distance north of the Lyman-Gregory county line, South Dakota. (back)
\n
7. \n Newman. (back)
\n
8. They camped in Brule County, South Dakota. (back)
\n
9. \n Whitehouse again may have written \"4\" over \"6\" for the year. (back)
