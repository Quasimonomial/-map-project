\n September 21, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Saturday September 21st 1805.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nWe were detained this morning untill 11 OCk. in consequence of not being able to collect our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 we then set out and proceeded along the ridge on which we had encamped, leaving which at 1\u00bd we passed a large creek runing to the left just above it's junction with another which run parrallel with and on the left of our road before we struck the creek; [1] through the level wide and heavy timbered bottom of this creek we proceeded about 2\u00bd miles when bearing to the right we passed a broken country heavily timbered great quantities of which had fallen and so obstructed our road that it was almost impracticable to proceed in many places. \u00a0\u00a0 though these hills we proceeded about 5 Ms. when we passed a small creek [2] on which Capt Clark encamped on the 19th \u00a0\u00a0 passing this creek we continued our rout 5 Ms thro' a similar country when we struck a large creek at the forks, [3] passed the Northen branch and continued down it on the West side 1 mile and encamped in a small open bottom [4] where there was tolerable food for our horses. I directed the horses to be hubbled to prevent delay in the morning being determined to make a forced march tomorrow in order to reach if possible the open country. we killed a few Pheasants, and I killd a prarie woolf [5] which together with the ballance of our horse beef and some crawfish [6] \nwhich we obtained in the creek enabled us to make one more hearty meal, not knowing where the next was to be found. \u00a0\u00a0 the Arborvita [NB: Copy for Dr Barton\n ] [7] increases in quantity and size. I saw several sticks today large enough to form eligant perogues of at least 45 feet in length.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 I find myself growing weak for the want of food and most of the men complain of a similar deficiency and have fallen off very much. \u00a0\u00a0 the general course of this day S 30 W 15 M.\u2014

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n Septr. 21st Saturday 1805\n [8]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na fine morning \u00a0\u00a0 Sent out all the hunters early in different directions to Kill Something and delayed with the Indians to prevent Suspicion & to acquire as much information as possible. \u00a0\u00a0 one of them Drew me a Chart of the river & nations below informed of one falls below which the white men lived from whome they got white beeds cloth &c. &c. The day proved warm, 2 Chifs of Bands visited me to day\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters all returned without any thing, I collected a horse load of roots & 3 Sammon & Sent R Fields with one Indian to meet Capt Lewis \u00a0\u00a0 at 4 oClock Set out with the other men to the river, passed thro a fine Pine Country \u00a0\u00a0 decended a Steep ruged hill verry long to a Small river which comes from our left and I suppose it to be [blank] River \u00a0\u00a0 passed down the river 2 miles on a Steep hill side \u00a0\u00a0 at 11 oClock P. M. arrived at a camp of 5 Squars a boy & 2 Children \u00a0\u00a0 those people were glad to See us & gave us drid Sammon \u00a0\u00a0 one had formerly been taken by the Minitarries of the north & Seen white men, our guide called the Chief who was fishing on the other Side of the river, whome I found a Cherfull man of about 65 \u00a0\u00a0 I gave him a Medal.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n Thursday [NB: Saturday] 21st Septr. 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nA fine morning \u00a0\u00a0 Sent out all the hunters in different directions to hunt deer, I myself delayd with the Chief to prevent Suspission and to Collect by Signs as much information as possible about the river and Countrey in advance. The Cheif drew me a kind of chart of the river, and informed me that a greater Cheif than himself was fishing at the river half a days march from his village called the twisted hare, and that the river forked a little below his Camp [9] and at a long distance below & below 2 large forks one from the left & the other from the right [10] the river passed thro'gh the mountains at which place was a great fall of the water passing through the rocks, [11] to those falls white people lived from whome they preceured the white Beeds & Brass &c. which the womin wore; a Chief of another band visit me to day and Smoked a pipe, I gave my handkerchief & a Silver Cord with a little Tobacco to those Chiefs, The hunters all return without any thing, I purchased as much Provisions as I could with what fiew things I chaned to have in my Pockets, Such a Salmon Bread roots & berries, & Sent one man R. Fields with an Indian to meet Capt. Lewis, and at 4 oClock P M. Set out to the river, met a man at dark on his way from the river to the village, whome I hired and gave the neck handkerchief of one of the men, to polit me to the Camp of the twisted hare, [12] we did not arrive at the Camp of the Twisted hare but oppost, untill half past 11 oClock P M. [13] \u00a0\u00a0 found at this Camp five Squars & 3 Children. \u00a0\u00a0 my guide called to the Chief who was Encamped with 2 others on a Small island in the river, he Soon joind me, I found him a Chearfull man with apparant Siencerity, I gave him a medal &c. and Smoked untill 1 oClock a. m. and went to Sleep. The Countrey from the mountains to the river hills is a leavel rich butifull Pine Countrey badly watered, thinly timbered & covered with grass\u2014 [14] \u00a0\u00a0 The weather verry worm after decending into the low Countrey,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the river hills are verry high & Steep, Small bottoms \u00a0\u00a0 to this little river which is Flat head\n [15] & is 160 yards wide and Sholey \u00a0\u00a0 This river is the one we killed the first Coalt on near a fishing were\n

\n

\nI am verry Sick to day and puke which relive me.\n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday 21st Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we could not find all our horses untill about 10 oClock at which time we Set out, and proceed. on \u00a0\u00a0 Soon crossed a creek [16] and proceed. on nearly a west course, over a rough trail. Some of the ridges the timber has been killed Some time past by\nfires, and is fell across the trail So that we have Some difficulty to pass. \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening we descended down a Mount. and came on a large creek running S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 we came down it a Short distance and Camped \u00a0\u00a0 had come 11 miles this day. Capt. Lewis killed a wolf \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the party killed three pheasants and a duck. \u00a0\u00a0 we eat them and caught a fiw craw fish in the creek and eat them.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 21st. \u00a0\u00a0 The morning was pleasant; but it was late before we got our horses collected. About 10 o'clock we were ready to start; and passed along the ridge with a great deal of difficulty and fatigue, our march being much impeded by the fallen timber. A great portion of the timber through which we passed along this ridge is dead, and a considerable part fallen; and our horses are weak and much jaded. One of them got into a small swamp, and wet a bale of merchandize. About 4 o'clock in the afternoon we got down the mountain to a creek, [17] which runs nearly southwest. This course we suppose is a very good one for us. We went down this creek about a mile, and encamped on it for the night in a small rich bottom. Here we killed a duck and two or three pheasants; and supped upon them and the last of our horse meat. We also killed a wolf and eat it. The hunters did not join us this evening, nor the two men who went to look for the horse.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 21st Sept. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear pleasant morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we went out eairly to hunt up our horses, but they were much Scatered. \u00a0\u00a0 we did not find them all untill about 10 oClock at which time we Set out and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 crossed a creek & went on a west course over a hilley rough trail. \u00a0\u00a0 on Some of the ridges the timber has been killed by fire and fell across the trail So that we had Some difficulty to git a long the trail. \u00a0\u00a0 in the after part of the day we descended down a hill & came to the forks of a creek [18] where it is large \u00a0\u00a0 we went down it a Short distance and Camped at a good place for feed near the creek. \u00a0\u00a0 had Come 11 miles this day. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis killed a wolf. \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the men killed a duck and three Phesants. \u00a0\u00a0 we caught Some craw \nfish in the creek, and eat them.

\n

\nSaturday Septemr 21st \u00a0\u00a0 A Clear pleasant morning, some of our party were sent out to collect our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 they found them after much difficulty, which detained us 'till about 10 o'Clock A. M. at which time we set out, & \ncontinued on our Journey. \u00a0\u00a0 We crossed a Creek, and went on a West course over a hilly rough trail, on a ridge of mountains \u00a0\u00a0 The timber on this trail, had been killed by fire, and fell across the path so that we had great difficulty to get along it. \u00a0\u00a0 In the afternoon we descended down a hill, & came to the forks of a creek, where the Creek got to be large.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We went down the Creek a short distance, & encamped at a flatt piece of land which lay along the same Creek, where we found plenty of fine Grass for our horses. \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis went out to hunt from this place, and took some of our party with him. \u00a0\u00a0 They killed a Wolf, a duck & 3 Pheasants, & some of the party catched some craw fish in the Creek.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came about 11 Miles this day.\u2014\n\n\n\n

13. This camp was on the Clearwater River on the \"Fishing Island\" on fig. 9 and Atlas map 71, about a mile above present Orofino, Clearwater County. Space, 17. While there is no archaeological information on this specific locality of Fish Island, a major concentration of Nez Perces lived at the confluence of the North Fork and the Clearwater because of the salmon fishing. The area has been extensively modified by construction of Dworshak Dam on the North Fork just above the confluence, and an associated fish hatchery at the confluence itself. However, limited archaeological research in the area has exposed archaeological remains extending back several thousand years. Ames; Mattson. (back)
