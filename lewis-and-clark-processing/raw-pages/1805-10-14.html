\nat this rapid the Canoe a Stern Steared by drewyer Struck a rock turned the men got out on a rock \u00a0\u00a0 the Stern of the Canoe took in water and She Sunk \u00a0\u00a0 the men on the rock hel her, a number of articles floated \u00a0\u00a0 all that Could be Cought were taken by 2 of the othr Canoes, Great many articles lost among other things 2 of the mens beding Shot pouches Tomahaws &c. &c. and every article wet \u00a0\u00a0 of which we have great Cause to lament as all our loose Powder two Canisters, all our roots prepared in the Indian way, and one half of our goods, fortunately the lead canisters which was in the canoe was tied down, otherwise they must have been lost as the canoe turned over \u00a0\u00a0 we got off the men from the rock \u00a0\u00a0 toed our canoe on Shore after takeing out all the Stores &c. we Could & put them out to dry on the Island on which we found Some wood which was covered with Stones, this is the Parts of an Indian house, which we used for fire wood, by the wish of our two Chiefs\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Those Chees, one of them was in the Canoe, Swam in & Saved Some property, The Inds. have buried fish on this Isld. which we are Cautious not to touch. \u00a0\u00a0 our Small Canoe & three Indians in another was out of Sight at the time our missfortune hapined, and did not join us. \u00a0\u00a0 wind hard S W.\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n October 14th \u2329Sunday\u232a Monday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na Verry Cold morning wind from the West and Cool untill about 12 oClock When it Shifted to the S. W. \u00a0\u00a0 at 2 \u00bd miles passed a remarkable rock verry large and resembling the hill [hull] of a Ship [3] Situated on a Lard point at Some distance from the assending Countrey \u00a0\u00a0 passed rapids at 6 and 9 miles. \u00a0\u00a0 at 12 miles we Came too at the head of a rapid which the Indians told me was verry bad, we viewed the rapid found it bad \u00a0\u00a0 in decending three Stern Canoes Stuk fast for Some time on the head of the rapid and one Struk a rock in the worst part, fortunately all landed Safe below the rapid which was nearly 3 miles in length. \u00a0\u00a0 here we dined, and for the first time for three weeks past I had a good dinner of Blue wing Teel, [4] after dinner we Set out and had not proceded on two miles before our Stern Canoe in passing thro a Short rapid opposit the head of an Island, run on a Smoth rock and turned broad Side, the men got out on the [rock] all except one of our Indian Chiefs who Swam on Shore, The Canoe filed and Sunk \u00a0\u00a0 a number of articles floated out, Such as the mens bedding clothes & Skins, the Lodge &c. &c. the greater part of which were cought by 2 of the Canoes, whilst a 3rd was unloading & Steming the Swift Current to the relief of the men on the rock who could with much dificuelty hold the Canoe. \u00a0\u00a0 however in about an hour we got the men an Canoe to Shore with the Loss of Some bedding Tomahaws Shot pouches Skins Clothes &c. &c. \u00a0\u00a0 all wet \u00a0\u00a0 we had every articles exposed to the Sun to dry on the Island, our loss in provisions is verry Considerable all our roots was in the Canoe that Sunk, and Cannot be dried Sufficint to Save, our loose powder was also in the Canoe and is all wett This I think \u2329may\u232a, we Shall saved.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 In this Island [5] we found some Spilt [Split] timber the parts of a house which the Indians had verry Securely covered with Stone, we also observed a place where the Indians had buried there fish, we have made it a point at all times not to take any thing belonging to the Indians even their wood. \u00a0\u00a0 but at this time we are Compelled to violate that rule and take a part of the Split timber we find here bured for fire wood, as no other is to be found in any direction. our Small canoe which was a head returned at night with 2 ores which they found floating below. The wind this after noon from the S. W. as usial and hard\n

[Ordway]

\nMonday 14th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear cold morning. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind high N W. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out as usal and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 the current rapid. \u00a0\u00a0 about noon we came to a bad rockey rapid where 2 canoes ran fast on a rock but we got off without Injury. \u00a0\u00a0 a Small Island on the Lard. Side of the rapids \u00a0\u00a0 we halted jist below to dine. \u00a0\u00a0 then proceeded on about 8 miles then came to another bad rapid at the head of an Island. \u00a0\u00a0 the canoe I had charge of ran fast on a rock in the middle of the river and turned across the rock. \u00a0\u00a0 we \nattempeted to git hir off but the waves dashed over hir So that She filled with water. \u00a0\u00a0 we held hir untill one of the other canoes was unloaded and came to our assistance \u00a0\u00a0 considerable of the baggage washed overboard, but the most of it was taken up below when the canoe got lightned \u00a0\u00a0 She went of[f] of a sudden & left myself and three more Standing on the rock half leg deep in the rapid water untill a canoe came to our assistance. \u00a0\u00a0 we got the most of the baggage to Shore \u00a0\u00a0 two mens bedding lost one tommahawk, and some other Small articles a Small copper kittle &C. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped [6] on an Island Stard. Side at an old fishery where the natives had dryed Sammon burryed their wood covred over it. \u00a0\u00a0 we took Some for our use &C. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the men killed 8 ducks \u00a0\u00a0 the country continues barron and broken in places &C\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 14th. \u00a0\u00a0 We embarked early in a fine clear cool morning; passed some rapids; and at 11 came to one very bad, but we got over without injury. We saw some geese and ducks this forenoon and killed some of the ducks. \nAbout 1 o'clock a canoe hit a rock, and part of her sunk, and a number of the things floated out. With the assistance of the other canoes all the men got safe to shore; but the baggage was wet, and some articles were lost. We halted on an island to dry the baggage, having come 14 miles.\n\n\n

\n\u2329Saturday\u232a Monday 14th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear cold morning the wind high a head & west. \u00a0\u00a0 we took an eairly breakfast and Set out and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 as usal the current mostly rapid about noon we went down a verry bad rockey rapid the worst we have passd in this River. \u00a0\u00a0 three of the canoes ran fast on a Solid rock at the head of the rapids two on at a time and was in great dangr. of being lost. \u00a0\u00a0 one Struck a rock in the middle of the rapids and luckily escaped being Stove. \u00a0\u00a0 as luck would have it we all got Safe down. \u00a0\u00a0 a Small Island near the Lard. Side, in the rapids we halted a little below to dine. \u00a0\u00a0 two Indians are rideing down the River, and have to ride verry fast to keep up with us. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on verry well about 8 miles \u00a0\u00a0 then came to a rockey rapid at the head of an Island in which one of the canoes under charge of Sergt. ordway ran fast on a Solid rock and Swung across the rock. \u00a0\u00a0 they got out on the rock and attempted to Shove the canoe off the rock, but could not Start hir for Some time. \u00a0\u00a0 the waves dashed over hir bow So that when we got hir loose from the rock She filled full of water and considerable of the baggage and bedding washed out. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes below unloaded and \u2329came\u232a went to their assistance. \u00a0\u00a0 took out Some of the loading. \u00a0\u00a0 the canoe then broke away from them and left 4 men Standing on the rock. \u00a0\u00a0 the water half leg deep over the Smooth rock & rappid. \u00a0\u00a0 a canoe Shortly went \nand took them off the rock, and got all to Shore except a Small brass kittle & bowl, 2 mens robes & blankets 2 Spoons, one bag of root bread one Shot pouch & powder horn a dressed Elk Skin and Some other Small articles. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped below the rapids on Sd. Island and put out all the wet baggage to dry. \u00a0\u00a0 we found Some wood on the Island covered up with Stones where the natives burryed Sammon everry Spring. \u00a0\u00a0 wood was So Scarse that we made use of that which was covred So carefully with Stone. \u00a0\u00a0 we came [blank] miles this day. \u00a0\u00a0 the country continues barron. \u00a0\u00a0 Some places broken, other places high Smooth plains &c. \u00a0\u00a0 Some or one of the men killed 7 or 8 ducks to day

\n

\nMonday October 14th \u00a0\u00a0 A Clear cold morning, the Wind blowing hard from the Westward, We took an early breakfast and proceeded on our Voyage as usual, We found the current to run mostly rapid. \u00a0\u00a0 About noon we went down a very bad Rockey rapid, it being the worst rapid that we had passed in this River. \u00a0\u00a0 Three of our canoes ran a solid rock & stuck fast. \u00a0\u00a0 This was at the head of a rapid. \u00a0\u00a0 Two of these canoes were fast at the same \ntime, and had nearly been lost. \u00a0\u00a0 the other Canoe struck the same rock, & got fast in the middle of the fall, however with great exertion our Men got them afloat again & brought them safe over these falls, & got \u2329brought\u232a \nthem safe down to a small Island lying near the South side of the River, which Island lay still in the rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 We continued on a small distance below this Island, where we halted to dine.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We have since we set out this morning, constantly saw two Indians riding down along the bank of the River, who had to ride fast to keep up with us.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We left this Island at 1 o'Clock P. M. and proceed on down the River about 8 Miles very well, We then came to a \nRockey rapid, which lay at the head of an Island, in passing through this rapid, one of our Canoes, that was in the charge of Serjeant Ordway ran fast on a solid rock, and swung across it. \u00a0\u00a0 The Men that was on board this canoe all got out of her into the water on the rock, & attempted to shove her off, but could not for some time start her. \u00a0\u00a0 The Water all this while dashed over the bow of this Canoe, & when they got her loose she filled with water & a considerable quantity of baggage & bedding washed out. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our Canoes that was a small distance below where this accident happened, unloaded and went to their assistance; and took out some of the loading.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Canoe that was on the rock then broke away from the Men, & left 4 Men standing on the Rock in the Water half leg deep over the smooth rocks & rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our Canoes went in a short time to their assistance & took these Men from off the Rock on board of it. \u00a0\u00a0 We saved some part of that Canoes loading & lost a small brass kettle, Buffalo robes, blanketts, Spoons shot pouches & powder horns, dressed Elk Skins, a bag of Root bread &ca. \u00a0\u00a0 We encamped on an Island lying below the Rapids & put out all the wet baggage to dry. \u00a0\u00a0 We found on the Island, some Wood that was covered up with Stones, which the Natives had buried. \u00a0\u00a0 The Natives come to this place to fish in the Spring of the Year. \u00a0\u00a0 We made use of the Wood that we had found, to dress our Victuals with. \u00a0\u00a0 We came about 25 Miles this day, \u00a0\u00a0 The land continues to be a Barren & in many places broken & high smooth plains.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 In the Evening one of our Men killed 8 Ducks which he got. \u00a0\u00a0 our Course still continues West.\u2014\n\n\n\n\n

\n
5. The island was thought to have been at former Pine Tree Rapids, between Franklin and Walla Walla counties, Washington according to Coues (HLC), 2:631\u201332 n. 10. That area is just downstream from Burr Canyon and is now covered by the waters of Lake Sacajawea. Pine Tree Rapids was probably an earlier rapids that the party passed. It may have been Clark's \"bad rapid on which 3 Canoes Stuck\" (October 14, 1805). The \"island and bad rapid\" is probably in the area of Rescue Island Rapids. The camp was also apparently in this area in Franklin County. Atlas map 74 shows \"Indian Caves\" in the cliffs of the northwest shore of the Snake River and a \"Tomb.\" The most conspicuous caves in this general area are the Windust Caves, but they are some miles upstream from where the camp seems to have been. Clark may have misplaced the caves, found other than the Windust Caves, or the locating of the camp may be in error. In any case, archaeological excavations of these series of nine caves at Windust have proved extremely significant in studies of prehistoric cultural history and paleoenvironments of the area. Rice (CSWC); Thompson (WC). (back)
