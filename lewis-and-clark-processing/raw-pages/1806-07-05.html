\n July 5, 1806\n

\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n July 5th 1806.\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out at 6 A. M.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 steered

\n
\n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n \n
N. 75 E.\u00a0\u00a06 \u00bdM. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a stout C. N Side at 2\u00bd M. \u00a0\u00a0 anoth[er] just
\nabove [1] \u00a0\u00a0 saw an old indian encampment of 11 lodges of
\nbark and [leather?] on S. side at 3\u00bd M. \u00a0\u00a0 killed a deer.
N. 25 E.12 \u232910\u232am. passing a \u2329large\u232a small creek at \u2329\u00bd\u232a one m. on S sid [2] on
\nwhich there is a handsom and extensive Valley and plain
\nfor 10 or 12 ms. also another creek 12 yd. wide at \u00bd a mile
\nfurther on N. side [3] and another 8 yds. wide on \u2329S.\u232a N. [L?]
\nside at 5 ms further [4] \u00a0\u00a0 one & \u00bd m. short of the extremity
\nof this course arrive at a high prarie on \u2329S.\u232a N. [L.?] side
\nfrom one to three miles in width extending up the river. [5]\n
\nhalted and dined in the mouth of a little drane on the left
\nof the plain where there was a considerable quantity of
\nquawmash. \u00a0\u00a0 saw a gang of antelopes [6] here of which we
\nkilled one \u00a0\u00a0 the does at this season herd with each other and
\nhave their young. \u00a0\u00a0 the bucks are alone \u00a0\u00a0 there are many
\nwild horses on Clarkes river \u00a0\u00a0 about the place we passed it
\nwe saw some of them at a distance. \u00a0\u00a0 there are said to be
\nmany of them about the head of the yellowstone river.\u2014\n
East\u00a0\u00a06m. to the entrance of Werner's Creek\n [7] 35 yds. wide through
\na high extensive prairie on N. side. \u00a0\u00a0 hills low and timbered
\nwith the long leafed pine, larch, and some fir. \u00a0\u00a0 the road
\npasses at some distance to the left of the river and \u2329their\u232a
\nthis couses \u2329are\u232a is with the river.
N. 22 W.\u00a0\u00a04miles to a high insulated knob just above the entrance of a
\nCreek 8 yards wide which discharges itself into Werners
\n\u2329run\u232a Creek. [8]\n
N. 75 E.\u00a0\u00a02 \u00bdM. to the river passing through an extensive and handsom
\nplain on Werner's Creek, crossing that creek at 1 m. and
\nleaving a high prarie hill to the right seperating the plain
\nfrom the river. \u00a0\u00a0 saw two swan in this beautiful Creek.\u2014
East\u00a0\u00a03m. to the entrance of a large creek 20 yds. wide Called
\n Seamans' Creek\n [9] passing a creek at 1 m. 8 yds. wide. [10] this
\ncourse with the river, the road passing through an exten-
\nsive high prarie rendered very uneven by a vast number of
\nlittle hillucks and sinkholes \u2329holds\u232a. \u00a0\u00a0 at the heads of these
\ntwo creeks high broken mountains stand at the distance
\nof 10 m. forming a kind of Cove generally of open un-
\ntimbered country.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we encamped on the lower side of
\nthe last creek just above it's entrance. [11] \u00a0\u00a0 here a war party
\nhad encamped about 2 months since and conceald their
\nfires.\u2014 [12]\n
31 m.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n Saturday July 5th 1806\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nI rose at day light this morning \u00a0\u00a0 despatched Labeash after a Buck which he killed late last evening; and I with the three men who I had Sent in Serch of a ford across the West fork of Clarks river, and examined each ford neither of them I thought would answer to pass the fork without wetting all the loads. \u00a0\u00a0 near one of those places pointed out by Colter I found a practiable foard and returned to Camp, ordered everything packed up and after Brackfast we Set out \u00a0\u00a0 passed 5 Chanels of the river which is divided by Small Islands \u00a0\u00a0 in passing the 6th & last Chanel Colter horse Swam and with Some dificuelty he made the Opposite Shore, Shannon took a different derection from Colter rained his horse up the Stream and passed over very well \u00a0\u00a0 I derected all to follow Shannon and pass quartering up the river which they done and passed over tolerably well the water running over the back of the 2 Smaller horses only. \u00a0\u00a0 unfortunately my trunk & portmantue Containing Sea otter Skins flags Some curiosites & necessary articles in them got wet, also an esortment of Medicine, and my roots. \u00a0\u00a0 about 1 mile we struk the East fork which had fallen and was not higher than when we passed it last fall \u00a0\u00a0 we had not proceeded up this fork more than 1 mile eer we struck the road by which we passed down last fall and kept it \u00a0\u00a0 at one mile we crossed the river at a very good foard and continued up on the East Side to the foot of the Mountain nearly opposite flour Crek\n [13] & halted to let our horses graze and dry our wet articles. I saw fresh Sign of 2 horses and a fire burning on the side of the road. I prosume that those indians are spies from the Shoshones. Shannon & Crusat killed each a deer this morning and J. Shields killed a female Ibex or bighorn on the side of the Mountain, this Animal was very meager. Shannon left his tomahawk at the place he killed his deer. I derect him to return for it and join me in the Vally on the East Side of this mountain. \u00a0\u00a0 gave Shields permission to proceed on over to the 1st Vally and there hunt untill my arival this evening at that place, after drying every article which detained us untill \u00bd past 4 P. M. we packed up and Crossed the Mountain into the vally where we first met with the flatheads\n [14] \u00a0\u00a0 here I overtook Shields he had not killed any thing. I crossed the river which heads in a high peecked mountain Covered with Snow N. E. of the Vally at about 20 Miles. [15] \u00a0\u00a0 [NB: see note] [16] \u00a0\u00a0 Shields informed me that the Flat head indians passed up the Small Creek which we came down last fall about 2 miles above our Encampment of the 4th & 5th of, Septr. I proceeded up this South branch 2 Miles and encamped on the E. side of the Creek, [17] and Sent out several men to examine the road. Shields returned at dark and informed me that the best road turned up the hill from the creek 3 Miles higher up, and appeared to be a plain beaten parth. [18] \u00a0\u00a0 as this rout of the Oat lash shoots can be followed it will evidently Shorten our rout at least 2 days and as the indians informed me last fall a much better rout than the one we came out. \u00a0\u00a0 at all events I am deturmined to make the attempt and follow their trail if possible \u00a0\u00a0 if I can prosue [EC: prosue = pursue] it my rout will be nearer and much better than the one we Came from the Shoshones, & if I should not be able to follow their road; our rout can't possibly be much wors. The hunters killed two deer this evening. The after part of the day we only come 8 miles makeing a total of 20 Miles\u2014. Shannon Came up about Sunset haveing found his tomahawk.\n\n\t \t\t\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSaturday 5th July 1806. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair M. \u00a0\u00a0 we took breakfast as usal and Set out to cross the right fork of the river which we found nearly Swimming. \u00a0\u00a0 proceed. on up the river Some distance \u00a0\u00a0 crossd. the other fork. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters killed a deer and a Mountain Sheep or big horn animel. \u00a0\u00a0 about noon we halted in a bottom to dine. Shannon left his tommahawk back where he killed the deer & went back for it. \u00a0\u00a0 we delayed about 3 hours and proceed on over the hills \u00a0\u00a0 towards evening we came to the Smooth plains where we Saw the 1st flat heads or Tus e paw last year as we passd down. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped [19] on the branch & plain \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters killed two deer. Shannon joined us with his tommahawk &C.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Saturday 5th. \u00a0\u00a0 We had another beautiful morning, set out early and proceeded on the same course as yesterday through a rough country, with a number of branches or small streams flowing from the hills. We killed one deer, and about 11 o'clock came to a valley three quarters of a mile wide, [20] all plains, where we halted to dine and to let our horses feed. The hills upon \neach side are handsomely covered with timber of the fir kind. While we rested here one of hunters killed a cabre or antelope. At 1 o'clock we proceeded on again up the valley. When we had gone about nine miles we came to and crossed a river, [21] about 35 yards wide, which flows in with a rapid current from some snow topped mountains on the north, [22] where the valley is two or three miles wide. Having gone about four miles further we came to the head of the valley, where the hills came close upon the river for two miles. After we had passed these narrows we came to another large and beautiful valley four or five miles wide, and all plains, except some timber on the river banks. In the evening we encamped [23] on the bank of a handsome creek which comes in from the north, bold stream of 15 yards wide.\n\n\n

13. \n Clark crossed from the north to the south side of the West Fork Bitterroot River, in Ravalli County, Montana, then crossed the East Fork to its east side and continued southeasterly along that stream. Flour Creek (\"Flower creek\" in Biddle) is shown on Atlas map 68 (\"Flour Camp Creek\") flowing into the East Fork on the west side below the camp of September 6, 1805; it is present Warm Springs Creek. (back)
\n
14. \n Ross, or Ross's, Hole, in Ravalli County near present Sula, where the paty met the Flatheads (Salish) on September 4, 1805. Appleman (LC), 326\u201327; Atlas maps 68, 103. (back)
\n
15. \n East Fork Bitterroot River heads near West Pintlar Peak on the Ravalli-Beaverhead County line, Montana, which is also the Continental Divide. (back)
\n
16. \n Biddle's interlineation refers to notes he took in conversation with Clark after the expedition. In this case, he wrote, \"Shields & others I had sent out to examine the route which the Oolashoots we had met last fall had taken across the mountain when we had seen them 4 & 5 Sept. 1805 said he had found a large trail which turned up the hill from the creek 3 miles higher, an easterly branch of the creek we had come down last fall. Shannon found a trail not so large, nor so good.\" Biddle Notes [ca. April 1810], Jackson (LLC), 2:521. (back)
\n
17. \n Clark's camp is not marked on Atlas map 68 but is on maps 103, 104; it was in Ravalli County on Camp Creek, near Camp Creek Ranger Station and U.S. Highway 93, some two miles southeast of the party's camp of September 4\u20135, 1805. (back)
\n
18. Apparently the road which Clark pursued to Camp Fortunate, diverging from the old westbound route; see entries for subsequent days. (back)

19. The party crossed from the north to the south side of the West Fork Bitterroot River,\nRavalli County, Montana, then crossed the East Fork to its east side and continued southeasterly along that stream. From there they reached Ross, or Ross's, Hole, near Sula, where they had met the Flatheads, or Salish, on September 4, 1805. They camped on Camp Creek near Camp Creek Ranger Station and U.S. Highway 93, Ravalli County. (back)

22. \n Clearwater River is formed by streams rising in the Mission Range and the Swan Range. (back)

23. On the west side of Monture Creek, just above its entrance into Blackfoot River, Powell County, Montana. (back)
