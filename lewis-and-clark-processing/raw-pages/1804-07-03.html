\n July 3, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark] [1]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nJuly 3rd 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out verry early this morning and proceeded on under a gentle Breeze from the South \u00a0\u00a0 passed two Islands \u00a0\u00a0 one a Small Willow Island on the L. S. (1) \u00a0\u00a0 The other a large Island Called Cow I. (\n Isle Vache\n ), [2] this Island is large, opposit to the head on the S. S. is a (2) large pond, a Bad Sand bar on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we attempted without Success, & was oblige to Cross back, I Saw a White horse on the L. S. in view of the upper point of the Island, (3) passed a large Sand bar at the S. point, w[e] halted to day about a mile above the Island and found a horse, which had been lost by the Indians, verry fat and jentle, Sent him on to join the others which was ahead on the L S \u00a0\u00a0 at this place, the french had a tradeing house, for to trade with the Kanzes on a high bottom on the L. S. near the hills which is Prarie \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on round a large Sand bar on the L. S. & Camped [3] (opposit a large Sand bar in the middle of the river). \u00a0\u00a0 on the L. S. a Butifull Small Stream passes back of the trading house, before mentioned [4]\n

\n July 3td, Tusday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out verry early this morning and proceeded on under a gentle Breeze from the S. \u00a0\u00a0 passed two Islands (1) one a Small willow Island on the L. S. the other large Called by the french \n Isle \u2329la\u232a de Vache\n or \n Cow Island, opposit the head on the S. S. is a large Pond Containg Beever, & fowl, a bad Sandbar on the S. S. above the Island, on the L. S. we halted at an old Tradeing house, [NB: deserted] here we found a verry fat horse, which appears to have been lost a long time \u00a0\u00a0 a butifull Small run passes back of the Tradeing house near the high land, we came to at a round bend on the L. S. and Camped

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday July 3rd 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly & proceeded on under a gentle Breese from the South. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a long Island & Several Small ones \u00a0\u00a0 we found a white horse on the bank of the river near whare their was an old Trading house built by a French merchant from St. Louis to Trade with the kansars Indians. The land is Good high bottom pine Timber & black wallnut honey locas oak &C. &C\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 I Saw waat they call bucks Eye with the nuts on them; [6] \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a high prarie oposite to the Trading house & Camped at a point on the South Side of the Missouris\n

[Floyd]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday July 3dth \u00a0\u00a0 Set out verry erley this morning under a Jentel Breas from the South \u00a0\u00a0 found a Stray Horse on the South Side \u00a0\u00a0 Havg Had Ben Lost for Som time \u00a0\u00a0 water verry Strong So Hard that we Could Hardley Stem it \u00a0\u00a0 Came 10 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Campt on the South Side \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is verry mirey\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 3rd. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded again at five, and continued our voyage until 12, when we stopt at an old trading place on the south side of the river. There we found a grey horse; but saw no appearance of any persons having lately encamped at that place.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0

\nTusday 3rd [7] \u00a0\u00a0 Got on our way [page torn] \u00a0\u00a0 Island oposite the [page torn] \u00a0\u00a0 Wind Rose \u00a0\u00a0 Saild. [page torn] \u00a0\u00a0 Lat 38\u00b0 31 13 N.

\n

\nTuesday July 3rd \u00a0\u00a0 We started from Ordaways Island, opposite to a place called the French Garrison. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 The wind being in our favour, and blowing a good Breeze, The hunters this day killed 3 deer. \u00a0\u00a0 We encamp'd in the Evening, having Sailed 14 Miles this day.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Latitude of Ordaways Island being 38\u00b0 31' 13 North\n\t \t\t\t \n
