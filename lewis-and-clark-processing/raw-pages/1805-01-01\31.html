\n January 1, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n \n \n Fort Mandan\n on the N E bank
of the Missouries 1600 miles up Tuesday\n
\n January the 1st 1805\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThe Day was ushered in by the Discharge of two Cannon, we Suffered 16 men [1] with their musick to visit the 1st Village for the purpose of Danceing, by as they Said the perticular request of the Chiefs of that village, about 11 oClock I with an inturpeter & two men walked up to the Village (my views were to alay Some little miss understanding which had taken place thro jelloucy and mortificatiion as to our treatment towards them[)]

\n

\nI found them much pleased at the Danceing of our men, I ordered my black Servent to Dance which amused the Croud verry much, and Some what astonished them, that So large a man Should be active &c. &.

\n

\nI went into the lodges of all the men of note except two, whome I heard had made Some expressions not favourable towards us, in Compareing us with the trabers from the north\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Those Cheifs observed [NB: to us that] what they Sayed was in just [NB: in jest] & lafture.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 just as I was about to return the 2d Chief and the Black man, also a Chief returnd from a mission on which they had been Sent to meet a large party 150 of \n Gross Ventres\n who were on their way down from their Camps 10 Miles above to revenge on the \n Shoe\n tribe an injurey which they had received by a Shoe man Steeling a \n Gross Venters Girl, those Chiefs gave the pipe turned the party back, after Delivering up the girl, which the Shoe Chief had taken and given to them for that purpose. I returned in the evening, at night the party except 6 returned, with 3 robes, an 13 Strings of Corn which the indians had given them, The Day was worm, Themtr. 34\u00b0 abov 0, Some fiew Drops of rain about Sunset, at Dark it began to Snow, and Snowed the greater part of the night, (the temptr for Snow is about 0) \u00a0\u00a0 The Black Cat with his family visited us to day and brought a little meet\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 1st Jany. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy but moderate. \u00a0\u00a0 we fired a Swivel & drank a Glass. \u00a0\u00a0 about 9 o.C. 15 of the party went up to the 1st village of Mandans to dance as it had been their request. \u00a0\u00a0 carried with us a fiddle & a Tambereen & a Sounden horn. \u00a0\u00a0 as we arived at the entrence of the vil. we fired one round \u00a0\u00a0 then the music played. \u00a0\u00a0 loaded again. \u00a0\u00a0 then marched to the center of the vil, fired again. \u00a0\u00a0 then commenced dancing. \u00a0\u00a0 a frenchman\ndanced on his head [2] and all danced round him for a Short time then went in to a lodge & danced a while, which pleased them verry much \u00a0\u00a0 they then brought victules from different lodges & of different kinds of diet, they brought us also a quantity of corn & Some buffalow Robes which they made us a present off. So we danced in different lodges untill late in the afternoon. \u00a0\u00a0 then a part of the men returned to the fort \u00a0\u00a0 the remainder Stayed all night in the village\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 rained a little in the eve.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 1st Jan. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 Two shot were fired from this swivel, followed by a round of small arms, to welcome the New year. Captain Lewis then gave each a glass of good old whiskey; and a short time after another was given by Captain Clarke.

\n

\nAbout 11 o'clock one of the interpreters and half of our people, went up, at the request of the natives, to the village, to begin the dance; and were followed some time after by Captain Clarke, and three more men. [3] The day was warm and pleasant. Captain Lewis in the afternoon issued another glass of whiskey; and at night Capt Clarke and part of the men returned from the village, the rest remained all night.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n January 1st 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 Tuesday. \u00a0\u00a0 2 Guns was discharged from the Swivel to celebrate the new year, a round of Small arms immediately after by each man of the party, a Glass of old ardent Spirits was given. \u00a0\u00a0 a short time after Capt. Clark gave another. \u00a0\u00a0 about 10 oClock one of the Intrepters & one half of the party went up to the 1st village of Mandans\n [4] by their request to dance. \u00a0\u00a0 Some time after Capt. Clark and 3 more men Came up also. \u00a0\u00a0 the day was warm and pleasant. \u00a0\u00a0 in the afternoon Capt. Lewis Gave another glass. \u00a0\u00a0 in the evening Capt. Clark & Some of the party came home & Some Stayed all night.

\n

\nJanuary 1st Tuesday \u00a0\u00a0 This being the first day of the Year, we Early this morning fired off our Swivels twice, and immediately after, fired a Volley of small Arms, in honor of the day.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Captain Lewis gave each of the party a Glass of spirit, which was a short time after, repeated by Captain Clark, about 10 o'Clock A. M. One of our Interpreters, and one half of the Men of our party; went up to the first Village of the Mandan Nation, by request of their Chiefs, to dance, they having a desire to see our manner of dancing. \u00a0\u00a0 The party had not been long arrived at this Village, when Captain Clarke and three more of the Men arrived.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Men commenced dancing, which the Natives much admir'd, frequently signifying their approbation by a Whoop they gave, In the evening Captain Clark and some of the Men returned, and part of the Men staid all Night at the Village\u2014\n\n\n\n
