\n October 22, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n 22nd of October 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 last night at about 1 oClock I was violently attacked with Rhumetism in my neck, which was so violently I could not move, Cap L. applied a hot Stone raped in flannel which gave temperry ease, we passed a War party of Tetons on their way as we Supposed to the Mandans of 12 men on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we gave them nothing and refused to put them across the river, passed 2 old Villages at the mouth of a large Creek L. S [1] and a Small Island at the head of which is a bad place, an old Village on the S. S. and the upper of the 6 Villages the Mandans occupied about 25 years ago [2] \u00a0\u00a0 this village was entirely cut off by the \n Sioux\n & one of the others nearly, the Small Pox distroyed great Numbers

22nd October Monday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\nlast night at 1 oClock I was violently and Suddinly attacked with the Rhumitism in the neck which was So violent I could not move \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. [Lewis] applied a hot Stone raped in flannel, which gave me some temporry ease,\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out early, the morning Cold \u00a0\u00a0 at 7 oClock we Came too at a Camp of Teton\n Seaux on the L. S. \u00a0\u00a0 those people 12 in number were naikd and had the appearanc of war, we have every reason to believ that they are going or have been to Steel horses from the Mandins, they tell two Stories, we gave them nothing \u00a0\u00a0 after takeing brackfast proceeded on\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 my Neck is yet verry painfull at times Spasms. \u00a0\u00a0 [NB: Passed old Mandan village near which we lay, another at 4 miles one at 8 miles (4 miles further) at mouth of large creek all on Larboard Side.]

\n

\nCamped on the L Side, [3] passed an Island Situated on the L. Side at the\nhead of which & Mandans village S. S. [NB: 2 miles above] we passd a bad place\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 [NB: \u2329above the island 2 miles from last village\u232a] \u00a0\u00a0 The hunters killed a buffalow bull, they Say out of about 300 buffalow which they Saw, they \u2329only\u232a did not See one Cow. Great Deel of Beaver Sign. Several Cought every night.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 22nd Oct. Some Snow last night. \u00a0\u00a0 the frenchman in the cannoe who are in company with us have Caught Several large Beaver every night for Several nights back. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly Cloudy & cool this morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on at 9 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 we met a 11 Indians of the Souix nation yankton tribe. \u00a0\u00a0 they were naked & Sd. they were on their way down the River \u00a0\u00a0 Gave them Some meat and proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 at 11 oC. I went out hunting. \u00a0\u00a0 the after part of the day pleasant. I Saw on the hills flocks of Goats & Buffaloes. Several Salt Runs in the vallies. I killed a Buffalow and Returned to the Boat in the evening. Camped on the South Side.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Monday 22nd. \u00a0\u00a0 Some snow fell last night, and the morning was cloudy and cold. We embarked early and went on. At 9 we saw 11 Indians of the Sioux nation coming down from the Mandans, who, notwithstanding the coldness of the weather, had not an article of clothing except their breech-clouts. At 1 o'clock the day became clear and pleasant and we encamped at night on the south side.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nMonday October 22d \u00a0\u00a0 This morning was Cold & Cloudy, We set off about 9 oClock A. M. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed on, and saw Eleven of the Souix Indians coming down the River in Canoes, from the Mandan Nation, at One oClock P. M. the weather cleared off and became pleasant \u00a0\u00a0 In the Evening we encamped on the South side of the River.\u2014\n\n\n

1. Most of these villages, in Morton and Burleigh counties, North Dakota, are marked on Atlas map 28. The creek\u2014Hunting Creek on the Atlas map\u2014is Square Butte Creek, in Morton County. MRC map 50; MRY map 142. The most conspicuous village in this locale is the Boley site, which appears to be a late eighteenth century Mandan site. It lies half-way between the camp of October 21 and the mouth of Square Butte Creek. Will & Hecker, 103\u20136. (back)
\n
2. The village is today's Double Ditch\n Mandan site, now a North Dakota State Historic Site. Will & Spinden, 82 passim. (back)
\n
3. Probably in southeast Oliver County, North Dakota, just above the Morton County line. Atlas map 28; MRC map 50. (back)
\n
4. \"L. S.\" in the Field Notes. \"S. S.\" is probably correct; see Atlas map 28. (back)
