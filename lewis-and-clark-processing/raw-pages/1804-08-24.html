\n August 24, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n24th August \u2329Sunday\u232a Friday 1804. Some rain last night & this morning, we Set out at the usial time and proceeded on the Same Course of last night \u00a0\u00a0 Continued S. 48\u00b0 W. 2\u00bc mes. to the Commencement of a Blue Clay Bliff on L S. about 180 or 190 feet high \u00a0\u00a0 West under rugged Bluffs 1\u00be ms. passing Several Small Dreens [drains], falling into the river \u00a0\u00a0 those Bluffs has been lately on fire and is yet verry Hott, [1] Great appearance of Coal, & imence quantities of Cabalt in Side of that part of [2] the Bluff which Sliped in, on the Sides of the hill great quantities of a kind of Current or froot resembling the Current in appearance much richer and finer flavd. \u00a0\u00a0 grows on a Scrub resembling a Damsen and is now fine and makes a Delightfull Tart [3] \u00a0\u00a0 above this Bluff I took my Servent and a french boy I have and walked on Shore \u00a0\u00a0 I killed a Deer which york Packed on his back \u00a0\u00a0 In the evening I Killed two Buck Elk and wounded two others which I could not pursue by the Blood as my ball was So Small \u2329I\u232a to bleed them well, my boys each Shot an elk\u2014 [4] \u00a0\u00a0 it was late and I Crossed a Point Struck the river above and halted the boat and 12 men went out brought in the meat \u00a0\u00a0 all the after part of the day it rained \u00a0\u00a0 we are all wet. Capt Lewis and my Self Concluded to visit a High Hill Situated in an emence Plain three Leagues N. 20\u00b0 W. from the mouth of White Stone river, this hill apear to be of a Conic form and by all the different Nations in this quater is Supposed to be a place of Deavels or [5] that they are in human form with remarkable large heads and about 18 inches high; that they \u2329remarkably\u232a are very watchfull and ar armed with Sharp arrows with which they can kill at a great distance; they are said to kill all persons who are so hardy as to attemp to approach the hill; they \u2329have a\u232a state that tradition informs them that ma[n]y indians have suffered by these little people and among others that three Maha men fell a sacrefice to their murceyless fury not meany years since\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 so much do the Mahas\n Souix\n Ottoes and other neibhbouring nations believe this fable that no consideration is sufficient to induce them to approach this hill. [6]\n

24th August Friday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\t \nSome rain last night, a Continuation this morning; we Set out at the usial time and proceeded on the Course of last night to the (1) Commencement of a blue Clay Bluff of 180 or 190 feet high on the L. S. Those Bluffs appear to have been laterly on fire, and at this time is too hot for a man to bear his hand in the earth at any debth, gret appearance of Coal. An emence quantity of Cabalt or a Cristolised Substance which answers its discription is on the face of the Bluff\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Great quantities of a kind of berry resembling a Current except double the Sise and Grows on a bush like a Privey, and the Size of a Damsen deliciously flavoured & makes delitefull Tarts, this froot is now ripe, I took my Servent and a french boy and Walked on Shore \u00a0\u00a0 Killed Two Buck Elks and a faun, and intersepted the Boat and had all the meat butchered and in by Sun Set at which time it began to rain and rained hard, Cap Lewis & my Self walk out & got Verry wet, a Cloudey rainey night,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 In my absence the Boat Passed a Small (2) River Called by the Indians White Stone River. [10] \u00a0\u00a0 this river is about 30 yards wide and runs thro: a Plain & Prarie in its whole Course \u00a0\u00a0 In a northerley direction from the mouth of this Creek in an imence Plain a high Hill is Situated, and appears of a Conic form and by the different nations of Indians in this quarter is Suppose to be the residence of Deavels. \u00a0\u00a0 that they are in human form with remarkable large heads and about 18 Inches high, that they are Very watchfull, and are arm'd with Sharp arrows with which they Can Kill at a great distance; they are Said to Kill all persons who are So hardy as to attempt to approach the hill; they State that tradition informs them that many Indians have Suffered by those little people and among others three \n Mahar\n men fell a Sacrefise to their murceyless fury not many years Since\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 So much do the Maha, Souis, Ottoes and other neighbouring nations believe this fable that no Consideration is Suffecient to induce them to apporach the hill

[Clark]

[undated, ca. August 24, 1804] [13]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n(1) About the center of this Sand Island the river of white Stone (as Called by Mr. Evins\n Kenvill R.[)] [14] falls in on the Stard. Side \u00a0\u00a0 it appear to be about 25 or 30 yards Wide; at the mouth of this river 10 Indians had latterly cross Supposed to be Soues, the part of a band which are at war with the \n Mahars, This Soues nation are divided into bands Som 100 to 500 men in a band \u00a0\u00a0 at peace with eath other, ther Interest & prejudices different, for instance one band the most envetterate enimy of the mahars, all the other Bands in the greatest harmony with that nation and even go with thim to War, those Soues, follow the Buffalow, & Kill them on foot, they pack their Dogs, which carry their Bedn. [burden?]\n\n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 24th \u00a0\u00a0 Some Small Showers of rain the latter part of last night. \u00a0\u00a0 rainy morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off about Sunrise, proceeded on past a high Bottom of wood land large Cottonwood, Elms &.C. Some Small Sized timber on N. S: near the river.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Smooth praries back from the River. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted \u00a0\u00a0 took Breakfast at high Ragged Bluffs on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 a nomber of round knobs on Sd. Bluffs covered with verry Short Grass. Some ceeder on the edge of the Bluff \u00a0\u00a0 along under those Bluffs we found a great quantity of red berries [15] which grows on a handsome bush about as high as I could reach. \u00a0\u00a0 these Berries are a little Sour (& are called Rabbit berries) (English) But pleasant to the taste \u00a0\u00a0 we found also a burning bank or Bluff which was verry high & had fire in it. \u00a0\u00a0 it had a Sulpheras Smell, we found in it a great quantity of\nasney & a great quantity of different kinds of mineral Substance, &.c. Capt. Clark went out hunting on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed the upper end of the Butiful Bottom prarie. \u00a0\u00a0 there the high butiful prarie commenced which is extensive & Smooth. Back about 2 miles we are informed that their is a verry high hill called The Hills of the little \u2329Christian\u232a Devils by the natives & they amagan that it is inhabited by little people with Big heads & they are afraid to go up to them for fear they will shoot them with their Bows & arrows, we passed the mouth of White Stone River, which came in above the high prarie \u00a0\u00a0 their is large points of land covered with Timber on boath sides of the river Such as cottenwood ash Elm &.C\u2014. Capt. Clark joined us towards evening \u00a0\u00a0 had killed 2 Buck Elk & 1 faun. Capt. Clark & Capt. Lewis & 10 more of the party in order to fetch the meat in, they returned with the meat, which was jurked \u00a0\u00a0 at the Same time we had a fine Shower of Rain which lasted abt. half an hour, at which place we Camped on South Side. \u00a0\u00a0 the Musquetoes Troublesome.\n\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 24th. \u00a0\u00a0 This morning was cloudy with some rain. Captain Clarke went by land. We passed cedar [16] bluffs on the north side, a part of which were burning; and there are here to be found mineral substances of various kinds. There is also a quantity of small red berries, the Indian name for which in English means rabbit berries. They are handsome small berries and grow upon bushes about 10 feet high. Captain Clarke came to us and had killed two elk and a fawn, we passed a creek called White-stone creek landed and remained here all night to jirk our meat.\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 24th \u00a0\u00a0 Some Small Showers of rain last night \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off about Sun rise. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed rugged Bluffs on the S. S. where we found Some red berreys which they call Rabbit berrys. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a handsom prarie on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 in this prarie we are informed that their is a high hill which they call the hill of little Devills. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed the mouth of a large Creek on N. S. called white Stone River which is abt. 50 yards wide & extends a Great distance in to the Countrey, we Camped on the S. S. of the Missouri.\u2014

\n

\nFriday August 24th \u00a0\u00a0 We had some showers of Rain during the night. \u00a0\u00a0 we set off about sun rise and passed a ragged bluff laying on the South side of the River, where we found some Berries, which is called Rabbit berries.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We passed a handsome Priari, laying on the North side of the River, not far from the Priari, is a high hill, which is called little Devil, we likewise passed a large Creek, lying on the North side call'd white stone River, which is 50 Yards wide at its mouth, and runs a great distance in the Country. \u00a0\u00a0 in the evening we encamped on the South side of the Mesouri River,\n\n\n

1. As late as 1877, some scientists believed that the bluff was a true volcano caused to erupt when the flooding Missouri River poured water onto molten rock in subterranean caverns. By 1900, investigations proved that the eruptions were due to the heat of oxidation of damp pyritiferous and carbonaceous Carlile shale on fresh exposures provided by the rapid erosion of the river bluffs. Threet; Barbour, 231\u201332. This bluff was later called the \"Ionia volcano,\" after the now defunct town of Ionia, Dixon County, Nebraska. Coues (HLC), 1:84 n. 52; Nebraska Guide, 395; Atlas map 17; MRC map 29; MRR map 80. (back)
