\n October 25, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n October 25th \u2329Monday\u232a Friday 1805
\u00a0
\n \n

\nA Cold morning, we deturmined to attempt the Chanel \u00a0\u00a0 after brackfast I took down all the party below the bad places with a load & one Canoe \u00a0\u00a0 passed well, a 2d passed well \u00a0\u00a0 I had men on the Shore with ropes to throw in in Case any acidence happened at the Whirl &c\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the Inds on the rocks veiwing us \u00a0\u00a0 the 3rd Canoe nearly filled with water \u00a0\u00a0 we got her Safe to Shore. The last Canoe Came over well which to me was truly gratifying \u00a0\u00a0 Set out and had not passed 2 mils before 3 Canoes run against a rock in the river with great force \u00a0\u00a0 no damg. \u00a0\u00a0 \u2329at\u232a met with a 2d Chief of the nation from hunting, we Smoked with him and his party and gave a medal of The Small Size & Set out \u00a0\u00a0 passed great numbers of rocks, good water and Came to at a high \u2329bluff\u232a point of rocks below the mouth of a Creek which falls in on the Lard Side and head up towards the high Snow mountain to the S W. \u00a0\u00a0 this Creek [1] is 20 yards wide [2] and has Some beaver Signs at its mouth \u00a0\u00a0 river about \u00bd a mile wide and Crouded with Sea otters, & drum was Seen this evening \u00a0\u00a0 we took possession of a high Point of rocks to defend our Selves in Case the threts of those Indians below Should be put in execution against us. Sent out Some hunters to look if any Signs of game, one man killed a Small deer & Several others Seen \u00a0\u00a0 I killed a goose, and Suped hartily on venison & goose. Camped on the rock [3] \u00a0\u00a0 guard under the hill.

[Clark]

\n \n
\n October 25th Friday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\na cool morning \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis and my Self walked down to See the place the Indians pointed out as the worst place in passing through the gut, which we found difficuelt of passing without great danger, but as the portage was impractiable with our large Canoes, we Concluded to Make a portage of our most valuable articles and run the canoes thro \u00a0\u00a0 accordingly on our return divided the party Some to take over the Canoes, and others to take our Stores across a portage of a mile to a place on the Chanel below this bad whorl & Suck, with Some others I had fixed on the Chanel with roapes to throw out to any who Should unfortunately meet with difficuelty in passing through; great number of Indians viewing us from the high rocks under which we had to pass, the 3 first Canoes passed thro very well, the 4th nearly filled with water, the last passed through by takeing in a little water, \u2329we\u232a thus Safely below what I conceved to be the worst part of this Chanel, felt my Self extreamly gratified and pleased. \u00a0\u00a0 we loaded the Canoes & Set out, and had not proceeded, more than two mile before the unfortunate Canoe which filled crossing the bad place above, run against a rock and was in great danger of being lost, This Chanel is through a hard rough black rock, from 50\u2013100 yards wide. [6] Swelling and boiling in a most tremendious maner Several places on which the Indians inform me they take the Salmon as fast as they wish; we passed through a deep bason to the stard Side of 1 mile below which the River narrows and divided by a rock \u00a0\u00a0 The Curent we found quit jentle, here we met with our two old Chiefs who had been to a village below to Smoke a friendly pipe, and at this place they met the Cheif & party from the village above [7] on his return from hunting all of whome were then crossing over their horses, we landed to Smoke a pipe with this Chief whome we found to be a bold pleasing looking man of about 50 years of age dressd. in a war jacket a cap Legins & mockersons. \u00a0\u00a0 he gave us Some meat of which he had but little and informed us he in his rout met with a war party of Snake Indians from the great river of the S. E. which falls in a few miles above and had a fight. \u00a0\u00a0 we gave this Chief a Medal, &c. a parting Smoke with our two faithful friends the Chiefs who accompanied us from the head of the river, (who had purchased a horse each with 2 robes and intended to return on horse back) \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on down the water fine, rocks in every derection for a fiew miles when the river widens and becoms a butifull jentle Stream of about half a mile wide, Great numbers of the Sea Orter [NB: or Seals] about those narrows and both below and above. \u00a0\u00a0 we Came too, under a high point of rocks on the Lard. Side below a creek of 20 yards wide and much water, as it was necessary to make Some Selestial observations we formed our Camp on the top of a high point of rocks, which forms a kind of \u2329artif\u232a fortification in the Point between the river & Creek, with a boat guard, this Situation we Concieve well Calculated for defence, and Conveniant to hunt under the foots of the mountain to the West & S. W. [8] where timber of different kinds grows, and appears to be handsom Coverts for the Deer, in oke woods, Sent out hunters to examine for game \u00a0\u00a0 G. D. [9] Killed a Small Deer & other Saw much Sign, I killed a goose in the creek which was verry fat\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 one of the guard saw a Drum fish [10] to day as he Conceved \u00a0\u00a0 our Situation well Calculated to defend \u2329us\u232a our Selves from any designs of the natives, Should They be enclined to attack us.

\n

\nThis litle Creek heads in the range of mountains which run S S W & N W for a long distance on which is Scattering pine white Oake &c. The Pinical of the round toped mountain which we Saw a Short distance below the forks of this river is S. 43\u00b0 W. of us and abt 37 miles, it is at this time toped with Snow \u00a0\u00a0 we called this the \n falls mountain\n or \n Timm\n mountain. [NB: This the Mount Hood of Vancouver\n ] \u00a0\u00a0 The face of the Countrey, on both Side of the river above and about the falls, is Steep ruged and rockey open and contain but a Small preportion of erbage, no timber a fiew bushes excepted, The nativs at the upper falls raft their timber down \n Towarnehooks River & those at the narrows take theirs up the river to the lower part of the narrows from this Creek, and Carry it over land 3 miles to their houses &c. \u00a0\u00a0 at the mouth of this creek Saw Some beaver Sign, and a Small wolf in a Snare Set in the willows \u00a0\u00a0 The Snars of which I saw Several made for to catch wolves, are made as follows vz: a long pole which will Spring is made fast with bark to a willow, on the top of this pole a String [NB: Described elsewhere] [11]\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 25th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we carried some of our baggage by land about one mile past the worst of the narrows. \u00a0\u00a0 then took one canoe at a time down the narrows and whorl pools. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes filled with water running through the narrows we got all below and loaded the canoes \u00a0\u00a0 we have now 16 bags of Sammon on board \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oClock P. M. we Set out and proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 the narrows continued about 2 miles and verry rapid. \u00a0\u00a0 2 Small Islands of Sollid rock Stood in these narrows \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes was near dashing in peaces by Strikeing hir bow against the upper point of one of them. \u00a0\u00a0 the River between these narrows and the great falls rises at high water 48 feet perpenticular by its being confined by the different narrows. \u00a0\u00a0 near the lower end of the narrows we Saw a war party of Indians which had jest Swam the River to the Stard Side with their horses. \u00a0\u00a0 they had some vension &C with them. \u00a0\u00a0 we halted a fiew minutes and our officers Smoaked with them \u00a0\u00a0 they gave us some bears oil and a little vension and Some fresh fish. \u00a0\u00a0 we went on \u00a0\u00a0 2 chiefs told us that their was a nation below which they were at war with and they did not wish to go any further with us so our officers Settled with them and they returned back to their nation. \u00a0\u00a0 we then went on \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Some drumm fish [12] jumping in the River \u00a0\u00a0 the River gitting Smoth. Saw pine and oak timber near the Shores \u00a0\u00a0 we can Still See the round high mountain Some distance a head yet. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped below \nthe mouth of a creek on a point of rocks on the Lard. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 the country timbred back a little from the River.\u2014\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 25th. \u00a0\u00a0 We found there were bad rapids in the narrows and therefore carried over part of our baggage by land, about three quarters of a mile; and then took the canoes over, one at a time. In going over one of them \nfilled with water, on account of which we were detained three hours. The rapids continued 3 or 4 miles, when the river became more placid. At night we came to a place where there is a considerable quantity of timber on the hills; both oak and pine, and encamped at the mouth of a creek on the south side. The natives about here are, or pretend to be, very uneasy, and say the Indians below will kill us. We purchased from them a quantity of dried pounded fish, which they had prepared in that way for sale. They have six scaffolds of a great size for the purpose of drying their fish on.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 25th Oct. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 a clear morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we carrd. Some of our baggage about a mile, which took us below the worst of the rapids, then took one canoe down the rapids and narrows where the whole channel is confined in a narrow channel only about 25 yards wide. \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes nearly fi[lled] running through the rapids waves & whorl pools. \u00a0\u00a0 we got all the canoes down and loaded them. \u00a0\u00a0 we have bought a large quantity about 16 common bags of pounded Sammon Some white bread cramberies &c. \u00a0\u00a0 about 3 oClock we Set out and proceeded on down the narrows which lasted abt. 2 miles verry rapid. \u00a0\u00a0 2 [13] Small Islands of Solid rocks Stood in the channel \u00a0\u00a0 one of the canoes ran hir bow aggainst the point and glanced off without Injury. \u00a0\u00a0 the water or River between these narrows and the falls, rises at high water 48 feet perpinticular by its being confined by the different narrows. \u00a0\u00a0 a little [below] or at the lower end of the narrows we Saw a war party of Indians, with horses. \u00a0\u00a0 they had deer & bear meat with them \u00a0\u00a0 the head chief had on a jacket that was made of Some kind of worked Splits which would defend off the arrows. \u00a0\u00a0 our Capts. gave him a meddle, then he gave our Capts. Some bears oil and a fresh Sammon \u00a0\u00a0 our 2 chiefs came to us and told us that their was a nation below that which had a design to kill them and us \u00a0\u00a0 So they left us in order to return to their own village again.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we then \nproceeded on about eight miles \u00a0\u00a0 the hils high \u00a0\u00a0 Some pine and oak timbr. to be Seen \u00a0\u00a0 the River got Smooth. \u00a0\u00a0 we Campd. on a high point of rocks little below the mouth of a creek on the Lard. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 timbered country hack from the River each Side. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw drumm fish jump in Rivr.

\n

\nFriday October 25th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning clear & pleasant; Our Men that were well (all excepting a Guard left with our Canoes,) set off with the loading to a place, below the worst of the rapids; about one Mile distance, which we carried on our backs, The party that were left with the Canoes, took one of our Canoes down the rapids, and narrows of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 The channel of the river lays in a narrow place, not about 25 Yards wide, & with difficulty they got through it. \u00a0\u00a0 they then returned, & brought the remainder of the Canoes to us, at the place where we had deposited our loading. \u00a0\u00a0 One of the Canoes nearly filled, in passing through the Rapid waves & whirlpools in the Rapids.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We found an Indian village, laying below these falls, or rapids, \u2329from whom\u232a our officers purchased from the Indians that resided in this Village 16 bags of pounded Salmon; some bread made out of Roots, Cranberries &ca. \u00a0\u00a0 The whole of our party having come to us below the rapids; we loaded our Canoes & About 3 o'Clock P. M. & we set out, and proceeded on down through the rapids, which lasted about 2 Miles further & the water running very Rapid, the whole way.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We found 2 small Islands of solid rock, which lay in the channel of the River in these Rapids. \u00a0\u00a0 One of our Canoes run her bow against the point of one of these Islands, & glanced off without receiving any injury.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The River between the falls & narrows at that place rises at high water 48 feet perpendicular, which is occasion'd by the water being confined by the different narrow places, & particularly at the lower end of them.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We came too, at the lower end of these falls, and halted for a short time, at which place a Warr party of Indians, came to us. \u00a0\u00a0 These Indians were all on horse back, & had Deer & bear meat with them. \u00a0\u00a0 Our officers gave their Chief a medal, & he in return gave them some Bears Oil & a fresh Salmon.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 This Warr party of Indians staid with us but a short time. \u00a0\u00a0 The Two Indian Chiefs who descended the River with us, told us by signs, that there was a Nation of Indians, that resided on this River below us, who would certainly kill them; & the whole of our party, and that they must leave us, in order to return to their own Village again. \u00a0\u00a0 These Indians left \nus at this place, after taking a friendly leave. \u00a0\u00a0 Our Officers gave them some presents & they left us much pleased.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 The Chief or head Man that had been with us of the Warr party; wore a curious kind of Jacket, This Jacket was made out of a kind of Splits, which were worked in such a manner, as to defend him against the Arrows shot by his Enemies.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, & went about 8 Miles further down the River. \u00a0\u00a0 The hills the whole of this distance, were high on both sides of the River, and we saw some pine & Oak timber, The River ran smooth all this way, We encamped near a high point of Rocks, a small distance below the mouth of a creek, which lay on the South side of the River; The Country laying a small distance back, from where we are encamped, is cover'd with Timber.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 And the Land on both sides of the River, is barren land \u00a0\u00a0 We saw a Number of fish jumping in the River from where we are encamped which we supposed to be Drum fish.\u2014\n\n\n
