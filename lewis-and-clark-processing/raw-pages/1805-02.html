\n February 1, 1805\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
1st of February Friday 1805\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\na cold windey Day \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters returnd. haveing killed only one Deer, a war Chief of the \n Me ne tar ras\n Came with Some Corn \u00a0\u00a0 requested to have a War hatchet made, & requested to be allowed to go to war against the Souis & Ricarres who had Killed a mandan Some time past\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we refused, and gave reassons, which he verry readily assented to, and promised to open his ears to all we Said \u00a0\u00a0 this man is young and named (\n Seeing Snake\u2014Mar-book, She-ah-O-ke-ah\n [)] [1] \u00a0\u00a0 this mans woman Set out & he prosued her, in the evening\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday 1st day of Feby. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 clear & cold. \u00a0\u00a0 one man went out a Short distance to hunt and killed a Deer \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters who went out yesterday returned \u00a0\u00a0 the weather being bad they killed nothing.\u2014\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Friday 1st Feb. 1805. \u00a0\u00a0 A cold day. About 11 our hunters came home, but had killed nothing. One of the men at the fort went out a short distance, and killed a small deer. On the next day he went out and killed another deer. This and the third were cold.\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nFriday February 1st \u00a0\u00a0 This morning we had pleasant weather. \u00a0\u00a0 about 11 o'Clock A. M. our Hunters came to the Fort, they had killed a number of Elk and Deer,\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 One of our Men went out a small distance, and Shott a deer which he brought to the Fort, he then went out again and kill'd another Deer which he likewise brought into the Fort.\n\n

\n \n
\n \n \n
\n
1. This seems to be from the Hidatsa words maap\u00fak\u0161a, \"snake\" and k\u00eda, \"to fear,\" perhaps translating to \"fears the snake.\" (back)
