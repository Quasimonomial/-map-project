\n June 7, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday 7th of June 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed the head of the Isd from the Isd. \u2329to a pt on Lbd Side\u232a N. 61\u00b0 W. to the mouth of a Creek Called big monitu\n [1] on St. Sd. 4\u00bd ms. psd. a Sand bar in the river, Som Buffalow Sign \u00a0\u00a0 Sent out George Drewyer & Newmon to hunt \u2329them\u232a \u00a0\u00a0 Capt Lewis and 6 men went to a Lick up this Creek on the right Side over 2 mes. & 2 other not far above \u00a0\u00a0 the water runs out of the bank & not verry Strong. \u00a0\u00a0 3 to 500 G[allons] for a bushell.

\n

\nS 88\u00b0 W. 2 Miles to a pt. on Lbd. Side, high bluff on the Stbd. Side, Monitou Creek is 30 yds. Wide at the mouth, passed a painted part of a Projecting rock [2] \u00a0\u00a0 we found ther a Den of rattle Snakes, [3] Killed 3 proceeded \u00a0\u00a0 on \u00a0\u00a0 passed, S 81\u00b0 W 4 ms. to a pt. on S. Side \u00a0\u00a0 passed an Island in the Middle of the river, [4] S. 87\u00b0 W. to a pt. of high Land on the L. S: pass:'g over the Middle of a willow Island, ms. 3\u00bd \u00a0\u00a0 proceed on \u00bd a mile on this Course a[nd] Camped at the mouth of Good womans river\n [5] on the S. S. about 35 yds wide, & navagable Som D[istance]. \u00a0\u00a0 our hunters brought in 3 bear [6] this evening\u2014& infd. that the Countrey between this R. & the Monitou R is rich and well watered, Capt. Lewis went out an hour this evening.

\n

[Clark]

\n June 7th Thursday 1804\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out early passed the head of the Island opposit which we Camped last night, and brackfast at the Mouth of a large Creek on the S. S. of 30 yds wide Called big Monetou,\n from the pt. of the Isd. or Course of last night to the mouth of this Creek is N 61\u00b0 W 4\u00bd ms. a Short distance above the mouth of this Creek, is Several Courious Paintings and Carveing in the projecting rock of Limestone inlade with white red & blue flint, of a verry good quallity, [7] the Indians have taken of this flint great quantities. We landed at this Inscription and found it a Den of rattle Snakes, we had not landed 3 minutes before three verry large Snakes wer observed on the Crevises of the rocks & Killed\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 at \u2329whilst from\u232a the mouth of the last mentioned Creek Capt. Lewis took four or five men & went to Some \u2329Creeks\u232a Licks or Springs of Salt water from two to four miles up the Creek on Rt. Side \u00a0\u00a0 the water of those Springs are not Strong, Say from 4 to 600 Gs. of water for a Bushel of Salt \u00a0\u00a0 passed Some Small willow Islands and Camped at the Mouth of a Small river called \n Good Womans River \u00a0\u00a0 this river is about 35 yards wide and Said to be navagable for Perogues Several Leagues \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis with 2 men went up the Creek a Short distance. \u00a0\u00a0 our Hunters brought in three Bear this evening, and informs that the Countrey thro: which they passed from the last Creek is fine rich land, & well watered.

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday June 7th 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out at 5 OClock \u00a0\u00a0 fair day & f. wind \u00a0\u00a0 passed high Clifts & a fine large Spring which Run from under the clifts of Rocks, about 2 miles further we Come to a Creek Called the Big Devil. [8] \u00a0\u00a0 we Stayed to Breakfast. \u00a0\u00a0 Capt. Lewis & Several men went with him out to a lick, up the Creek. \u00a0\u00a0 high Bluffs on N. Side. \u00a0\u00a0 this Maneteau Creek is 30 yds. wide at the mouth. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed a pointed part of a projecting Rock \u00a0\u00a0 we found this to be a den of rattle Snakes, we killed three. \u00a0\u00a0 high Land on South Side of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 we Encamped at the mouth of good womans River\n [9] on Right \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters came in with 3 Bears [10] this evening & informed us that the Country between these Rivers is Rich\n\n

[Floyd]

\nthursday 7th June 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out 5 oclock \u00a0\u00a0 Came 2 miles \u00a0\u00a0 past Som [s]prings Comes out of Clifte \u00a0\u00a0 2 miles past a Creerk on the N Side Called the River of the Big Devil \u00a0\u00a0 one mile past a rock on the N. Side whare the pictures of the Devil and other things \u00a0\u00a0 We Kild 3 Rattel Snakes at that Rock \u00a0\u00a0 5 miles to Creek on the N Side Called \u2329pon\u232a Good woman Creek \u00a0\u00a0 Strong watter \u00a0\u00a0 past severall Isd. George Druer [X: George Drewyer or Druillard\n .] [11] Kild one Bar\u2329e\u232a \u00a0\u00a0 encampet at the mouth \u00a0\u00a0 the Land is Good well timberd, &c.\n\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nThursday 7th June 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 the hunters and Capt. Lewis went out to a buffaloe lick two miles. \u00a0\u00a0 Saw this Salt Spring, but no buffaloe. \u00a0\u00a0 on N. E. Side of the Missourie up the Monetuie river [12] in a timbred country. \u00a0\u00a0 fine bottons along each Side of the River, under brush thick. [13] \u00a0\u00a0 Killd 3 Rattel Snakes of Different Sorts at a large rock Close by the latter river \u00a0\u00a0 after roed. 10 miles \u00a0\u00a0 Campd. at the Mouth of Creek namd the \u2329Old\u232a Good woman N. E. Side of the Missurie \u00a0\u00a0 Our daily hunters [14] met us there with three Bears, One Old famel & her two Cubbs brought By G. Drewyer\u2014

\n

\nThursday June 7th \u00a0\u00a0 This morning Captain Lewis & the hunters went to a Buffalo lick, two Miles from the River, and had a view of a Salt spring, but found no Buffalo,

\n

\n Captain Lewis & the hunters proceeded to the Moniture River, (which lays on the North East side of the Mesouri River,) were they found a well timber'd country, having fine bottoms with rich soil, and along the River on each side of it, found thick brush; On their route they kill'd 3 Rattle snakes near a large Rock, close to the said River, we proceeded with the boat 10 Miles, and encamped at the Mouth of a Creek, call the good Woman Creek lying on the North east side of the Mesouri, where we were met by the party that went hunting; One of which, (G Drewer,) kill'd a She Bear, and her two Cubbs & brought them to the opposite side of the River where we were encamped \nat.\u2014\n\n
