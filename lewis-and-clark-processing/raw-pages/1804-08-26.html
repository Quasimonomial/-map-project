\n August 26, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n26th August Sunday 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 arrived at the boat at 9 oClock A. M. \u00a0\u00a0 Set out at 10 oClock after Jurking the meet & Cutting the Elk Skins for a Toe Roap and proceeded, leaveing G. Drew[yer] & Shannon to hunt the horses, \u2329at 9 miles\u232a the river verry full of Sand bars and Wide \u00a0\u00a0 Course S. 66\u00b0 W. 2 mes. to a Sand bar Makeing out from the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 N. 82\u00b0 W. 7 mes. to a pt. of willows S S \u00a0\u00a0 passd. a Island & large Sand bars on both sides \u00a0\u00a0 river wide and a Clift of White earth on the L. S of 2 ms. in length to a point of Willows on the S. S opposit \n Arch Creek\n [1] \u00a0\u00a0 above the mouth of this Creek a Chief of the Maha nataton displeased with the Conduct of Black bird the main Chief came to this place and built a Town [2] which was called by his name \n Petite Arch (or Little Bow) \u00a0\u00a0 this Town was at the foot of a Hill in a handsom Plain fronting the river and Contained about 100 huts & 200 men, the remains of this tribe Since the Death of Petite arch has joined the remaining part of the nation \u00a0\u00a0 This Creek is Small\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we apt. Pat Gass Sergeant Vice Floyd Dicesed, Geathered great quantites of Grapes & three Kinds of Plumbs, one yellow round, & one ovel, & the Common wild Plumb. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 Misquetors bad to night\u2014 [4] \u00a0\u00a0 I have apt. you [5]\n

[Lewis]

\n \n
\n Orders August 26th 1804.\n [9]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\t\n\nThe commanding officers have thought it proper to appoint Patric Gass, a Sergeant in the corps of volunteers for North Western Discovery, he is therefore to be obeyed and respected accordingly.

\n

\n Sergt. Gass is directed to take charge of the late Sergt. Floyd's mess, and immediately to enter on the discharge of such other duties, as by their previous orders been prescribed for the government of the Sergeants of this corps.

\n

\nThe Commanding officers have every reason to hope from the previous faithfull services of Sergt. Gass, that this expression of their approbation will be still further confirmed, by his vigilent attention in future to his duties as a Sergeant. \u00a0\u00a0 the Commanding officers are still further confirmed in the high opinion they had previously formed of the capacity, deligence and integrety of Sergt. Gass, from the wish expresssed by a large majority of his comrades for his appointment as Sergeant.

\n

\n \n Meriwether Lewis\n
\nCapt. 1st U' S. Regt Infty.
\n Wm Clark Cpt &.\n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 26th \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly in order to overtake the Boat. \u00a0\u00a0 we came to the Boat abt. 9 o.C. A M \u00a0\u00a0 they had not left their last nights Camp. G. Shannon had killed an Elk the evening before, they delayed to jurk it &.C; one of the horses lost \u00a0\u00a0 Drewyer & one man hunting them \u00a0\u00a0 we then proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a white clay Bluff on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 found a fine place of plumbs in a prarie N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on to the mo. of little petark (French) [(]little Bow)\n [10] \u00a0\u00a0 English S. S. abo. the hill opposite to which we camped on N. S. at petite wave formerly an old Indian village,\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Sunday 26th.\n [11] \u00a0\u00a0 Some of the men went out to dress and bring in the elk. About 10 o'clock Captain Lewis and Captain Clarke with the party accompanying them came to camp; but had not been able to discover any of those small people. The hill is in a handsome prairie: and the party saw a great many buffaloe near it. About 11 we renewed our voyage and passed some timberland on the south side; and black and white bluffs on the same side, we encamped on the north side opposite a creek called Pettit-Ark, or Little-bow.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\t\nSunday 26th \u00a0\u00a0 the Boat detained this morning to Jurk an Elk which Shannon killed, abt. 9 oClock the party returned to the Boat much fatigued \u00a0\u00a0 they informd us that their was nothing but Birds to be Seen & that it is about nine miles from the Missouri & a handsom round hill in a mence large prarie. \u00a0\u00a0 \nthey Saw a Great many Buffelow from the hill. \u00a0\u00a0 they were all most famished for water &c\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a white clay Bluff on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we found a large plumb orched [12] in a prarie N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped on a large Sand bar N. S. opposite a Creek called pet arck or little Bow where their was formerly an Indian village.\u2014 [13]\n

\n

\nSunday August 26th \u00a0\u00a0 This day we remain'd at the place that we encamp'd the precing Evening, we employ'd ourselves in Order to Jerk the Meat of an Elk, which one of our hands shot early this morning, about 9 oClock A. M. our hunting party [14] returned to the Boat much fataigued, having seen nothing on their Route but Birds, they mention'd that about 9 Miles from the Mesouri, they saw a handsome round hill and a very large Priari, They saw a number of Buffalo from the Top of the hill, The Men were almost famished for want of Water. \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and passed a white Clay bluff, lying on the South side of the River, and a Plumb Orchard in a Priari on the North side, We encamped in the Evening \u2329we encamped\u232a on a Sand barr, which was very large lying on the North side of the River, opposite to a Creek called pittarc or little bow, where there was formerly an Indian village\n\n\n

\n \n \n
\n
1. Present Bow Creek, Cedar County, Nebraska. \"Pettite Arch\" and \"Village\" appear on Evans's map 1 (Atlas map 7). Atlas map 18; MRC map 29; MRR map 81. (back)
\n
2. An Omaha Indian village called \"Bad Village\" was built near the mouth of Bow Creek in the early eighteenth century. The same general location was the site of a later Omaha village, \"Little Bow\" (from the French Petite Arch of Clark). The location Clark gives for the village is questionable. Nicollet shows an \"Old Mahaw Village\" near the August 25 camp. Nicollet (MMR), 399; Lewis, 290 and n.; Wood (TL); Fletcher & La Fiesche , 1:85\u201386; Atlas map 18. Ludwickson, Blakeslee, & O'Shea, 79\u201385, discuss the historical (and confusing) record of the locations of the villages. (back)
\n
3. For these plums see previous day's entry. The identification of a third species of wild plum for this area may be in error. The \"ovel\" plum may be a variant of the common wild plum. (back)
\n
4. Probably Aedes vexans. (back)
\n
5. An incomplete sentence, apparently a draft for a document appointing Gass as sergeant. The number 9 follows this\u2014the day's mileage accumulation. (back)
\n
6. The white material probably is Niobrara Chalk which overlies the Carlile Shale (the dark material) in this region. Both are Late Cretaceous in age. The white color might refer to a calcareous zone within the Carlile. (back)
\n
7. This passage is crossed out in red, apparently by Biddle. (back)
\n
8. In Clay County, South Dakota, opposite the mouth of Bow Creek and at the lower end of later Audubon Bend. Atlas map 18; MRC map 29; MRR map 81. (back)
\n
9. From the Orderly Book in Lewis's hand, except for Clark's own signature. (back)
\n
10. \n Bow Creek, Cedar County, Nebraska. Ordway is attempting the French petit arc, \"little bow.\" (back)
\n
11. This day Clark notes, \"apt. Pat Gass a Sergt. Vice Floyd Deceased.\" Gass (or his editor) did not think his promotion worth mentioning. (back)
\n
12. The day before, Clark noted the abundance of plums, including the common wild plum, Prunus americana Marsh., and big-tree plum, P. mexicana Wats. (back)
\n
13. \n Bow Creek, Cedar County, Nebraska; \"pet arck\" is Whitehouse's version of the French petit arc, \"little bow.\" On the village, see Clark's entry for this day. The camp was in Clay County, South Dakota. (back)
\n
14. This is the captains' party which the day before the copyist had said returned that day. (back)
