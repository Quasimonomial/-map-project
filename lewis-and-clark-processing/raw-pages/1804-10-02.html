[Clark]

\n \n

\n 2nd of October Tuesday 1804, \u00a0\u00a0 Mr. Vallie Came on board, Lat. 44\u00b0 19' 36 N. \u00a0\u00a0 we observed Some Indians on a hill on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one Came to the river & fired off his gun and asked us to come [hole] he wish us to go to his Camp near at hand [1] \u00a0\u00a0 we refused, passed a large Island [2] on the S. S., here we expected the Tetons would attempt to Stop us, and prepared for action, &c. \u00a0\u00a0 opposit this Island on the L. S. a Small Creek comes in, w[e] call this Caution Island, Camped on a Sand bar \u00bd mile from the main Shore [3] \u00a0\u00a0 the wind hard from the N W. \u00a0\u00a0 Cold, the current of the river less rapid, & retains less Sediment than below.

\n 2nd of October Tuesday 1804\n \n

\n

\na Violent wind all night from the S. E. \u00a0\u00a0 Slackened a little and we proceeded on. \n Mr. Jon Vallee\n Came on board and proceeded on 2 miles with us, a verry Cold morning \u00a0\u00a0 Some black clouds flying \u00a0\u00a0 took a meridian altitude & made the Lattitude \u00a0\u00a0 44\u00b0 19' 36\" North \u00a0\u00a0 this was taken at the upper part of the gouge of the Lookout bend, the Sentinal heard a Shot over the hills to the L. S. dureing the time we were Dineing on a large Sand bar. \u00a0\u00a0 the after part of this day is pleasent, at 2 oClock opposit a wood on the L. S. we observed some Indians on a hill on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one Came down to the river opposit to us and fired off his gun, & beckind. to us to Come too, we payed no attention to him \u00a0\u00a0 he followed on Some distance, we Spoke a few words to him, he wished us to go a Shore and to his Camp which was over the hill and Consisted of 20 Lodges, we excused our Selves advised him to go and here our talk of Mr. Durion \u00a0\u00a0 he enquired for traders \u00a0\u00a0 we informed him one was in the next bend below & parted, he returned\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 & we proceeded on (1) passed a large Island, \u2329on\u232a the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 here we expected the Tetons would attempt to Stop us and under that idear we prepared our Selves for action which we expected every moment. \u00a0\u00a0 opsd. this Island on the L. S. a Small Creek Comes in, This Island we call Isd. of Caution \u00a0\u00a0 we took in Some wood on a favourable Situation where we Could defend our men on Shore & (2) Camped on a Sand bar \u00bd a mile from the main Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind changed to the N. W. & rose verry high and Cold which Continud. The Current of the Missourie is less rapid & contains much less Sediment of the Same Colour.\u2014

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday 2nd Oct. 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 (we Set off as usal) \u00a0\u00a0 a frenchman came over to us this morning, we found him to be Mr. Valley, the Trador among the Souix nation \u00a0\u00a0 he could talk English. \u00a0\u00a0 he informed us that we Should not See many more of the Souix to Trouble us. \u00a0\u00a0 he came with us a Short distance & returned. \u00a0\u00a0 we proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a large Bottom on S. S. Some Timber on the edge of the river. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 o.Clock we came round a bend where we had come 20 miles round, & it was only 2 miles across by land. \u00a0\u00a0 we discovered Some Indians on the hills N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one of them came down to the River. \u00a0\u00a0 we asked him what he wanted \u00a0\u00a0 he Said (their was 20 lodges) or so in the Yanktown\n Souise language that he wanted us to come to Shore. \u00a0\u00a0 we told him we had Spoke to his chief &.C. & proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 the wind Shifted to N. W. \u00a0\u00a0 passed an Island [6] on N. S. & a creek on the S. Side. Camped on a Sand bar in the middle of the River.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 no hunting for Indians Troublesom.\u2014\n\n\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 2nd. \u00a0\u00a0 We set sail before day light. A Frenchman [7] came on board, who could speak English. He mentioned it as his opinion, that we should see no more Indians, until we should arrive at the nation of Rees. We passed a range of black bluffs on the north side and a large bottom on the south, where there was some timber on the bank of the river. About 2 o'clock we discovered some Indians on the hills on the north side, and one of them came down to the bank and fired a gun; the object or intention we did not well understand, but were ready to meet an attack. We passed black bluffs on the south side, an island covered with timber, and a handsome bottom on the north side. We halted and spoke to the Indian, who said he belonged to the Jonkta or Babarole band, [8] and that there were 20 lodges of them. We told him we had seen two of their chiefs, and given them a flag and medal. [9] \nWe passed a creek on the south side, and encamped on a sand bar in the middle of the river.\n\n\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday 2 October 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 Set off eairly. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on. \u00a0\u00a0 passed a range of black bluffs on N. S. and a large bottom on S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 about 2 oClock we discovered a nomber of Indians on the hills on N. S. \u00a0\u00a0 one of them came down on the bank of the river & fired off his Gun and cryed out. \u00a0\u00a0 we hardly new his meaning but we held ourselves in rediness \u00a0\u00a0 in case they Should attack us we were determined to fight or dye. \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Creek [10] on the S. S. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on a Sand bar in the middle of the river.

\n

\nTuesday October 2nd \u00a0\u00a0 We set off early this morning, and proceeded on, and passed a range of black bluffs, lying on the North side, and a large bottom on the South side of the River; about 2 oClock P. M. we discovered a number of Indians, on the hills on the North side of the River, One of those Indians came on the bank of the River, and fired off his Gun, and hallowed to us. \u00a0\u00a0 We hardly knew his meaning, but stood in readiness, in case \u2329they\u232a any of these Savages should attackt us, Our Officers being determin'd to proceed on our Voyage, at the risque of their lives, and the Men determin'd to support them in the attempt

\n

\nWe proceeded on, and passed a Creek lying on the South side of the River, and in the evening we encamped on a Sand barr, lying in the middle of the River\u2014\n\n\n\n\n

3. Just above Plum (Caution) Island, with Sully County, South Dakota, on the starboard shore and Dewey County on the larboard. The area is now inundated by Oahe Reservoir. Atlas map 24; MRC map 42. (back)

8. Evidently either the Yankton or Bois Brul\u00e9 divisions of the Sioux. (back)

9. Presenting medals to Indian dignitaries was a longstanding custom. Lewis and Clark carried medals of various sizes and inscriptions. The most common displayed the profile of President Jefferson on one side, while the reverse showed clasped hands and crossed tomahawk and pipe. (back)
