\n May 24, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday May the 24th 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Small Isd in the Midlle of the river, opposit the on the Lbd. Side is projecting Rock of \u00bd a mile in extent against which the Current runs, this place is called the Devils race grounds, [1] above this Coms in a Small Creek called the little quiver, [2] a Sand Island on the Stbd Side, passed Several Islands & 2 creeks, on the Stbd Side [3] a Small Island on the Lbd Side above we wer verry near loseing our Boat in Toeing \u00a0\u00a0 She Struck the Sands (which is continerly roaling) \u2329& turned\u232a the Violence of the Current was so great that the Toe roap Broke, the boat turned Broadside, as the Current Washed the Sand from under her She wheeled & lodged on the bank below as often as three times, before we got her in Deep water, nothing Saved her but [sentence unfinished]\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Clark] [4]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n\nMay 24th \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early, Killed a Deer last night. \u00a0\u00a0 examined the mens arms, & Saw that all was prepared for action, passed an [sm? i.e., small?] Island in the M. R, [Missouri River? midriver?] opposit a hard place of water called the Devill race grown, S 63\u00b0 W 4 miles to a point on the Sd: Starboard Side \u00a0\u00a0 N 68 W to a point on Lbd Side 3 ms: Passd. a Small Willow Island on the Lbd. Side to the point of a Isd. L'Side\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 S 75\u00b0 W to a point on Stbd Side 3 Miles, Passed the upper point of the Island. Crossed and in a verry bad place we got our Boat a ground & She Bocke the Toe Roap & turned the Land, [bring?] [one word illegible] the in Wheeling three times, got off returned to the head of the aforesaid Island, and Came up under a falling Bank. \u00a0\u00a0 hard water \u00a0\u00a0 this place being the worst I ever Saw, I call it the retregrade bend. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped at an old house. [5]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 24th Thursday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\nSet out early \u00a0\u00a0 passed a Verry bad part of the River Called the Deavels race ground, this is where the Current Sets against Some projecting rocks for half a mile on the Labd. Side, above this place is the mouth of a Small Creek Called \n queivere, passed Several Islands, two Small Creeks on the Stbd. Side, and passed between a Isld. an the Lbd. Shore \u00a0\u00a0 a narrow pass above this Isld is a Verry bad part of the river, we attempted to pass up under the Lbd. Bank which was falling in So fast that the evident danger obliged us to Cross between the Starbd. Side and a Sand bar in the middle of the river, we hove up near the head of the Sand bar, the Sand moveing & banking caused us to run on the Sand. The Swiftness of the Current wheeled the boat, Broke our Toe rope, and was nearly over Setting the boat, all hand Jumped out on the upper Side and bore on that Side untill the Sand washed from under the boat and wheeled on the next bank \u00a0\u00a0 by the time She wheeled a 3rd Time got a rope fast to her Stern and by the means of Swimmers was Carred to Shore and when her Stern was down whilst in the act of Swinging a third time \u2329She was Drawn\u232a into Deep water near the Shore into Deep water near the Shore, we returned, to the Island where we Set out and assended under the Bank which I have just mentioned, as falling in, here George Drewyer & Willard, two of our men who left us at St. Charles to Come on by land joined us, we Camped about 1 mile above where we were So nearly being lost, on the Labd Side at a Plantation. \u00a0\u00a0 all in Spirits. This place I call the retragrade bend as we were obliged to fall back 2 miles\n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 24th. \u00a0\u00a0 We continued our voyage, and encamped at night on the south side. This day our boat turned in a ripple, and nearly upset.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n[Thu]rsday 24th May 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly and proceeded on as usal \u00a0\u00a0 passd. timbred land on each Side \u00a0\u00a0 Current of the river Swift \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the SouthSide.

\n

\nThursday May 24th \u00a0\u00a0 We started early this morning, having fair weather; and proceeded on as usual. \u00a0\u00a0 Passed a country that appeared to be well timbered, on both sides of the River. \u00a0\u00a0 The current of the River still being strong, and our course nearly West, in the evening we encamped on the south side of the River, the banks of which was high.\n\n
