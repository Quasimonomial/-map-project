\n December 6, 1803\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nDecember 6th \u00a0\u00a0 at the mo. of the Leagle\u2014. \u00a0\u00a0 A Dark wet morning \u00a0\u00a0 I was informed this morning that Capt Lewis passd by this place yesterday on his way to St Louis accompanied by an Officer. [1] I set out this morning \u2329about\u232a at 11 oClock, after receiving a Small Supply of Provisions on Board; (1) passed a Bieaue [bayou] which Comunicates with Leagle Creek \u00a0\u00a0 (2) passed the Lower Point of the first Island at \u00bd a mile also the upper point at 2\u00bd miles, a Small creek of runing water opposite on the Larbd. Side. [2] \u00a0\u00a0 Passed a Small Isld. near the middle of the river nearly opposite the upper point of the last on the Larbd. Side of this Island the current is verry Swift, Seting imediately against the high lands, which terminates in a Bluff at the river \u00a0\u00a0 (3) pass'd a Small Creek of running water on the Larbd. Side above the last Island \u00a0\u00a0 (4) passd. the mouth of a Small Creek Lbd Side Called little Rock Creek, [3] Several Settlements are formed on this Creek also on the River above its mouth \u00a0\u00a0 (5) passed two Islands [4] on the Larbd. Side, one of those Islands large and lies near the main Shore \u00a0\u00a0 the other verry Small with a large Sand bar\u2014; the head of those two Ilds. are imedeately below the Meremeck River\n [5] (6) passed the mouth of the Merrenek and came to at a farm \u00be of a mile above, [6] this River is about 110 yards wide at its mouth.
