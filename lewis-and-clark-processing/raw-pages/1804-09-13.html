\n \n 13th Septr. Thursday 1804\n

\n

\na Dark Drizzley Day, G D Cought 4 Beaver last night \u00a0\u00a0 the winds from the N W. Cold \u00a0\u00a0 Set out early and proceeded on verry well \u00a0\u00a0 passed a number of Sand bars, Capt Lewis killed a Porcupin on a Cotton treee fieeding on the leaves & bowers of the Said tree, the water is verry Shallow [X: in places] being Crouded with Sand bars \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the \u2329L\u232a S. Side under a Bluff. [3] \u00a0\u00a0 the Bluffs on the S. S. not So much impregnated with mineral as on the L. S. [4] \u00a0\u00a0 muskeetors verry troublesom\u2014.\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Lewis]

\n \n
September 13th [5]\n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nKilled a bluewinged teal [EC: Querquedula discors] and a Porcupine [EC: Erethizon dorsatum]; found it [the porcupine] in a Cottonwood tree near the river on the Lard. Shore\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the leaves of the Cottonwood were much distroyed\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 as were those of the Cottonwood trees in it's neighbourhood. I therefore supposed that it fed on the folage of trees at this season, the flesh of this anamal is a pleasant and whoalsome food\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 the quills had not yet obtained their usual length\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 it has four long toes, before \u2329and\u232a on each foot, and the same number behind with the addition of one short one on each hind foot on the inner side. \u00a0\u00a0 the toes of the feet are armed with long black nails particularly the fore feet\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 they weigh from 15 to 20 lbs\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 they resemble the slowth very much in the form of their hands, or fore feet. \u00a0\u00a0 their teeth and eyes are like the bever\u2014\n\n

[Ordway]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 13th Sept. 1804. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set off eairly \u00a0\u00a0 proceeded on \u00a0\u00a0 passed high hills on N. S. & a Bottom prarie. Some part covered with Timber Grape vines covered with ripe grapes.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 pasd. a black Bluff on S. S. & Broken hills & a run of allum & copperass water.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 Some Musquetoes, rainy.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer caught 4 Beaver in his Traps last night. \u00a0\u00a0 myself Sergt. Pryor & Shannon walked on Shore S. S. in order to get Some plumbs in a bottom prarie. \u00a0\u00a0 we found pleanty but they were not quite ripe. Shannon killed a porcupine. \u00a0\u00a0 we could not git to the Boat for a willow Island which was between & Sand bars &.C. N. B. the Boat passd. Several Isds. & camped on N. S\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 we Camped [6] in a grove of cottonwood Timber. Eat one porcupine for Supper. \u00a0\u00a0 the Musquetoes Troubled us verry much.\u2014 \u00a0\u00a0 passd. a range of black Bluffs on S. S. &.c.\n\n

\n \n

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 13th. \u00a0\u00a0 Four beaver were taken last night. We set sail early; the morning was cloudy with some rain and wind ahead; passed a creek and a long range of bluffs on the south side. Some of our men [7] went out to hunt; but did not return this evening. We encamped on the north side.\n\n\n

\n \n
\n
\n \n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Thursday 13th Sept. 1804 \u00a0\u00a0 cloudy and hard rain. \u00a0\u00a0 G. Drewyer caught 4 beaver last night \u00a0\u00a0 high wind, passed a creek on S. S and range of black bluffs. \u00a0\u00a0 three of the party went out to hunt and has not returned yet. \u00a0\u00a0 Camped on the N. S.\u2014

\n

\nThursday Septemr 13th \u00a0\u00a0 We started early this morning it being Cloudy and some Rain, One of our Men (G drewyer the Hunter) caught four Beaver in the Traps overnight, The wind blew hard. \u00a0\u00a0 we passed \u2329the\u232a a Creek, lying on the South side of the River, and a range of black bluffs, Captain Clark & the two Men who went hunting Yesterday, [8] had not yet return'd \u00a0\u00a0 We proceeded on, and encamped on the North side of the River\n\n\n\n

1. The courses and distances for September 13, 1804, on the reverse of document 53 of the Field Notes are written over a sketch map of part of the Missouri River (fig. 2). Though there are no names, it appears to show the river between the camps of September 8 and 9. See Atlas maps 19, 20. This area in Charles Mix and Gregory counties, South Dakota, is now inundated by Fort Randall Reservoir. (back)
\n
2. Pierre Shale (see the geology note of September 12). (back)
\n
3. In Brule County, South Dakota. Atlas maps 20, 21, 22; MRC map 36. (back)
\n
4. This portion of the Pierre Shale has less gypsum and other minerals. (back)
\n
5. \n Lewis's natural history notes from Codex Q. The bird is the blue-winged teal, Anas discors [AOU, 140]. Holmgren, 33. The porcupine is mentioned on September 11. (back)
\n
6. \n Ordway and his group camped in Lyman County, South Dakota, while the main party stayed on the opposite shore in Brule County. (back)
\n
7. Sergeants Ordway and Pryor, and Shannon. (back)
\n
8. Apparently Whitehouse wrote this entry on the next day; Ordway, Pryor, and Shannon went out hunting this day, September 13, and did not return until the next day. See Ordway's entries for those dates. (back)
