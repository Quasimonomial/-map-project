\n May 22, 1804\n

\n
\n \n

[Clark]

\n \n
Tuesday May 22nd
\u00a0
\n \n

\ndelayed a Short time for the three french men who returned and we Set out at 6 oClock \u00a0\u00a0 a Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 rained Violently hard last night \u00a0\u00a0 Saw Several people on the bank to day & passed \u2329many\u232a Several Small farms. Capt. Lewis walk on Shore a little & passed a Camp of Kickapoo Indians, [1] & incamped in the mouth of a Small Creek in a large Bend on the Stbd Side. [2]\n

[Clark]

\n \n
\n May 22nd Tuesday 1804
\u00a0
\n \n

\na Cloudy morning \u00a0\u00a0 Delay one hour for 4 french men who got liberty to return to arrange Some business they had forgotten in Town, at 6 oClock we proceeded on, passed Several Small farms on the bank, and a large creek on the Lbd. Side Called \n Bonom\n [NB: bon homme] \u00a0\u00a0 a Camp of Kickapoos [NB: an Indian nation residing on the heads of Kaskaskia & Illinois river 90 miles N E of the mouth of the Missouri, & hunt occasionally on the Missouri\n ] on the St. Side \u00a0\u00a0 Those Indians told me Several days ago that they would Come on & hunt and by the time I got to their Camp they would have Some Provisions for us, we Camped in a Bend \u2329under\u232a at the Mo: of a Small creek, Soon after we came too the Indians arrived with 4 Deer as a Present, for which we gave them two qts. of whiskey\u2014

[Gass]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\n Tuesday 22nd. \u00a0\u00a0 We continued our voyage; passed Bonum creek on the \nsouth side, and having made fifteen miles, encamped at the Cliffs on the north side of the river. Here we were visited by some Indians.\n\n

\n \n
\n

[Whitehouse]

\n \n
\u00a0
\n \n

\nTuesday 22nd May 1804 [6] \u00a0\u00a0 a fair morning. \u00a0\u00a0 we Set out eairly proceeded on verry well \u00a0\u00a0 passed canom [7] Creek on the Stard \u00a0\u00a0 Several Indians came to us this evening. \u00a0\u00a0 Gave us some venison

\n

\nTuesday May 22nd \u00a0\u00a0 This morning being fair, we set out early and proceeded on very well. \u00a0\u00a0 We passed Bonhom Creek, laying on the South side of the River, in the Evening, several Indians came to where we encamped, & behaved very friendly, and gave us some Venison. \u00a0\u00a0 The course of the River \nstill being nearly West, and the current rapid.\u2014\n\n

\n \n \n
\n
1. Possibly the Kickapoo town referred to on May 18, 1804, but \"town\" usually refers to a more permanent Indian settlement than the hunting camp this seems to have been. (back)
\n
2. \n Clark indicates that this stream was \"Osage Woman's River,\" that is, Femme Osage River, or Creek. On May 23 he states that they had to go two miles to reach Femme Osage River. Perhaps the stream was one of several small watercourses downstream from Femme Osage River that are nameless on later maps. MRC map 3; MRM map 7. (back)
\n
3. \n Bonhomme Creek, in St. Louis County, Missouri. See fig. 9. MRC map 2. (back)
\n
4. \n Tavern Island seems to have disappeared, unless it was the later Howell Island. The name must have come from the cave nearby (see below, May 23, 1804, n. 2). See fig. 9. MRC map 3. (back)
\n
5. As indicated in n. 2, there is confusion about the identity of this stream. (back)
\n
6. \n Whitehouse may have written \"4\" over \"6\" for the year. See his entry of May 27. (back)
\n
7. \n Whitehouse means Bonhomme Creek, St. Louis County, Missouri, but he has it on the wrong side. It is correct in the fair copy. (back)
